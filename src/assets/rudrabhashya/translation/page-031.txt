                        Upoddhāta (Introduction)                             29

...the position is adopted that the Śrī Rudra Upaniṣad is directed at Brahman, on the strength of hundreds of śruti passages containing the names Śiva, Śaṃkara, Bhava, Śarva, Paśupati, and Rudra.

And just as in the adhikaraṇas (interpretive sections) — "ākāśas talliṅgāt" (Brahman, because of the indicative mark 'space'), "antas tad-dharmopadēśāt" (the inner [being is Brahman] because its qualities are described), "antar-yoni-ādhidaivādiṣu tad-dharma-vyapadēśāt" (in the passages about 'inner womb' and the like, it is [Brahman] because its attributes are ascribed), "ahṛdayatvādigua-ko dharmebhiḥ" (that which is without heart [and other limitations] is Brahman, through [its] attributes), and so on — the śruti passages such as "What is the final destination of this world? He said, 'Space.' Indeed all these beings are born from space alone..." and "That golden person who is seen within the sun..." are established as referring to Brahman, on the strength of the indicators of being the cause of all beings (sarva-bhūta-kāraṇatva) and of being the self of all (sarvātmakatva) — so too here, on the strength of the indicative marks of lordship over all (sarveśvaratva), being the self of all (sarvātmakatva), being the inner controller of all (sarvāntaryāmitva), and the like, the reference to Brahman must be adopted.

Indeed, in the Śrī Rudra Upaniṣad one hears — beginning with "Salutation to the Lord of the directions (diśām ca pataye namaḥ)" and proceeding through individual declarations of lordship over each part of the cosmos, culminating in "Salutation to the Lord of the worlds (jagatāṃ pataye namaḥ)" — the lordship over all worlds (sarva-jagad-īśvaratva); further, beginning with "You who carry arrows and bows..." declarations of being the self of each particular entity are built up, yielding the quality of being the self of all (sarvātmakatva); and similarly "Salutation to you who dwell in all" and the like establish the quality of inner-controllership of all (sarvāntaryāmitva) — all of these being qualities unique to Brahman (brahmasādhāraṇa-dharma). Further, the blue-throated form, the matted-hair, the wielder of Pināka bow, and the like are unique marks of the Supreme Lord (pāramēśvara-sādhāraṇa).
