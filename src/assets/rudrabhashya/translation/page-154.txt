    asmin mahaty arṇave 'ntarikṣe bhuṣā adhi |

Now the second mantra — beginning "asmin mahati" (in this great). Those Rudras who dwell above (adhi, over) this great visible ocean (mahati arṇave) and the atmosphere (antarikṣe) — as lords of the ocean and the atmosphere — for them [the bows at a distance of a thousand yojanas are unstrung] — this is the connection. Here "asmin mahaty arṇave 'ntarikṣe" is a compound expression for ocean and atmosphere, using the device of conjoining similar things. As the saying goes: "Space is like space, and the ocean is like the ocean" — meaning, things of supreme magnitude are described by comparison with themselves. By this the immense greatness of those Rudras who preside over them is also established. From this mantra and through the ninth, the phrase "teṣāṃ sahasra-yojane" (at a thousand yojanas from them) is to be carried along as an understood connection; and to make this connection clear, the phrase "teṣām" (of them) and what follows is repeated again in the seventh mantra.

    nīlagrīvāḥ śitikaṇṭhāḥ śarvāḥ adhaḥ-
    kṣamā-carāḥ ||

    Blue-necked (nīlagrīvāḥ), white-throated (śitikaṇṭhāḥ),
    those Śarvas who dwell beneath the earth —

Now the third mantra — beginning "nīlagrīvā" (blue-necked). The blue-necked ones (nīlagrīvāḥ) — those who, after consuming the Kālakūṭa poison, became blue-throated and thus manifest as forms of the Supreme Lord; and those who, prior to that, have white throats — the white-throated ones (śitikaṇṭhāḥ) — both these states of having a blue or white throat have been explained earlier according to their respective phases. Such are those Śarvas (the Rudras called Śarva) who dwell in the worlds below the earth (adhaḥ-kṣamā-carāḥ), in the nether worlds (pātāla-lokeṣu), as their sovereign lords — for them [the same applies] as before.

    nīlagrīvāḥ śitikaṇṭhāḥ divaṃ rudrā
    upāśritāḥ ||

    Blue-necked, white-throated Rudras
    who have resorted to (upāśritāḥ) the heavens —

Now the fourth mantra — beginning "nīlagrīvā" (blue-necked). Those who have ascended to (upasaṃprāptāḥ) the heavens (divaṃ, the sky) as the controllers of the senses (indriya-niyantṛtayā) — the rest as before. The four verses from "sahasrāṇi sahasraśaḥ" up to this constitute a single mantra. The seer is Turvāśa. The meter is Anuṣṭubh. The deity is Rudra. Meditation verse: "One should meditate upon him with a fearful and gaping face, terrible, with hair like blood, three-eyed (tri-netra), blazing with light (nīśikha-dīpta), adorned with serpents." This mantra is primarily for destruction of enemies. Mantra-siddhi is attained by reciting five thousand times, preceded by the Cāndrāyaṇa (lunar fast observance).
