In the world, the deity manifests to devotees only after they have worshipped for a long time through sustained practice; but the blessed Aditya (the Sun-god) manifests by himself every single day before all creatures, appearing in the form of dawn and the like. This very fact has been declared: "The cowherd beheld Him, and likewise those who were unseen beheld Him" — so the Shruti (Vedic scripture) states. Thus, even for the sake of obtaining the vision (darshana) of one who manifests in this way, no sacrifice is required; for the blessed Lord rises facing each and every person individually. It is heard: "Therefore all people think He has risen facing me alone." In this manner, by manifesting of His own accord and standing facing those who seek refuge, the Supreme Lord inspires in them the mere cognition — "This is the Supreme Lord" — and through that alone, one obtains all that is auspicious. So too the Shruti declares: "The learned Brahmin who meditates upon the Sun as He rises and as He sets attains all good; that Sun is Brahman (the Absolute)." Therefore the meaning is that the Supreme Lord, who effortlessly bestows all the goals of human life upon all persons, is alone worthy of worship. In the Shruti passage "upon the rising one" (udayantam), the meditation is enjoined specifically at the time of sunrise; but in the world, adherence to the time of sunrise is not possible for all people, due to negligence and the preponderance of sloth.
