Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                   63

---

Now through the sixth [mantra] onwards, six mantras are used to pray for the withdrawal of the weapons. Although [the prayer] "śivāṃ giritrar tāṃ kuru mā hiṃsīḥ" (make it auspicious, O dweller on the mountain; do not injure us) had already been offered earlier, yet out of extreme fear the prayer is made again; therefore there is no occasion for the objection of redundancy (punarukti). "Prabhuśca" — O Bhagavan (Blessed Lord), [i.e.,] one endowed with the six qualities of complete sovereignty, valour, glory, prosperity, knowledge, and dispassion, as stated in the teaching: "Sovereignty in full, valour, glory, prosperity — knowledge and dispassion too — these six are designated by the word 'bhaga.'" [The grammatical rule] "matvarthasya r — in the Vedic usage, in the vocative" [accounts for the form "Bhagavaḥ"]. O Lord, the two ends (ārvī) of your bow (dhanvan) — the string (jyā, the bowstring, the mauṟvī) — loosen that; for no one else is capable of loosening the bow of you, who are endowed with complete sovereignty and the rest, as the Vedic text [attests]: "Praise be to Rudra with his firm bow (iṃā rudrāya sthiradhanvane giraḥ)." Therefore, being yourself gracious, please unstring your bow — such is the meaning. Even when the bow has been unstrung, the arrows appear as if about to devour [one], therefore: O Bhagavan, the arrows that are in your hand — those, scatter far (parā vapa), make them go away. Thus [ends] the tenth [mantra].
