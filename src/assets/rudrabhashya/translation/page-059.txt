SRI RUDRA BHASHYAM.                       57

[Continuation of the commentary on the sixth mantra "adhyabocat..."]

— by removing the sins that are the cause of their future births and thus
purifying them (making them pure, anagha), He progressively leads them
to happiness — therefore His compassion is not diminished.

[The text shows that:] since He [acts] declaring His protection through
"adhy abocat" rather than praying "jambhayatu" (may He destroy) — the
Lord's blameless sovereignty (nirdosham ishvaratvam) is demonstrated.
It is not that, being incapable Himself of subduing the ahis and yatudhanyahs
and the deity of death (mrityu-devata) who impels them, He propitiates them
with the request "this person who is mine should not be harmed" — thus
supplicating the ahis and yatudhanyahs and freeing the prostrated devotee
by such intercession — [the Lord does not act in that manner]. Rather,
being perfectly capable Himself, He first protects the devotee by destroying
the ahis and yatudhanyahs; and then, when the gods come asking "how is
this sinner to be protected?", He makes the partisan declaration: "This one
is mine — what sin does he have?"

Hence [this applies] even to great sinners: hunters and others, whose Yama
messengers were destroyed by the Lord's own divine emissaries (shiva-duta)
as they were being led away; and when they were brought by the Supreme
Lord to Kailasa, and Yama, Chitragupta, and others reported the incident
to the Lord — He said to them: "Even if inadvertently (yad ricchaya),
they performed acts such as throwing bilva leaves [at My image] and
prostrations — they are mine; therefore you have no authority over them."
Such stories are repeatedly proclaimed in the Puranas.

Understanding this intent, in this [mantra] — "raksha ca no adhi ca deva
bruhi" (protect us, O God, and then champion our cause) — protection
(rakshana) comes first, and the declaration of partisanship (adhivacana)
comes afterward. Thus this mantra is the one that removes all afflictions
from serpents, rakshasas (demons), pishachas (spirits), and all [such
beings]. This is precisely why its designation is kavacha (armor, protective
formula) — as stated by the revered Bodhayana: "adhyabocat iti kavacham"
(the verse 'adhy abocat' is the armor).

[End of the sixth mantra of the first anuvaka of Shri Rudra Bhashyam.]
