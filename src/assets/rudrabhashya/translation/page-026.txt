24                        Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

The Vamana Smṛti declares: "By studying the Veda, one is purified a hundredfold; by studying the Rudra Ekādaśinī (the eleven anuvākas of Rudra), one is purified right there [i.e., by that alone]." Likewise, the statement in Chandas-śāstras and related texts — "One who recites Rudra while standing in water..." and so forth — where the recitation of the Puruṣasūkta and similar texts is mentioned alongside Śrī Rudra, is intended from the standpoint of the element of removing sin (pāpa-haratva), not as indicating that those texts are of equal primacy. The distinction of degrees (tāratamya) is indeed recognized by those who seek to compare them; such is the intent of the smṛti.

Similarly, the Itihāsas (epics) also proclaim the supreme status of Śrī Rudra among all Vedic texts. In the Anuśāsana Parvan of the Mahābhārata, in response to Yudhiṣṭhira's question — "By the grace of Durvāsas and the great-souled Śaṃkara, a certain knowledge arose in me here. Please explain it to me. I wish to hear everything about the great glory of that great-souled one, and the names of Śarva, O best among speakers" — Śrī Kṛṣṇa's reply begins with "Let me tell you, after paying homage to Kapardin (Śiva)," and the following is heard: "His names, many and meaningful, are explained in various ways in the worlds; they are manifold because of his all-pervasiveness and his manifold functions. And among these, the wise know the Śatarudrīya śruti as the most excellent in the Veda." In the Droṇa Parvan, the same statement of Vyāsa is heard addressed to Arjuna.
