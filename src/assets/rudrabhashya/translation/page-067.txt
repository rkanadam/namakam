Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                   65

---

    "Let the bow of the matted-haired one (Kapardin) be unstrung, and the quiver too be emptied of arrows. Let the arrows of his become incapable of wounding, and let his quiver be swift [but empty]."

Now, even for the arrows that have been blunted, the prayer is offered for their incapacitation: "Vijyam iti" — may the bow of Kapardin (the Lord with matted hair, who is the Supreme Lord), be vijya — without its bowstring; and may the arrow-holder (bāṇavān — one who bears arrows) also be viśalya — devoid of arrows. Even if the quiver holds shafts, there is the concern about arrows elsewhere, hence the [mantra] says: "Aneśan iti" — may the arrows of this Kapardin be incapable of wounding, having attained destruction in the form of inability to pierce (aneśan — may they fail to reach; nāśa in the optative-like usage of the Vedic [aorist]). Furthermore, may his niṣaṅghiḥ (the quiver) be āśu (swift/ready) — meaning capable only of holding the quiver but not qualified to perform the task of pulling out and discharging the arrows. Here, [the text] "Prabhuśca dhanvana" and so forth constitutes a single mantra for the teacher. The seer (ṛṣi) of this pair of [mantras] is Nārada, the metre is Anuṣṭup, and the deity is Śrī Rudra. [The dhyāna-śloka is:] "One should meditate upon Rudra, blazing like ten million rising suns, his form like a flaming fire, his limbs adorned with fearsome serpents, bearing various weapons." Through this [mantra] enemies are destroyed. Thus [ends] the twelfth [mantra].
