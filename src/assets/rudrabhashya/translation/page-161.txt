---
Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    159
---

    ṣuḥ sughanvā yo viśvasya kṣayati bheṣajasya |
    yakṣvāmahe saumanasāya rudraṃ namobhir devam-
    agharaṃ duvasya | ayam me astu bhagavān ayam me
    bhagavatīdaḥ | ayam me viśvam eṣajo 'yaṃ
    śivābhimarśanaḥ | ye te sahasramanta pā-
    śā mṛtyo marttyāya hantave | tāny añjhya
    māyayā sarvān ava yajāmahe | mṛtyave svā-
    hā mṛtyave svāhā | oṃ namo bhagavate
    rudrāya viṣṇave mṛtyumme pāhi ||

    We worship the three-eyed one (tryambakam), the supremely fragrant one,
    the nourisher of all beings (puṣṭi-vardhenam).
    Like a cucumber freed from its vine — may I be freed from death,
    not from immortality.
    He who is Rudra in fire, who is in the waters,
    who is in the herbs and plants — Rudra who has entered all the worlds —
    to that Rudra, salutation.
    Praise him who is swift with arrows and of beautiful bow (su-ghanvā),
    who is the lord and sustainer of all medicine and healing.
    We worship with salutations Rudra the benevolent god,
    the sinless one, that we may attain his gracious favor (saumanasā).
    May this blessed Lord be mine; may this one be my source of bounty.
    May this one be my universal healer (viśva-eṣaja),
    may this one touch me with his auspicious hand (śivābhimarśana).
    O Death, those thousands of nooses (pāśāḥ) of yours
    laid out to strike down the mortal —
    all of those we worship away through sacred rites and divine power (māyā).
    Svāhā to Death! Svāhā to Death!
    Oṃ. Salutation to the Blessed Lord Rudra-Viṣṇu — protect me from death.

    Thus is the Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam (Commentary on the Śrī Rudra Hymn) complete,
    composed by the most venerable Śrīmat Paramahaṃsa Parivrājaka Sārvabhauma
    (the supreme wandering renunciant of the highest order),
    the establisher of the Advaita (non-dual) Vidyā (sacred wisdom),
    the revered Abhinava Śaṃkara Bhagavatā.
