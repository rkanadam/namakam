Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                   73

---

— referring to the triple-quality (triguṇa) karma-kāṇḍa (the ritual section of the Vedas) characterised by leaves in the form of Vedic metres (chandas), as [indicated by the Vedic text]: "The Vedas deal with the subjects of the three guṇas," and "Leaves are its hymns (chandāṃsi yasya parṇāni)." Just as in the case of a tree, leaves serve to protect it, so the Vedic metres (chandas) that are of the nature of karma-kāṇḍa serve to protect the tree of saṃsāra, since they perpetuate worldly existence by inculcating action. And these, on account of their infinite abundance surpassing the understanding of the head (śiras), are described as hair (keśa). Thus the portion of the mantra "namo vṛkṣebhyo hirikeśebhyaḥ" is concluded in meaning as: salutation to the Supreme Lord in his aspect as the tree of saṃsāra, intertwined with the threefold web of karmic activity. Through this, the two questions — how [creatures] have pāśutva (the condition of being paśu, bound), and how [the Lord] has patitva (the condition of being pati, lord) — are both answered: creatures are paśu by virtue of being bound by the ropes of saṃsāra (saṃsārapāśa), and the Supreme Lord is pati of those [creatures] by virtue of causing them to perform their respective functions. This has been clearly amplified by some: "From Brahmā to the stationary beings, all are the paśus (bound livestock) of the trident-bearing Lord; they are called paśus because they are under the sway of saṃsāra. Because of his lordship over them, the Lord — indeed Śiva — is known as Paśupati (lord of the bound). Mahāśvara has bound the paśus from Brahmā to the pillar. By these bonds (pāśa), the Lord God himself causes them to perform their work. He alone is their liberator, when properly worshipped with devotion." Thus have similar statements from the Skānda, Śaiva and Kūrma Purāṇas been cited. Here, by the statement "from Brahmā to the stationary" it is explained that in the mantra "Eṣāmīśe" (the lord of these), the two-footed and four-footed [beings] serve as indicative of the entire manifest world, composed of conscious and inert beings.
