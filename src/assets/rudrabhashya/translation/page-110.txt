108                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

is anticipated here. For it is heard that: one of the Supreme Lord's varied powers (vicitraśakti) upholds the glory of waters; as stated: "One power uplifts the glory of waters; another [power] belongs to the sun (sūrya)."

      The word dvīpa (island) too is used here with the sense of the Lord's inner indwelling in water-surrounded places — the meaning being "a place where waters have divided into two" (dvidhābhūtā āpo yasmin deśa iti). The word is grammatically derived as follows: by the rule "dv-anty-asargebhyo'ḍap ī" the augment ī replaces the final vowel; and in the compound form "ṛkapūrchabha-bhūḥ-pathām-ānache" the final a is also transformed. [Grammatical analysis of the compound derivation of dvīpa.]

      Alternatively, by the word dvīpa — referring to all the seven continents (saptadvīpa) beginning with Jambūdvīpa — the entire world may be indicated by synecdoche. Thus, the implication is that all glory found across all seven continents and throughout every realm of the world belongs solely to the Supreme Lord who is the inner dweller of those places — this is the intended understanding.

                    Thus ends the Fifth Anuvāka.
