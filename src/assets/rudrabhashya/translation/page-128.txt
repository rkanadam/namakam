This great mantra is the crown jewel of the Rudropanishad (the Upanishad of Rudra), which all the Shrutis (Vedic scriptures) cherish as their innermost essence. As it has been said: "Among all learning, the one hundred branches of the Shruti are like the axe (in cutting through ignorance); among those, the eleven-syllabled Rudra hymn; within that, the Panchakshara (the five-syllabled mantra, Namah Shivaya); and within that, the two syllables 'Shi-va'." In the Agamas (Shaiva scriptural tradition), the word shiva is also used to refer to the individual soul (jiva) and to Brahman (the Absolute). All the passages of the Shivapurana are to be cited as authoritative testimony to this supreme greatness (of Shiva). Such is the summary.

           Namastirthya ya ca kulya ya ca namah pargha ya
           cavargha ya ca namah pratarana ya cottarna ya ca
           nama atargha ya caladhya ya ca namah shashpya ya
           ca phenya ya ca namah sikatya ya ca pravahya ya ca

           (Salutation to Him who dwells in holy fords and on riverbanks;
           salutation to Him on the far bank and on the near bank;
           salutation to Him who carries across and who lifts up;
           salutation to Him at the crossing point and on the shallows;
           salutation to Him in the tender grass and in the foam;
           salutation to Him in the sand and in the current)

Thus, through the mantras beginning with "Namah somaya" and ending with the foregoing, salutation has been offered to the Lord, distinguished by the various reasons why He is worthy of worship by all. Now, through the combined salutation with "Namastirthya ya" and the rest, the commentator salutes together both the foregoing and the following mantras. Tirthya means He who abides in holy fords (tirtha) such as the Ganga and others; Kulya means He who abides on riverbanks (kula); Para means the far bank of a river or the like, and Parya means He who abides on the far bank; Avara means the near bank, and Avarya means He who abides on the near bank; Pratarana means He who causes crossing — the means of crossing the ocean of sins through the excellent path; Uttarana means He who causes the crossing upward — the means of crossing over ignorance through the excellent path of knowledge.
