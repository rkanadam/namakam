Ninth Anuvaka (Chapter Nine)

---*---

   Nama irinya ya ca prapathya ya ca namah
   kimshilaya ca kshayanaya ca namah kapa-
   rdine ca pulastaye ca namo goshtya ca
   grhya ya ca namastalpya ya ca gehya ya ca
   namah kathya ya ca gahresthaya ca namo
   hridayya ya ca niveshpya ya ca namah
   pamsavya ya ca rajasya ya ca namah
   shushkya ya ca haritya ya ca namo
   lopya ya colpya ya nama u-
   rvya ya ca surmya ya ca namah
   pannya ya ca parneshadhya ya ca

   In the eighth chapter, the Lord was saluted through the quality of His being the inner controller (antaryamin) — by citing reasons why He is worthy of universal worship. In the ninth chapter too, the Lord is saluted in His role as inner controller — by the mantra beginning "Nama irinya ya." Irina means a salt desert or barren wasteland (ushara); He who abides there is Irinya. Prapatha means the well-worn path (ksunna-marga); He who abides there is Prapathya. Kimshila is the place where there are defective or jagged stones (kutsitah shilah); Kshayana is a region fit for habitation —
