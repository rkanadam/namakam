---
158                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

— the "namaḥ" (salutation) at the beginning of the mantra is the verbal salutation (vācika, expressed through speech). Thus we who bow with all three faculties (body, mind, and speech) — that which we hate (yaṃ dviṣmaḥ) and that which hates us (yaś ca no dveṣṭi) — that enemy, who embodies our sinful karmas, their fruits, and the instruments thereof — O Rudras, into your open jaws (jaghme, the gaping mouth) we place him (dadhāmi) — meaning: destroy him. The same connection (yojanā) applies to the second and third mantras. The Rudras who dwell in the atmosphere (antarikṣe) — whose arrows are wind (vāta): they afflict sinners by producing various diseases through disorders of wind (vāyu-vikāra), and through generating extremely violent storms and the like — this is the meaning. The Rudras who dwell in the heavens (divi) — whose arrows are rain (varṣima-iṣavaḥ): by excessive rainfall, drought, and the like they afflict sinners — the rest is to be understood in a pleasing manner throughout.

The entire collection of yajus-verses (Yajurveda mantras) beginning with "namo rudrabhyaḥ" through to the end forms a single mantra. Here the deity and seer are both Rudra. As for the meditation verse — the one stated under "sahasrāṇi sahasraśaḥ" is to be used here as well. This mantra is primarily for the granting of good fortune and for warding off evil influences. The method of puraścaraṇa (the ritual procedure for achieving mantra-siddhi, perfection in the mantra) — including the various fruits such as neutralizing hostile acts (kṛtya-utpādana), pacification (śānti), subjugation (stambhana), and so forth — these are elaborated in the Rudra-Kalpa (Rudra ritual manual) and other texts, and are to be consulted there.

    tryambakaṃ yajāmahe sugandhinṃ puṣṭi-vardhe-
    nam | urvārukam iva bandhanān mṛtyor mukṣīya
    mā 'mṛtāt | yo rudro agnau yo apsu ya
    oṣadhīṣu yo rudro viśvā bhuvanā viveśa
    tasmai rudrāya namo astu | tasmai stuhi yaḥ khi-
