                        Upoddhāta (Introduction)                             23

[Continuation of the refutation of the objection that Atharvaśiras is superior to Śrī Rudra]

...and so it is self-evident that within the context of the Atharvaśiras, that statement is a eulogy (prasaṃsā) directed at the Atharvaśiras itself, and is thus established as having an alternative explanation (anyathāsiddhi — i.e., it serves another purpose within its own context). The passage praising Śrī Rudra, however, is not found within the Śrī Rudra Upaniṣad itself — as would be required for a similar alternative explanation to apply. Rather, it occurs in the Jābāla and Kaivalya Upaniṣads and other texts of the same scriptural lineage as the Śrī Rudra Upaniṣad. If the passage in those texts were understood as merely a eulogy of Śrī Rudra and not as a literal statement of its own import, then that praise of Rudra — being unconnected to the Kaivalya and Jābāla texts as their own subject — would be without independent validity, lacking the authority to establish its own meaning. And by the principle of the section on Arthavāda (eulogistic statement in the Mīmāṃsā school), it would become entirely meaningless. Therefore, it must be accepted as a literal statement of its own import (svārthaparatva). Further, by the principle of "the praise of one sacred site [vouched for] by another sacred site" (tīrthe tīrthāntarastutiṃ nyāya) and by the principle of "the victory document signed by the opponent himself" (pratipādikara-pita-jayapatrikā-nyāya), the statement in the Jābāla and Kaivalya śrutis praising Śrī Rudra establishes its supreme status beyond all question, and no one can refute it. Enough of elaboration. ॥

This supremacy of Śrī Rudra, established by śruti (revealed scripture), is also confirmed by smṛti (secondary scripture). As Bodhāyana the revered one has shown in his nyāsa (ritual placement of mantras) for the Śrī Rudra mahānyāsa: "The Śivāsaṃkalpa is the heart [mantra], the Puruṣasūkta is the head [mantra], the Uttara-nārāyaṇa is the topknot [mantra], the Apratiratha is the protective mantra" — and so forth — the Śivāsaṃkalpa and other texts, together with the Tryambaka, Pañcabrahma mantras and others, are shown to surpass all sin and yield supreme fruits in accordance with the prescriptions of Manu and others; they are presented as manifestations of Śrī Rudra, thereby demonstrating that Śrī Rudra is supreme among all Vedic texts. Similar examples may be multiplied. Thus, upon careful examination of both śruti and smṛti, the supremacy of Śrī Rudra among all Vedic texts is firmly established.
