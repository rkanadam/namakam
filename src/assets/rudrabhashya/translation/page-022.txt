20                        Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

After the teaching that Śrī Rudra is a means to liberation, in response to the question posed by Janaka to Yājñavalkya — "O Venerable One, please instruct me in the nature of sannyāsa (renunciation)" — the complete description of sannyāsa along with its accessories is taught, and then its efficacy as a means to liberation is declared: "The wandering monk, wearing ochre robes, with shaved head, possessing nothing, pure, free from malice, subsisting on alms — he becomes fit for Brahman."

Now, since both sannyāsa and Śrī Rudra equally serve as means to liberating knowledge (jñāna-sādhanatva), the word 'atyāśramī' (supreme renunciant) is introduced to accommodate both of them. By this word, the primary prescription of Rudra-japa (Rudra recitation) for the attainment of liberating knowledge is shown to be specifically for sannyāsins as well, and its superiority over sannyāsa alone is demonstrated. This is because, for the arising of liberating knowledge, sannyāsa depends upon Śrī Rudra, whereas Śrī Rudra — being prescribable as a recitation for liberation for brahmacarins (celibate students) and others as well — does not depend on sannyāsa. Such is the purpose of using the word 'atyāśramī' as a synecdoche for all āśramas.

Nor should it be objected that this interpretation conflicts with the prohibition against sannyāsins reciting mantras other than Praṇava (Oṃ): For the Āraṇyaka Upaniṣad text — "Let him recite the Upaniṣad" — prescribes Śrī Rudra for sannyāsins in general by virtue of its status as an Upaniṣad; further, texts of Viśveśvara-smṛti and others specifically prescribe Rudra recitation for sannyāsins: "Let the ascetic always recite Śrī Rudra and the Praṇava"; and in the Kaivalya Upaniṣad, "Let the supreme renunciant always or even once recite it" prescribes Śrī Rudra primarily as the means of direct liberating knowledge in relation to the entire section on knowledge. By these three means of authority, the mandatory nature of Śrī Rudra recitation for sannyāsins aspiring to liberation is firmly established.
