                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                    113

      Just as in the sixth, so too in the seventh anuvāka, the Lord is saluted by virtue of His inner presence in all (sarvāntaryāmitva) and His being the Soul of all (sarvātmatva). "Namo dundubhyāya" — He who dwells in the kettledrum (dundubhi, the large battle-drum) is Dundubhya. That which is struck (āhanyante tādyante) by the drum-stick is Āhana — the drumstick used for beating the drum — He who dwells in that is Āhananya.

      "Namo ghṛṣṇave ca" and the rest are salutations to the Lord as the Soul of all. Whoever in the world is a Ghṛṣṇu — one who does not retreat in battle — and a messenger (dūta) and so forth — to Him in the form of all those is the meaning. Ghṛṣṇu is one who does not flee the battlefield; Pramṛśa is one expert in examining and thinking through matters at hand; Dūta is one who is skilled in knowing the news of that [enemy]; Prahita is a person dispatched by his master; Niṣaṅgin and Iṣudhimat have been explained [earlier]. He whose arrows are sharp (tīkṣṇā iṣavaḥ) is Tīkṣṇeṣu; he who possesses many weapons is Āyudhī; he who possesses an excellent weapon is Svāyudha; he who possesses an excellent bow is Sudhanvā.

      The salutations beginning with "namaḥ srutyā" are concerned with the Lord's inner presence (antaryāmitva). A path suitable only for foot-traffic (śrutir, lit. what is traversed) is a narrow path (rudra-mārgaḥ) — He who dwells in that is Śrutya. A great road (prauḍha-bhogas, a well-used thoroughfare) suitable for cart-traffic and other travel is a Panthas — He who dwells there is Pathya. A Kāṭa is a region of little water where impure [or minimal] water collects (kutsita... alpa-jala-pradeśa) — He who dwells there is Kāśya. The place where water falls downward in a vertical flow is Nīpa — He who dwells there is Nīpya. Sūda is muddy ground (kardama) — He who dwells there is Sūdya. Sara (a lake/pond) is well-known — He who dwells there is Sārasya. A small lake is Vaiśanta — He who dwells there is Vaiśantya. He who dwells in a well (kūpe) is Kūpya. He who dwells in a pit (avaṭe garte) is Avaṭya. He who dwells in rain (varṣe) is Varṣya. The absence of rain is Avarṣa — He who dwells in that is Avarṣya. [He is] the impeller of both the states of rain and its absence.
