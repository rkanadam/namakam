28                        Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

[The author begins his refutation of the claim that Śrī Rudra is directed at Agni or is merely eulogistic]

They argue: From the salutations, from the indicators of prayers for the attainment of desired things and the avoidance of undesired things, it follows that Śrī Rudra is eulogistic in character, and thus its true meaning (tāttvika) — lordship over all, inner-controller of all (sarvāntaryāmitva), and the like — cannot be established. To this we reply:

Just as there is a śruti passage explaining [Śrī Rudra] as being directed at Agni — "This [fire] is indeed Rudra, that which is Agni" — so too there is a śruti passage explaining it as being directed at the Supreme Lord: "These are the names of the immortal one." The latter passage is corroborated by the Itihāsa and Purāṇa texts cited earlier — "In the Veda the Śatarudrīya is declared to be the most excellent" and "These are my secrets" — and is thus beyond doubt as to its intended meaning; it cannot be an occasion for suspecting that it refers to something other than the Supreme Lord.

The maxim "Strengthen the Veda through the Itihāsa and Purāṇa" establishes that śruti, smṛti, Itihāsa, and Purāṇa, in the manner of the "pride of lions" (vana-siṃha-nyāya), mutually imply and confirm one another, and thus when they converge on a meaning, that meaning becomes unshakeable. Now, since even when [the text is interpreted as] pointing to Agni, and also [when interpreted as] pointing to Brahman, the explanatory commentary produced by the Lord himself is equally applicable — how is one to decide between them in a case of doubt? The resolution must be made through the method of reasoned interpretation. As stated in the Bhaṭṭa-vārtika: "Where Dharma is being established through the Veda as an instrumental authority, the Mīmāṃsā [school of exegesis] will supply the missing portion of what is to be done." Accordingly, by the methods of śruti (direct statement), liṅga (indicative mark), and other principles of interpretation, the meaning of Śrī Rudra is established as being directed at Brahman (brahmaparatva).
