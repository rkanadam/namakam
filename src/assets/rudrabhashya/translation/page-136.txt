134                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam

---

supremely auspicious (shiva, i.e., most peaceful) — with that form of Yours, make us glad (mridaya) so that we may live (jivase) in good health. The suffix "se" is here a Vedic usage in place of the infinitive. The meaning is: by granting us happiness, cause us to live in wholeness and wellbeing. What is the auspiciousness of that form? The text answers: "vishvahabheshaji" — on every day (vishveshu ahahsu), it is the medicine (bheshaji), the means of destroying disease, poverty and the like. Since this is so, it is auspicious. And its auspiciousness is not merely that of providing worldly happiness — it is also the provider of the happiness of liberation (moksha). This is stated by: "shiva rudrasya." It is Your own innate medicine (bheshaji), O Rudra — the remedy for the disease of samsara (the cycle of birth and death). Since this is so, it is auspicious (shiva). With that, O Lord, make us glad (mridaya). The Rishi and deity of this mantra is Rudra in the form of Skanda (the war god). The meditation verse is: "Contemplate Shiva in the form of Skanda, visible from a distance, with a spear in his hand, six-faced, mounted upon a peacock." Mastery of this mantra is achieved through repetitions of five or six hundred thousand (japa), preceded by one hundred and eight pranayamas (breath-controls). This mantra yields as its fruit — the pacification of magical attacks, bewilderment, bad dreams, good sons, nourishment, the granting of health, and the attainment of prosperity.

           Imam rudra ya tapase kapar dine kshadavira ya
           prabhara mahi mitam yatha nah sha masad
           vipade catushpade vishvam pushtam grame a-
           bhishannaturam

           (This dedication we offer to Rudra who is empowered by
           austerity, who has matted locks, whose enemies are overcome;
           may all bipeds and quadrupeds in our village be prosperous
           and free from disease)
