Introduction (Upoddhatah).                                     17

[Continuing on the jnana-kanda supremacy:]

The text demonstrates jnana-kanda supremacy through the statement: "One of whatever ashram should always recite the Rudra, or at least once. By this one attains knowledge — the destroyer of the ocean of samsara."

Here, through the phrase "always, or at least once" (sarvada sakrid va), offering the option that even a single recitation of the Shri Rudra produces the attainment of knowledge, the argument for its jnana-kanda supremacy is established. For it is settled in the "Avritti-adhikarana" (the section on repetition in the Brahmasutras) that the jnana-kanda consists of the Upanishads, and that they are to be studied repeatedly in order to attain knowledge. Thus, [Upanishads] that require repeated recitation are superseded by the Shri Rudra, which generates knowledge through even a single recitation — hence its supremacy over all the jnana-kanda is proven.

Now, although it is also the case that for the Shri Rudra Upanishad — as for other Upanishads — repeated hearing, reflection, and deep meditation (shravana, manana, nididhyasana) until the direct realization of the Brahman-Atman identity may be required, nonetheless two distinct modes by which knowledge arises [from the Shri Rudra] are possible: one follows the method of hearing, reflection, and so on; the other — as described in the Jabala Shruti, "By these verily one becomes immortal" — arises through mere recitation alone, without the processes of hearing and so on. In this second mode, the sacred divine names of the Supreme Lord, which are themselves eager to bestow liberation, bring about the arising of knowledge through the grace of the Lord, who approaches [the devotee] spontaneously.

For the first mode, the repeated recitation of the Shri Rudra is operative, impelled by the practice of hearing and so on. For the second mode — through the prior conditions laid down in the Kurma Purana, namely devotion to sacred ash (bhasmanishtha) and complete absorption in the Supreme Lord — knowledge arises through the grace of Shiva from the daily single recitation of the Rudra. Taking this second mode as the basis, the intent of the Shruti is: the complete supremacy of the Shri Rudra [over all sections of the Veda] is established.

The statement "One of whatever ashram" (ayashrami japeth) does not make a restrictive enumeration (parisankhya) meaning "only those not of the householder stage should recite" — for in the Jabala Upanishad, the questioners are brahmacharins [who ask about recitation] —
