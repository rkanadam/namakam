Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                   59

---

The Blessed Lord, who is of the nature of Āditya (the Sun), and the Moon — and the other Rudras who have become unified with the Sun — these, stationed in all the directions around this earth in accordance with the Lord's command and in consonance with the accumulated merit and demerit of all beings, in order to grant favour and to restrain — these, positioned in the eastern and other directions, numbered in many thousands (as will be stated in the [later mantra] "sahasrāṇi sahasraśaḥ") — we appease the heḍa (fury arising from transgression of what is prohibited and from improper conduct) of these Rudras, who are of the form of Āditya, through praise, salutation and other devotional acts. May they be pleased with us — such is the meaning. Thus [ends] the seventh [mantra].

    "That one who moves downward, blue-necked and deep red — the cowherds saw him, and the women who carry water saw him; and all living beings saw him; he, being seen, grants us happiness."

Thus having propitiated [the Lord] through the seventh [mantra], now through the eighth, the fulfilment of one's desired object is prayed for: "Asau yo'vasarpati" — He who is known in the Vedic texts such as "trilochanaṃ nīlakaṇṭham" (the three-eyed, the blue-throated one) — that very one is Asau (this one), who, being "Vilohita" (intensely red), moves downward (avasarpati, i.e., proceeds in motion).
