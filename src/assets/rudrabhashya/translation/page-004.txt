[Continuing the Introduction]

When the brahmacharins (students of sacred knowledge) asked [Yajnavalkya]: "By the recitation of what [mantra] does one attain immortality? Please tell us" — this being a question about the means of liberation through recitation (japa) — "Yajnavalkya replied: 'By the Shataraudriya.' These indeed are the names of the Immortal (the Eternal Lord). By these one verily becomes immortal." By this, the primacy of the Sri Rudra Upanishad — denoted by the word Shataraudriya — as a means of liberation through japa (recitation) is established.

[On the etymology of the word "Shataraudriya":]

The word "Shataraudriya" means: "that in which one hundred Rudras are the presiding deity" — thus, from the root Shataraudra, by the grammatical rule "sasyaita devata" (in the chapter on presiding deities), the sutra "Shataraudraddasha" enables the formation of the two grammatical forms "Shataraudriya" and "Shataraudriya" through the suffixes ca and cha respectively, as established by grammarians.

Furthermore, the word "shata" (hundred) signifies infinity, as the Shruti declares: "A hundred means infinite" (shatam anantam bhavati); and as the Mahabharata states: "Hundred, thousand, and all such words denote the inexhaustible." In this text, Rudras of infinite number are expounded, as the Veda itself declares: "Thousands upon thousands of Rudras dwell upon the earth" and "The Rudras who are spread in thousands across the directions around this [world]." Their status as deities is due to their being proclaimed by the mantras, in accordance with the Nirukta (etymology) author's statement: "The deity is the intended meaning of the mantra."

Alternatively, "Rudriya" means "that which belongs to Rudra" — by the suffix cha in the rule "shridhadhaccha," and also by the suffix ca, since the suffix ca is seen applied to the word Rudra alone in the sense of relationship, as in "ya te agne rudriya tanur yedha tokaya" and similar passages. Thus the word "Rudriya" refers to the Sri Rudra Upanishad, which is the property of (or connected with) Rudra.
