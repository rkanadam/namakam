                        Upoddhāta (Introduction)                             21

[Continuing the discussion on the prohibition of mantras other than Praṇava for sannyāsins]

...and therefore the scriptural injunction against reciting mantras other than [Praṇava] must necessarily be understood as applying to mantras other than Śrī Rudra. As for the accomplished sannyāsin (vidvān sannyāsin) who has fulfilled all duties — since the Gītā declares "He has nothing further to do" — both the injunction of Praṇava and the prohibition of other mantras are inapplicable to him; all injunctive and prohibitive scriptures apply only to those who are ignorant (avidvat) and the like. Therefore, it is firmly established that the statement "Let the supreme renunciant always, or even once, recite it" declares Śrī Rudra to be the supreme teaching in the section on liberating knowledge (jñāna-kāṇḍa).

Nor can it be argued that this passage does not concern itself with Śrī Rudra's capacity to generate liberating knowledge through a single recitation, but rather is itself making that claim about the Kaivalya Upaniṣad — and that the passage concerning Śrī Rudra only extends from "Whoever recites the Śatarudrīya" up to "he becomes free from that [bondage]." For if that were so, the mention of 'Śatarudrīya' in the Kaivalya Upaniṣad would have no connection [to the claim of liberating knowledge], and the sentence would be rendered meaningless. It cannot be claimed that the Śatarudrīya is not mentioned here as a means to liberating knowledge — because at the very beginning of the Kaivalya Upaniṣad, the sentence "Ascetics of purified being who are firmly established in the knowledge of Vedānta, through the yoga of renunciation" already addresses the Vedānta texts as means of liberating knowledge, and since that need is already satisfied there, a mention of Śrī Rudra as that same means would be redundant. Nor can it be argued that Śrī Rudra is mentioned merely as a means of mental purification (citta-śuddhi) required prior to liberating knowledge — for the means of mental purification such as Vedānta and the yoga of renunciation are themselves already mentioned in that opening passage, making a separate mention of Śrī Rudra for that purpose equally redundant.
