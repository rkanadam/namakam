38                    SRI RUDRA BHASHYAM

"yami," "ano niyuji," "vayam soma," "tam ishanam," "ase rudra,"
"syona prthivi" — having placed these as a sandwiching enclosure (samputa)
in the directions beginning with Indra's direction, and having thus performed
the raudrikarana (the rite of identifying oneself with Rudra) within oneself,
one is freed from all sins residing in the skin and bones, and becomes
unvanquished among all beings. Thereby, sorcerers, pretas (spirits of the
departed), pishacas (flesh-eating spirits), brahma-rakshasas (brahmin demons),
bhakshaya (devouring spirits), evil messengers, shakinis, dakinis, serpents,
dogs, quadrupeds, thieves, sudden adversities, and sudden attacks — all of
these see him as one blazing in a great conflagration.

The meaning of this is as follows: One should touch the feet [of the image]
while reciting the sadyojata mantra as the opening formula (mukharya), with
"panca-anga (five-limbed) namah" as the concluding formula. Similarly, one
should perform nyasa (ritual placement of mantras on the body) from the feet
upward to the crown of the head, using the formulas beginning with Vamadeva.
Thereafter, having said "hamsa hamsa," one should touch the crown of the
head. "Hamsa" is the well-known name of Sadashiva himself; and one who does
this indeed becomes Sadashiva, who is known by the name Hamsa. The action
to be performed thereafter is stated: "Then one should begin the samputa
(enclosure gesture)." Having formed the anjali mudra (gesture of cupped
hands) indicated by the word samputa, one should first direct it eastward and
recite the opening portion of the mantra "trataram indram," saying "namah to
Indra." Then turning toward the southeast, one should recite the opening
portion of the mantra "tvam no agne," saying "namah to Agni." In this manner
one should continue through the directions from the south to the northeast.
Thereafter one should perform raudrikarana (identification with Rudra). That
is of two kinds: the first is performed by appending the formula "raudrena
anikena pahi ma agne pitrhi ma ma ma himshi" to all the mantras from
"vishurasi pravahana" through "adhirasi khudribhyah"; the second is the one
performed beginning with "prajananam brahma tishthat" —
