---
142                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

Like the lion, which is called "mṛga" (the beast), being seized by a stronger beast and overcome — the apprehension that the Lord too might be overcome by something stronger and thus might then deserve praise only in that case — this apprehension is dispelled by the word "ugram" (the Terrible, the Invincible). He is one whom no one can overcome. As the Śruti declares in hundreds of passages: "His supreme power is heard to be manifold and natural — consisting of knowledge, strength, and action" (Śvetāśvatara Upaniṣad 6.8); "There is none mightier than you, O Rudra" (Taittirīya Saṃhitā); "He is the supreme great Lord over all lords" — thus is he established as the Lord of all worlds, of incomparable, supreme, and infinite power. Where then is the apprehension of his being overpowered? Such is the sentiment.

Having thus stated the undesirable consequence of not praising, the author now indicates the desirable result of praising, and prays to the Lord — "mṛḍa" (bring joy). O Rudra, you who are being praised (stavānaḥ) — bring joy (mṛḍa), bring happiness, to us who are housed in this aging body (jaritre, in one who grows old). May I live a full human lifespan free from disease, with sound limbs; and thereafter, may I be liberated, free from aging and death — grant happiness in this twofold sense. The armies (senāḥ) that are hostile to this end — those described in "namo rudrabhyaḥ" (which will be explained later), those well-known in passages like "triśambhayaśca gaṇino rujantaḥ" and similar — may those armies fall (niṣapantu) upon another, upon some sin other than ourselves, and destroy it. The seer of this mantra is Vaiyāghra (Vyāghra). The meter is Triṣṭubh. The deity is Śrī Rudra.
