— "may [the deity] stand on all sides, surrounded by a garland of flames; may
they protect me" — this is the raudrikarana (identification with Rudra)
consisting in the ritual installation of deities in one's body, in the manner
to be described below. It is to be practiced according to the tradition of one's
disciplic lineage (sampradaya). The results of all this are stated by the
formula: "one is freed from all sins residing in the skin and bones," etc.
This is the third nyasa (ritual placement). Now the fourth nyasa is stated:
"mano jyotir abodhy agnim murdho murdhanam marmani te jataveda — with this,
one should consecrate the genitals, navel, heart, throat, face, and head, and
then perform self-protection."

The meaning of this is: Using the mantra beginning with "mano jyotih" as
the opening formula, having said "namah to the generative organ," one should
touch the genitals. Similarly, using the formulas beginning with "abodhy agnih,"
having said "namah to the navel," one should touch the navel, heart, throat,
face, and head in succession. This very act of consecrating [the body parts]
beginning with the genitals is designated "atma-raksha" (self-protection).
This is the fourth nyasa.

Now the fifth nyasa is stated: "Shiva-sankalpa (the hymn of auspicious
resolve) for the heart; Purusha-sukta (the Vedic hymn on the Cosmic Person)
for the head; Uttara-narayana for the topknot (shikha); Apratiratha for the
armor (kavacha); Pratipurusha for the eyes; Shataraudriya (the Shri Rudra
hymn) for the weapon (astra); and bhur bhuvah suvah om for the directional
binding (digbandha)."

The meaning of this is: Having recited the Shiva-sankalpa-sukta consisting of
twenty-six verses beginning with "yenedam bhutam bhuvanam bhavishyat," one
should place it at the heart with the gesture "namah to the heart." Having
recited the Purusha-sukta, one should place it at the head with "svaha to the
head." The anuvaka (section) "pratipurusham ekakapa lam nirvapati" — and the
