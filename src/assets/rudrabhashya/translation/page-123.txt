There, having established the point by saying "Know Him as Rudra, O son of Kunti, who is capable of protecting you in the battle at hand," it was declared: "Having slain them previously through His very self, you have now slain the enemies. That God of gods, Uma's consort, of immeasurable power — worship that Nilakantha (the blue-throated one), the imperishable Lord of the universe." In the Gita too it is said: "These have already been slain by Me; be thou merely the instrument, O Arjuna." Since Sri Krishna is an avatar (incarnation) of the Supreme Lord, there is no contradiction between the statements "these have been slain by Me" and "having slain them previously through His very self."

           Namo hastraya ca haniyase ca
           (Salutation to the slayer and to the exceedingly violent one)

Now, to show that the worship of the Supreme Lord is to be pursued by all — because disrespecting the Supreme Lord is the cause of great calamity — this is the sixth mantra: "Namo hastraya" etc. The slayer (hanta, himsakah) is He who slays those who, through arrogance — thinking "I alone am supreme" — act out of ignorance. The exceedingly violent one (haniyam) is He who harms them exceedingly, preceded by contempt and censure. An example of His being the slayer is Ravana: that king, it is well known, in his arrogance over his strength and sovereignty, disrespected the Supreme Lord, extended his arms to shake Mount Kailasa, and for a long time cried out in agony — his own fingers being crushed by the mountain which had been pressed down by the Lord's foot — and was eventually released by the Lord's own compassion.
