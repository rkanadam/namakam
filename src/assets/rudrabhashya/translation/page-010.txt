---
8                              Sri Rudra Bhashyam.
---

[Continuing citation from the Brahma-uttara-khanda:]

"...assigned him to the work of creation and gave him the Vedas. Then again the Lord of the Universe, the essence of all Atman (Self), gave [Brahma] the Rudradhyaya — the supreme summary of all Upanishads. This [teaching] was first honoured by the ancient sages led by Atri and others. The gods received it from them, and their disciples received it in turn. This is your supreme mantra; this is your supreme austerity. The recitation of the Rudradhyaya is, for human beings, the supreme means of liberation (kaivalya). Those who are burdened with great sins, those who have committed minor transgressions — even they, through recitation of the Rudradhyaya, attain the highest state."

Having thus introduced the subject, the text recites: "The fruit of virtuous action is heaven; of sinful action, hell — these are the two paths, created by the Creator, bounded by the Indra and Antaka (Yama). Desire, anger, greed, pride, and other [vices] — these became the sons of Adharma (unrighteousness) and the lords of hell. From anger arose Patraghna (destroyer of the worthy), and then Matravadhah (matricide). Brahmahatya (brahminicide) arose as a daughter [of Adharma]; thereafter Gurutalpa (violation of the guru's bed), Surapana (drinking of liquor), and Pulkasi-love [sinful liaisons] — these are said to be the chief sons of Kama (desire). Deva-svahara (theft of the gods' property), Brahma-svahara (theft of Brahmic property), Suvarnasteyam (theft of gold) — these are counted among the sons of Greed. Calling together these outcaste [lords of sin], Yama, the lord of the departed and overseer of sin, established them in the governance of hell. They, thus directed by Yama, all the lords of sin, filled the hells with their followers and attendant sinners. [But then], the recitation of the Rudradhyaya — a direct means of liberation — having begun to prevail, all those lords of sin fled in terror. They went and informed Yama, along with those attendant sinners: 'We have been rendered powerless to function in our respective domains of hell. Now we are not able ourselves to summon [sinners] in the world, O Lord! We have been scattered, as if without a home, by the power of Rudra-recitation. In every village, on the banks of rivers, and in holy sanctuaries — everywhere is now filled with Rudra-reciters; how can we carry out our work? Even a thousand expiations (prayashcitta) we do not reckon as anything — but the recitation of the Rudra even for a moment we cannot endure. For us, who subsist on the great sinners, the Rudra-recitation is the supreme thief; the Rudra-recitation is the great poison.' Having been thus informed by the lords of sin, Yama himself came to Brahma and reported all this to him. Hearing this report, Brahma, greatly distressed by the predicament caused by the Rudra-recitation, contrived a stratagem to obstruct it. He sent into the mortal world two daughters of Doubt — Ashraddha (lack of faith) and Durmedha (poor intellect) — disguised as the daughters of Faith. Bewildered by those two, the world became turned away from the Rudradhyaya; and Yama, having returned to his abode, felt as if his purpose were accomplished."

Similarly, in other Puranas as well this meaning is indicated everywhere; but all of that is not written here out of fear of excessive length.
