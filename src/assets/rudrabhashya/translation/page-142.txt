---
140                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

Now the seventh mantra — beginning "ārāc ca" (be far away). O God (deva), from the ghoghne (slayer of cattle) and the pūruṣaghne (slayer of men) — these are in the dative used in the sense of the genitive, meaning: from one who destroys all living beings. There is no limit to this destroyer of all worlds, as he further states with "kṣayadvīrāya" (who destroys heroes). The heroes of gods, demons, and humans — however great their strength and valor — all perish when they reach him; hence he is called kṣayadvīra (destroyer of heroes). So the meaning is: "from the kṣayadvīra." As the Śruti (sacred scripture) declares: "One whose food consists of both brahman (the priestly power) and kṣatra (the royal power); one for whom death itself is a condiment — who truly knows where he dwells?" Such being your nature, may your "sumnam" — your gracious, auspicious form (aghora, the non-terrible aspect) — be near (ārāt, in proximity) to us. Your terrible (ghora) form, may it remain far away — this is the implied meaning. (The Nighaṇṭu states that "ārāt" means both far and near.) What is stated is this: may you be present in your gracious form. And being thus present, protect (rakṣa) us from all calamities. If the enemies go to the gods in protest, then advocate (abhi-brūhi) for us — speak on our behalf. Not merely in the protection from misfortune and establishment in fortune, but also O dviṣahāḥ (you who carry both, the bearer of two powers, the giver of the twofold bliss of liberation and happiness) — grant us śarma (shelter, liberation-joy). The deity of this mantra is Śaṃsu. The meter is Triṣṭubh.
