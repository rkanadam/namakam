---
144                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

— this being an invocation that contains its own rationale — your firm (sthirā) and irresistible weapon now, O maghavan (O bountiful one, you for whom the praises, obeisances, and offerings made by us constitute your wealth, maghā) — lay it down (avatanuṣva), cause it to be withdrawn. Even though your weapon is irresistible, yet it should be turned aside by you who are the protector of those who bow to you — this is the meaning. Moreover, O mīḍvas, especially for our toka (offspring in general) and particularly for our tanaya (son), give (mṛḍaya, grant) happiness.

The seer (ṛṣi) and deity (devatā) of this mantra are both the Lord himself. The meter is Triṣṭubh. Meditation verse: "One should meditate on him whose body is draped in elephant-hide (gaja-carma), who wields the blazing trident, who removes all sins — the slayer of the elephant-demon (kuñjara-bhedin)." This mantra is primarily for granting the boon of excellent sons and similar results. Mantra-siddhi is attained by reciting five thousand times on each of nine days, preceded by performance of the Ekakṛcchra (a specific purificatory observance).

    mīḍhuṣṭama śivatama śivo naḥ sumanā
    bhava | parame vṛkṣa āyudhaṃ nidhāya kṛttiṃ
    vasāna ācara pinākaṃ bibhrad āgahi ||

    O you who shower blessings most abundantly (mīḍhuṣṭama),
    O most auspicious one (śivatama), be auspicious (śiva) to us,
    come with a good mind (sumanā).
    Laying aside your weapon in the great tree,
    come wearing the skin (kṛtti), bearing the Pināka (your bow);
    approach and draw near.

Now the tenth mantra — beginning "mīḍhuṣṭama" (O most liberal showerer of blessings). O mīḍhuṣṭama — most supremely the showerer of blessings upon those who bow — O śivatama — most supremely the bestower of auspiciousness — these being vocatives that contain their own reasons. Be auspicious (śivaḥ, the bestower of śiva, auspiciousness) to us, come with a gracious mind (sumanāḥ, with a mind well-disposed toward us), be pleased with us. The sign of your being pleased is stated thus — "parame vṛkṣe" (in the great tree). In the supremely exalted vṛkṣa (tree), namely the banyan (vaṭa).
