---
84                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

Here the word "suvantaya" expresses the reason for His lordship over herbs: since the Supreme Lord is the master of the three worlds consisting of the entire earth-sphere, His lordship over herbs — which are transformations of the earth — also follows from that same [fact]. By the word "varivaskritaya," expressing His dwelling in the body of devotees, the being-eaten-by-the-Lord aspect is revealed — as the Lord Himself says through the mouth of [scripture], conveying His role as the enjoyer: "I am not pleased in the same way by fire-sacrifice, by the oblation offered by the sacrificer at the time of revelation, as I am by [the worship of] one who moves about freely and joyfully, satisfied [with simple service], marked with the signs of his own deeds." By that [status as] enjoyer of the fruit of herbs, the Supreme Lord's lordship over herbs is indicated — on the principle that it is appropriate that the master of something be the enjoyer of its fruit. Thus it is stated. Contemplating precisely this exalted quality of being food-for-the-Lord, [scripture] declares — through Shruti, Smriti, and other sources — that food prepared by devotees is indeed food prepared [for Him], and not that prepared by others: "The one who craves inwardly [the food offered] with the heart, the Supreme One grasps with the mind; he grasps the taste with the tongue; the exclusive sinner becomes the exclusive eater" — such Shruti passages; and from the Parashara Purana: "He who always craves [the food offered] inwardly with the heart and with devotion, grasps it with his tongue as taste-filled, immortal water. But those who do not offer [food] inwardly to Bhava together with Bhavani — they grasp only excrement with the tongue, without doubt." Thus ends the eleventh.

[Mantra 12:]
Obeisance to the one of loud cry, to the one who makes enemies weep, to the Lord of foot-soldiers — obeisance.
