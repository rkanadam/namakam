120                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam

---

"from whom Vata (wind) blows out of fear" — so the Shruti says. Thus, by being terrifying even toward the great Indra, Agni and others, the Lord controls and governs them all, and this establishes His supremacy — such is the meaning.

           Namo agrevadha ya ca durevadha ya ca
           (Salutation to Him who strikes the enemy who stands before and to Him who strikes the enemy who stands afar)

Now, to show that the Supreme Lord alone — being the cause of victory in all battles — is worthy of service by all who seek protection — this is the fifth mantra: "Namo agrevadhaya" etc. Agrevadha is He who, having entered the field of battle, stands in front of His own devotee and slays the devotee's enemies before him. Durevadhah is He who, even before battle has commenced, breaks the strength and valor of those who obstruct His devotee's path, even when they are stationed far away. The quality of being Agrevadha (one who strikes from the front) was stated by Arjuna in the Drona Parva (of the Mahabharata): "In the battle, while scattering the enemy forces with showers of arrows, I beheld before me a blazing Person, resplendent as fire, hurling a blazing spear. In whatever direction He proceeded, the enemies were shattered in that direction, O great-minded one. After He had burned all the enemies, I then pursued and slew them. The people thought that the army, routed and fleeing, was conquered by me." This same meaning is narrated in the Ashramanik section: "From the preceding night of Arjuna's prowess onward, the Lord smote the army with His trident, striking against what faced Arjuna. O great king, who could challenge that army, even in thought — guarded as it was by Drona, Karna, and Kripa with their mighty bows, and by Maheshvara Himself?"
