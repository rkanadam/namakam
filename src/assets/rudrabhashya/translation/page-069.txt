Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                   67

---

    "Salutation to Hirikeśa (the one with golden or dark locks), to the one wearing the sacred thread (upavītin). Salutation to the lord of all flourishing beings (puṣṭānāṃ pataye namaḥ)."

Now the fifth [mantra]: "Namo Hirikeśāya iti" — Hirikeśa refers to one whose matted locks are full of lustre and golden-green colour, signifying eternal youth (nityayuva), as it were. Upavītin — the one wearing the sacred thread (yajñopavīta), the primordial Brahmin (ādibrāhmaṇa). In the Sāmaveda, after introducing [the Lord] as "Virūpākṣo'si dantākiḥ" (you are the multi-eyed, the sharp-toothed), the text declares: "You are the Brahmin among the gods; I am the Brahmin among men; truly, a Brahmin approaches a Brahmin. I approach you." Based on this Vedic statement establishing the Lord as the primordial Brahmin, and from statements in texts such as the Pārāśara Saṃhitā: "The one who is the Brahmin among gods — from Brahmā to the most stationary, the one who knows kingly power and medical wisdom — Śiva is the superior Brahmin above all." The word "puṣṭa" here refers to one who has been fulfilled with sovereignty and prosperity, such as those holding the positions of Indra and the like. He who is the lord of all beings who are thus fulfilled — "puṣṭānāṃ pataye namaḥ." In this [mantra], the connection of the Supreme Lord, the primordial Brahmin, who by necessity is to be worshipped by all from Brahmā onwards, [with the word] "Upavītine" — thereby indicating that by bestowing prosperity upon his devotees and thereby showing his grace as the lord of the prosperous — is revealed. He who is himself fully prosperous grants prosperity to others. The Lord's eternal youth (nityayuva) implies the perpetual presence of prosperity in body, sense organs and the like; the appropriateness of the fact that he is the lord of the prosperous in the world — this inner fitness (aucitya) is conveyed by the word Hirikeśa.
