---
10                             Sri Rudra Bhashyam.
---

[On the Shataraudriya's supremacy in the Upasana-kanda (section on meditation):]

The text now demonstrates its supremacy in the upasana-kanda (the section of the Veda dealing with meditative practice) through the statement: "Therefore, one becomes established in Avimukta (the eternal abode)." Here, the word "tasmat" (therefore) refers back to the Shataraudriya study mentioned in the earlier sentence "He who studies the Shataraudriya"; thus the meaning is: "Through the study of the Shataraudriya, one becomes established in Avimukta." This means: one has as if performed the upasana (meditative practice) of taking refuge in Avimukta; one attains the fruit of the upasana of Avimukta. By this, its supremacy in the upasana-kanda is proven.

For in the Jabala Upanishad, the upasana of Avimukta is prescribed in the words "Therefore one should resort to Avimukta," and its fruit is stated in "As the life-breaths depart from a being, Rudra imparts to him the Taraka Brahman (the liberating knowledge of Brahman), by which he becomes immortal and is liberated." There, the upasana of Avimukta is of two kinds — adhyatmika (internal, pertaining to the individual self) and adhibhautika (external, pertaining to the physical location). The adhyatmika form is: meditation on the Supreme Lord at the junction of the eyebrow-center and the nose [i.e., the ajna chakra, the point between the brows]; the adhibhautika form is: the vision of the Supreme Lord in Kashi (Varanasi), the sacred field of Avimukta. Such is the settled determination of the Shankara school of interpretation.

Now, the fruit of both these forms of upasana — namely, the knowledge called Taraka Brahmavidya (the saving knowledge of Brahman), which is the knowledge of Atman — is easily obtained through mere recitation of the Shri Rudra, without the laborious effort involved in the upasana of Avimukta.
