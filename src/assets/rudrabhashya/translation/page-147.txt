Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    145
---

— a hundred yojanas in breadth, the divine banyan tree, your abode — "That tree, a hundred yojanas in height, with roots extending over hundreds of mountain-ranges, casting a pure and unobscured shade, purified and free from heat — in that great tree, beneath its excellent shade, the gods saw Śiva seated." In that supreme tree, having laid aside your fearsome weapon — so that we, with it laid aside, might not be struck down — place it there and approach; and as you come, wearing the kṛtti (the tiger-skin) as your garment, bearing (bibhrat) the Pināka (your great bow) in your hand as your ornament, approach and draw near. "Pināka-armed, wearing the tiger skin" and "the Lord of Prāṇa, wearing the tiger skin, bearing the Pināka" — as the Śruti attests. Such a form of yours is most praiseworthy. The import is this: the Supreme Lord's wearing of a hide is not prompted by desire for material gain — for he who is the giver of lordship to Indra and all the gods, who is the Lord of all, Kubera and others serving as his attendants — it is inconceivable that he would lack for fine garments. Rather, his wearing of animal skin is intended to demonstrate, to those who are attached to sense objects, his complete indifference (vairāgya) to the objects of enjoyment, his perfect fullness in self-bliss (ātmānanda), his state of self-contentment. This very point is established in the Bhāgavata Purāṇa — "Unfortunate ones (durbhagāḥ), those ignorant of the nectar of their own Self, mock the conduct of the Lord, who seeks nothing beyond his own ātman — whereas, those who have realized that the Ātman is better served by garlands, ornaments, and unguents —
