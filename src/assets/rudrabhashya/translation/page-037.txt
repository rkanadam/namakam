                        Upoddhāta (Introduction)                             35

[Continuing the description of the first nyāsa, explaining specific mantras]

Regarding "saśirā jātavedā" — one tradition holds that this refers to the verse-mantra: "saśirā jātavedā akṣaraṃ padaṃ vedānāṃ sāramuddhṛte jātavedase śirase svāhā" (the imperishable word, the essence of the Vedas, is extracted for Jātavedas, the head — svāhā). Another tradition holds that the six mantras "jātavedase," "soma-marātīyataḥ," "agne tvaṃ pāraya," "agne naya supathā rāye," "viśvāni no durgahā," and "pūrtanājitaṃ saha" are referred to by "saśirā jātavedāḥ." The practice is to be determined by one's own tradition (ācāra).

The mantra 'adhyavocat' is to be placed [on the body] with the utterance 'hum' for the armor (kavacāya hum). The mantra 'namo bilmine' is to be placed with 'hum' for the sub-armor (upakavacāya hum). The mantra 'namo astu nīlagrīvāya' is to be placed with 'namaḥ' for the third eye (tṛtīyanetram). The mantra 'pramukhca dhanvanaḥ' is to be placed with 'phaṭ' for the weapon (astram).

This completes the first nyāsa, which extends from the crown-tuft (śikhā) to the eye (akṣa) together with the directional binding (digbandha).

Now the second nyāsa is described: One is to place the salutation 'Oṃ namo bhagavate rudrāya' [as follows]:
"Place the syllable Oṃ on the crown of the head (mūrdhni);
The syllable 'na' then at the nose (nāsikā);
The syllable 'mo' at the forehead (lalāṭa); and 'bha' at the center of the face (mukha-madhya);
The syllable 'ga' at the throat region (kaṇṭha); place 'va' at the heart (hṛdaya);
Place 'te' at the right hand (dakṣiṇa-hasta); place 'ru' on the left side (vāma);
Place 'drā' at the navel region (nābhi); place 'ya' on the two feet (pādayoḥ)."

Its meaning: After the first nyāsa with the directional binding described above, one recites the mantra 'Oṃ namo bhagavate rudrāya' and pays salutation. Then one places: 'Oṃ — namaḥ to the crown of the head (mūrdhne namaḥ)' at the crown; 'naṃ — namaḥ to the nose (nāsikāyai namaḥ)' at the nose; 'moṃ — namaḥ to the forehead (lalāṭāya namaḥ)' at the forehead; 'bhaṃ — namaḥ to the face (mukhāya namaḥ)' at the face; 'gaṃ — namaḥ to the throat (kaṇṭhāya namaḥ)' at the throat; 'vaṃ — namaḥ to the heart (hṛdayāya namaḥ)' at the heart; 'teṃ — namaḥ to the right hand (dakṣiṇahastāya namaḥ)' at the right hand; 'ruṃ — namaḥ to the left hand (vāmahastāya namaḥ)' at the left hand; 'drāṃ — namaḥ to the navel (nābhyai namaḥ)' at the navel; and 'yaṃ — namaḥ to the two feet (pādābhyāṃ namaḥ)' at the feet. This is the second nyāsa.

Now the third nyāsa is described: "Place Sadyojāta (sadyam) at the two feet;
Place Vāmadeva (vāmam) at the middle of the belly (udara-madhya);
Place Aghora (aghora) at the heart (hṛdi);
Place Tatpuruṣa (tatpuruṣam) at the face (mukhe);
Place Īśāna (īśānam) at the crown (mūrdhni) — [this is] 'Haṃsaḥ' known as Sadāśiva.
Having performed this nyāsa procedure, one then worships [the deity] with palms joined (sapuṭam ārcayet)."
Then [follow the mantras:] 'trātābhimantrya', 'tvaṃ no agne', 'sugaṃ naḥ panthām', 'asunvantam', and 'tattvā-[dhāvana]'...
