30                        Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

In the Talavakāra Upaniṣad (Kena Upaniṣad), it is narrated: In a war between the gods and the demons of old, victory had come through the gods' own power, and they were filled with pride. To humble their arrogance, Śiva appeared before them in the form of a Yakṣa (spirit-being), and when Agni — who had come near, summoned by the desire to know the nature of that form — was set the challenge of burning even a single blade of grass placed before him, Agni could not burn even that much; similarly, Vāyu could not carry away even a single blade of grass. Then, when the gods asked "Who is this divine Yakṣa-being?", the blessed goddess Haimavatī Umā — the presiding deity of higher knowledge (brahma-vidyā), who came forward before the bewildered gods to reveal the nature of that being — taught them that the one who had appeared in the form of a Yakṣa was none other than the Supreme Lord himself (Parameśvara). And the gods recognized that the glory of that victory belonged entirely to the Supreme Lord — so runs the narrative, which is also elaborated in the Purāṇas.

Further, the passages — "The wind blows in fear of Him; the sun and fire also..." (Taittirīya), "By the fear of Him, the wind blows, by fear of Him the sun rises, by fear of Him Agni and Indra..." (Kaṭha), and "Rudra in fire, Rudra in the waters, Rudra in the plants, Rudra who has pervaded all the worlds" — are to be understood in connection with this narrative. Thus, since it is the Supreme Lord who is being praised, whose dependent (tādriya), whose power is subject to His (tad-āyatta-śakti) — since Agni cannot possess the qualities of lordship over all (sarveśvaratva), being the self of all (sarvātmakatva), and the like — the [Yakṣa narrative demonstrates that even] Agni, Indra, and all the worlds are [dependent upon and] eulogize the Supreme Lord alone. This quality of being praised by all the worlds is uniquely the indicator of the Supreme Lord, not of Agni.
