Upodghata (Preface/Introduction).                           43

[Then various upachara (service) offerings are made:]

Cloth (vastra) [is offered] with "vamadevaya namah." Sacred thread
(upavita) [is offered] with "jyeshthaya namah." Achamaniya (water for
sipping) [is offered] with "rudraya namah." Ornaments (bhushana) [are
offered] with the Pranava (Om). Fragrance (gandha) [is offered] with "kalaya
namah." Aksata (unbroken rice) [is offered] with "kalavikaranaya namah."
Flowers (pushpa) [are offered] with "balavikaranaya namah." Incense (dhupa)
[is offered] with "balapramathanaya namah." A lamp (dipa) [is offered] with
"sarvabhutadamanaya namah." Food-offering (naivedya) [is offered] with
"manonmanaya namah."

Then one offers eight flowers [to the deity] with eight mantras: "bhavaya
devaya namah" and the others [of the ashta-murti, the eightfold form]. Then
one should recite the Rudra-tanu-mantra (mantra for the body of Rudra)
"aghorebhyo tha ghorebhyah" before [the image]. Then one should recite the
Rudra-gayatri "tat purushaya vidmahe" a hundred times, or an unlimited
number of times, or at least ten times.

Then one should pronounce the auspicious benediction (ashish) over [the
Lord]: "Ishanah sarvavidyanam" (the Ruler of all knowledge). Thereafter,
while continuously pouring a stream of water from a pot over [the Lord's]
crown, one should recite the eleven anuvaka-s [of the Shri Rudra] beginning
with "namaste rudra manyave." One should also recite the twelve other Raudra
mantras such as "sarvo vai rudrah" and "ima rudraya." After the recitation,
one should recite one anuvaka at a time from the eleven anuvaka-s beginning
with "agnavishnuh sajoshasema."

One who seeks the removal of sins, the cure of illness, prosperity, peace,
long life, or good health should perform this [rite]; by performing it thus, one
attains all these [results]. One should offer a great oblation of payasam (rice
cooked in milk) and other such offerings. Then one should give a dakshina
(sacrificial fee) — eleven cows, or at minimum one cow if eleven are not
available, as stated by the revered Bodhayana. Thus the completeness of
