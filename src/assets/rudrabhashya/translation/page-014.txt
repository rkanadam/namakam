---
12                             Sri Rudra Bhashyam.
---

[On the Shataraudriya's supremacy in the Jnana-kanda (section on knowledge):]

The text demonstrates the jnana-kanda supremacy through the statement: "One of whatever ashram should always recite the Rudra, or at least once. By this one attains knowledge — the destroyer of the ocean of samsara." Here, through the phrase "always or at least once" (sarvada sakrid va), it is stated by way of option (vikalpa) that even a single recitation of the Shri Rudra can produce the attainment of knowledge. This constitutes the argument for the jnana-kanda supremacy. For it is established in the "Avritti-adhikarana" (the section on repetition [in the Brahmasutras]) that the jnana-kanda consists of the Upanishads, and that they are to be studied repeatedly for the sake of attaining knowledge. Thus, the Upanishads — which are to be repeatedly studied — are to be recited continually; but the Shri Rudra produces knowledge through even a single recitation. Hence its supremacy over all the jnana-kanda is proven.

Although, as with other Upanishads, repeated hearing, reflection, and deep meditation (shravana, manana, nididhyasana) until the direct realization of Brahman-Atman are certainly required even for the Shri Rudra Upanishad — two modes of arising of knowledge are possible here: one through the method of hearing, reflection, and so on; and another through the method described in the Jabala Shruti — "By these verily one becomes immortal" — where, without hearing and the rest, by mere recitation alone, the divine names of the Supreme Lord, which are themselves eager to liberate, bring about the arising of knowledge through the grace of the Lord who thereby approaches of his own accord. For the first mode, the repeated recitation of the Shri Rudra prompted by [the practice of] hearing and so forth is applicable. For the second mode, through the prior conditions of devotion to sacred ash (bhasmanishtha) and firm attachment to the Supreme Lord as taught in the Kurma Purana, knowledge arises through Shiva's grace from the daily single recitation of the Rudra. Taking the second of these two modes as the basis, the intent of the Shruti is this: the supremacy of the Shri Rudra over all [texts of the jnana-kanda] is established.
