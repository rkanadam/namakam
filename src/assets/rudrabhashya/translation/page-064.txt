In the Sāmaveda as well, they are described as lords of all through [the text beginning]: "Adhipatāya kṣatrapatalāya" and so forth. In the epic Bhārata also, in the worldly Parvan section: "Those who perform actions of desire, the perfected ones (siddha), the lords of the three worlds, the eternally blissful, the ever-joyful, the eloquent (vāgīśa), free from envy; those who, though possessing the eight sovereign powers (aiśvarya), are not astonished; before whom the Lord Bhava (Śiva) is perpetually present in his activities — those who have attained the auspicious union with Bhava through study, brahmacarya (celibate study), austerity (tapas), and self-restraint (dama)" — thus stated. And in the Droṇa Parvan: "His divine attendants, of various forms" and so forth — they are described as inexhaustible. In the Purāṇas also, there are well over a thousand statements concerning their greatness, and some will be cited at the point [in the later mantra] "sahasrāṇi sahasraśaḥ." Thus, "those sattvānaḥ, the pramathaganāḥ of this Lord — to them I offer salutation (namaḥ akaraṃ — I have performed namaskāra)." The seer and the deity of this mantra are the Blessed Lord himself. The metre is Triṣṭup. [The dhyāna-śloka is:] "One should meditate upon Śiva seated on a throne, of a form brilliant like the autumn moon, of cool radiance, together with Umā (his consort)." This mantra [is efficacious] for those who desire to obtain a good son. The entire procedure of purascaraṇa (preliminary ritual consecration) is to be found in the [ritual compendium called] Arṇava. Thus [ends] the ninth [mantra].

    "O Lord (Bhagavan), loosen the two ends of your bow and the bowstring on both sides. And the arrows that are in your hand — scatter them far away."
