102                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

      Now the fourth [mantra]: "Namaḥ kapardine" — Kapardas (matted hair coils) are the Lord's mass of matted locks; the one who possesses them is Kapardī. Through this name, the great glory of the Lord's matted-hair-bearing warrior Vīrabhadra — well-known from the Bhāgavata [Purāṇa] in the verse "Kill Dakṣa and his sacrifice; the hero of my matted locks shall lead the battle-forces for me" — is suggested. Thereby is indicated the glory of the destruction of Dakṣa's sacrifice, well-attested in the Śruti: "The gods were about to perform a sacrifice; Rudra would have destroyed that sacrifice" — and so forth.

      He whose hair is cut off (vyuptāḥ) and shaven (śuṇḍitāḥ) is Vyuṣkeśa (the shaven-headed one) — referring to the great Paramahaṃsa wandering ascetic (parivrājaka). This is established by the Śruti: "The wandering renunciant wears one garment, is surrounded by disciples, carries a water-pot and is shaven-headed; those who are knowers through wisdom wear the sacred thread of knowledge (jñānayajñopavītin)." Accordingly, renowned [forms of the Lord] such as Dattātreya and Durvāsas — well-known from the Jābāla Upaniṣad and similar texts — and in the Kali Age, the Śaṅkarācārya Svāmins — well-known from the Purāṇas as divine incarnations of the Supreme Lord, as stated in "Śaṅkara will descend with four accomplished disciples" and "Śaṅkara is Savitā and Agni" — are meant by this form. Through this name, the role of the Lord as the initiator of the tradition of Brahma-vidyā (knowledge of Brahman) is indicated, as well-attested in the Śruti: "He is the lord of the Brahman, the overlord of Brahman" (brahmadhipatiḥ brahmaṇo'dhipatiḥ).

      Homage to Sahasrākṣa and to Śataghānvane.

      Now the fifth [mantra]: "Namaḥ sahasrākṣāya" — He whose eyes are a thousand, i.e., infinite in number, is Sahasrākṣa (the thousand-eyed one). Through this name, the Lord's omniscience (sarvajñatva), well-attested in the Śruti by "He who is all-knowing," is indicated.
