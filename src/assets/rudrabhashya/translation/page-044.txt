42                    SRI RUDRA BHASHYAM

May Agni stand in the stomach. May the Vasus stand in the throat. May
Sarasvati stand in the mouth. May Vayu stand in the nostrils. May the Moon
and Sun stand in the eyes. May the Ashvins stand in the ears. May the Rudras
stand in the forehead. May Pinaki (the bearer of the bow Pinaka, i.e., Shiva)
stand at the back. May Shuli (the trident-bearer) stand in front. May Shiva
and Shankara stand at the two sides. May Vayu stand on all sides. Thereafter
may the deity stand on all sides, surrounded by a garland of flames. May all
deities stand in all the remaining [parts]. May they protect me.'

Having performed the nyasa (ritual placement) according to the scriptural
injunction as in "agnih me vaci shritah" (Agni is established in my speech),
one should worship oneself with fragrance, flowers, incense, a lamp, and
food-offerings made in silence.

Then one should prepare [the Lord]: 'You have been worshipped by humans,
by siddhas (perfected beings), by celestial nymphs and others. With devotion
I worship You; accept me, O Maheshvara.' And also [with the mantra]
"tryambakam." After this internal worship, the procedure of external worship
is described:

'May the benevolent Hari (Vishnu) come quickly — his banner gleaming,
with swift white horses, victorious over Vata (the wind) and others, mighty,
with the speed of the mind — O Sharva (Rudra), come swiftly for my oblation
— om.' With this, one should invoke [the deity]. For an established [image],
there is no invocation.

Then the seat [is offered] with "sadyojatam" [mantra]. Foot-water (padya)
[is offered] with "bhave bhava." Arghya (respectful offering of water) [is
offered] with "bhavodbhavaya namah." Achamaniya (water for sipping) [is
offered] with "vamadevaya namah." Then one bathes [the image] with three
verses beginning with "apo hi shtha," with four [verses] beginning with
"hiranyavarnah," after purifying [the water] with the anuvaka "pavamanas
suvarjana," and also with "sarvo vai rudrah," "kaya nash chitrah," and
"apo va idam sarvam."
