150                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

— capable of creating and dissolving all those worlds. Mutually devoted to one another and mutually offering salutations to each other. Ever dear to Śiva, bearing the marks and characteristics of Śiva. Some are gentle (saumya), some terrible (ghora), and some are mixed (miśra), encompassing the atmospheric and luminous realms as well. Some are deformed (virūpa) and some are of fine form (surūpa), bearing the various forms of the Lord. They too are divine attendants (pāriṣada) of various forms. Some are dwarfish (vāmana), some bear matted locks (jaṭila), some are shaven-headed (muṇḍa), some long-necked (kambu-grīva), some of great valor (mahodra). Some bear tridents (śūla-hastāḥ), some bear swords (khaḍga-hastāḥ), and all of them wear wondrous crowns. They slaughter the armies of enemies with irresistible and resistible valor. They grant the wishes of those who invoke them. They are the lords of the three worlds, accomplished siddhas. With these, his own emanations, the great lord Partha (Śiva) together with Mahāśvara — may he together with all his divine host grant liberation, fearlessness, and bliss to us." In the passage — "some are saumya, some ghora, and some miśra" — the Rudras described in the Taittirīya text as "aghore bhyo 'tha ghorebhyo ghora-ghoratarabhyaḥ sarvebhyaḥ sarva-śarvebhyo namas te astu rudra-rūpebhyaḥ" (salutations to the Rudra-forms who are non-terrible, terrible, and most terrible-terrible) are indicated. Their cosmic role in creation and dissolution, and the distinction between their terrible and non-terrible aspects, is established in the Liṅga Purāṇa. As stated therein — beginning with "by the mere remembrance of Rudra, these lords of gaṇas (attendant hosts) of innumerable great souls manifest" — it is said: "Those lords of gaṇas — great beings who are the great lord of all worlds — bowing before the God and Goddess, spoke these words: 'For what purpose have you remembered us, O God? Command us, O supreme Lord. Shall we dry up the oceans? Shall we destroy dharma with our servants? Shall we slay the armies of Mṛtyu (Death), the swift, the Sun himself?
