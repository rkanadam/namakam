---
154                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

    ye tīrthāni pracaranti sṛkāvanto ni-
    ṣaṅgiṇaḥ ||

    Those Rudras who roam the sacred fords (tīrthāni) —
    bearing fangs (sṛkāvantaḥ) and swords (niṣaṅgiṇaḥ) —

Now the ninth mantra — beginning "ye tīrthāni" (those at the sacred fords). Those who bear fangs (sṛkāvantaḥ, equipped with sṛkvaṇīs — this being a Vedic lengthening of the vowel for Chandas) and who are sword-bearers (niṣaṅgiṇaḥ, carrying swords) — those Rudras who roam about the sacred fords such as the Gaṅgā and others — some to grant the fruit of the pilgrimage (tīrtha-phala) to certain devotees, and to withhold it from others — for them [the formula applies].

From "ye pathām" to "ye tīrthāni" — these two verses form a single mantra. The seer of this mantra is Devañca. The meter is Anuṣṭubh. The deity is Rudra. This mantra is primarily for granting the fruit of successful undertakings (subhna-phala). Mantra-siddhi is attained by reciting five thousand times, preceded by performance of the Kṛcchra (purificatory observance).

All of the preceding — beginning with "sahasrāṇi sahasraśaḥ" — are stated under a single [heading], with all particulars gathered together.

    ya etāvantaśca sūryāṃsaśca diśo
    rudrā vitasthire | teṣāṃ sahasrayojane 'va
    dhanvāni tanmasi ||

    As many Rudras as these, and as many as the rays of the sun,
    who have spread in all directions (diśo vitasthire) —
    their bows we unstring at a distance of a thousand yojanas.

Now the tenth mantra — beginning "ya etāvantaḥ" (as many as these). "Beginning with 'sahasrāṇi sahasraśaḥ'" — as many Rudras as have been mentioned up to this point — these many (etāvantaḥ, this number), and beyond them, even more numerous than the rays of the sun (sūryāṃsaḥ, like the countless rays of the sun) — those who are innumerable (beyond count) and presently existing — those Rudras who have spread out and pervaded all the ten directions (diśo vitasthire, stationed in every direction) — for them: "teṣāṃ sahasra-yojane ava dhanvāni tanmasi" (their bows we unstring at a distance of a thousand yojanas). The seer and other particulars here are as before.
