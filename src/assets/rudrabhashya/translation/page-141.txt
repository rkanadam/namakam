---
Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    139
---

Do not harm (rīriṣaḥ). And further, do not slay (mā avadhīḥ) our heroes (vīrān), our excellent ones. But then one may ask — when you yourself have transgressed, why should one not be harmed? To this he answers with the phrase "viṣmantaḥ" (bearing oblations). The word havis refers to oblatory offerings such as caru (rice boiled in milk) and puroḍāśa (sacrificial cake), or it may refer to devotional offerings — as stated in the Bhagavad-scripture: "What do mortals do for me who am complete in myself? Yet it is the devotion (bhāva) with which offerings are made that I accept in all sincerity." And as stated in the Gītā: "Whatever is offered to me with devotion by a person of pure mind, that I accept." We who are endowed with such oblations would serve (vidhema, worship) you with namasā (obeisance, prostration). By the principle "Prostration is the refuge; indeed, for those who are on the path of devotion, it is the greatest means" — even I who am most sinful am deserving of compassion, such is the sentiment.

The seer and deity of this mantra are both the Lord himself. The meter is Jagatī. Meditation verse: "One should contemplate Rudra — whose body, though covered by a single skin-garment, shines with the purity of crystal; whose form is effulgent; who bears the trident and the crescent moon as crown, and who ever abides in that state." The fruits of this mantra — increase of longevity (āyuṣya-vṛddhi), growth of sovereignty (rājya-vṛddhi), good rainfall (su-vṛṣṭi), victory in litigation and worldly transactions, gain of a thousand gold coins, removal of great sins (mahāpātaka-nivṛtti), attainment of power of attraction (vaśya-siddhi), conquest over all people — these and similar results are elaborated in the Arṇava and other texts. The method of mantra-siddhi (ritual perfection) is also to be seen there.

    ārāc ca ghna uta pūruṣaghne kṣayadvīrāya
    sumnam asme te astu | rakṣā ca no adhi ca
    deva bṛṃhayā ca naḥ śarme yaccha dviṣahāḥ ||

    O God, be far from the slayer of cattle and the slayer of men;
    may your grace (sumna) come to us who destroy heroes.
    Protect us and build us up, O God;
    grant us shelter (śarma), O you who bear two powers.
