                        Upoddhāta (Introduction)                             33

The great preponderance of fear (bhaya-bhūyastva) is indeed itself an indicator of Parabrahman (Supreme Brahman), as the śruti declares: "By the fear of Him the wind blows..." and "Great indeed is the thunderbolt; it is spoken of as dreadful" and the like. Therefore, salutation is not an indicator of mere eulogy (stuti). As for the prayers for attaining desired things and avoiding undesired things — although these too can be indicators of eulogy, they apply in the case of someone other than the Supreme Lord, not to the Supreme Lord himself. If that were otherwise — and if the Puruṣasūkta is taken as forming a single sentence together with the Uttara-nārāyaṇa [section] — then in the Uttara-nārāyaṇa's statements such as "destroy this, destroy the vital breath, destroy all" — which illustrate the pattern of prayer — the Puruṣasūkta itself would also become a text of mere eulogy (stuti-mātra), and then the content it conveys — namely, Amṛteśānatva (lordship over immortality), Virāḍ-svarūpa (the cosmic form), and the like — would also be without ultimate import. What would your response be to such an objection? Therefore, this whole debate has become unnecessarily prolonged.

Thus, the supremacy of Śrī Rudra among all Vedic texts — well-established through śruti, smṛti, Itihāsa, and Purāṇa — and its intended meaning (tātparya) have been elaborated at length.
