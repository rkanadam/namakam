52                    SRI RUDRA BHASHYAM

Make that arrow shiva (auspicious) — O You who are shiva-kara (the maker
of auspiciousness), the intent [of the address] is: You who can make the
arrow auspicious, [please do so]. In what does the auspiciousness of the
arrow consist? That is answered: "ma himsih" — do not harm our men
(purusha) — our sons, grandsons, and others; and [do not harm] our cattle,
buffaloes, homes, gardens, fields, and all the rest that is ours. This
constitutes the making of the arrow auspicious. Such is the intent.

Now, since the [first] address "girishanta" has already served its purpose,
the additional address "giritra" conveys a distinct meaning — let us show
what that is: The promise of the Supreme Lord is: "Even if one has done
something improper, if he takes refuge in me, I shall protect him." Hence it
is heard in the Nabhanedishtha anuvaka (section): having first narrated the
story of Manu's son [Nabhanedishtha] — "Manu Nabhanedishtha yajnavasau
rudra bhagacha so 'danat 'mama va ime pashava' iti udurve 'mahyam
imani' ity atvabibhat vai tasya ta ishata ity atrabhavit 'yad yajnavasau
hiyate mama vai tad' idi tatsadyagravara-tunabhyavetyamu" — having recounted
this story of Nabhanedishtha, and then, addressing him, [Shiva's] promise
is heard: "yajne ma bhajatha te pashvabha abhiyamsye" (worship Me in
the sacrifice and I shall give you cattle). So too in the Puranas and in
esoteric texts, many statements of this kind are found on this subject.

Accordingly, "Giritra" means: He who, abiding in the speech (giri) that
has the form of a vow (pratijnya), protects (trayate) those who worship
Him — O Giritra! [The intent of this address is:] even for one who has
acted improperly [but has become Your devotee] — in the manner of
Nabhanedishtha — protect their sons and grandsons who worship You.

Thus [ends] the fourth mantra.
