---
Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    153
---

    ye vṛkṣeṣu saspiñjarā nīlagrīvā vilo-
    hitāḥ ||

    Those among the trees who are reddish-golden (saspiñjarā),
    blue-necked, and deeply red (vilohitāḥ) —

Now the fifth mantra — beginning "ye vṛkṣeṣu" (those among the trees). Just as in the various realms there are Rudras standing as sovereigns over the earth, so too [they dwell] in trees. Among them, some are saspiñjarāḥ (of the color of young, growing saplings, with a golden-green hue), and some are vilohitāḥ (especially red, of a deep crimson color). Such are those who are nīlagrīvāḥ (blue-necked) — for them [the formula applies] as before.

    ye bhūtānām adhipatayo viśikhāsaḥ ka-
    pardinaḥ |

    Those who are lords of the bhūtas (elemental beings and spirits),
    some without topknot (viśikhāsaḥ), some with matted locks (kapardinaḥ) —

Now the sixth mantra — beginning "ye bhūtānām" (those who are lords of the bhūtas). Of the bhūtas — the various hosts of elemental beings, spirits, and the like — those Rudras who are lords (adhipatayaḥ). Among them, some are viśikhāsaḥ (without a topknot, shaven-headed), and some are kapardinaḥ (with bound matted locks, jaṭā-bandha-upetāḥ). For them [the formula applies].

    ye anneṣu vividhyanti pātreṣu pibato
    janān ||

    Those who pierce (vividhyanti) people through food —
    and those who [pierce] people who drink from vessels —

Now the seventh mantra — beginning "ye anneṣu" (those in food). Those Rudras who lurk, in a hidden manner, in food that is to be eaten — they pierce (vividhyanti) people by inflicting various diseases and disorders through impure or unwholesome food. And those who lurk, hidden, in vessels (pātreṣu) — in bowls containing water, milk, and other liquids — who pierce (vidhyanti) the people who drink from them. For them [the formula applies] as before. From "ye vṛkṣeṣu" to here, these two verses form a single mantra. The seer and other particulars are all as before.

    ye pathāṃ pathirakṣaya ailavṛdā yavy-ughaḥ ||

    Those who are guardians of the paths (pathirakṣayaḥ),
    who are nourishing (ailavṛdā, providers of food),
    who are restrainers of the wicked (yavy-ughaḥ) —

Now the eighth mantra — beginning "ye pathām" (those of the paths). The word "pathin" here denotes both worldly paths and Vedic (spiritual) paths. The entire compound "pathirakṣayaḥ" (guardians of the paths) is also connected with "pathām" — meaning: guardians of the paths (laukika-vaidika-mārga-rakṣakāḥ). This guardianship is further substantiated by two epithets — "ailavṛdā" and "yavy-ughaḥ." The word "irā" denotes food; a collection of irās is "iram," and "airam" is the same; those who bear (vibhratīti) it are "ailavṛtaḥ"; the same are ailavṛdāḥ (with Vedic phonetic modifications of consonants and vowel lengthening for Chandas). They are providers of food — nourishing the righteous. "Yavi" means one who is mixed with (miśrīkṛta) sin — therefore a sinner; those who restrain (yuṣyantīti) sinners are "yavy-ughaḥ" (again with Chandas forms). They are restrainers of sinners. Thus, being guardians of worldly and Vedic paths means: for the virtuous (sat-puruṣāḥ), it is facilitating their passage on those paths and connecting them with the fruits thereof; for sinners, it is blocking their passage — such is the meaning. Such are those Rudras — for them [the formula applies].
