Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                   61

---

    "May there be salutation to the blue-throated one (Nīlagrīva), to the thousand-eyed one (Sahasrākṣa), to the boon-granting one (Mīḍhuṣe). And likewise may I offer salutation to those attendant beings (sattvānaḥ) of his."

Having thus propitiated the solar form through the seventh and eighth [mantras], now through the ninth [mantra] one offers salutation to seek the grace of Śrī Nīlakaṇṭha, who dwells on Mount Kailāsa: "Namo astu" — to Nīlagrīva, to the one of the form of the blue-throated one (Nīlakaṇṭhamūrti), may there be salutation. Of what nature [is he]? To Sahasrākṣa — the word "sahasra" (thousand) denotes the unlimited, as is known from the Smṛti: "Viśvam, śatam, sahasram ca — all these are used to mean 'all' or 'the totality'." He whose eyes are in the thousands — to him, Sahasrākṣa, meaning one of infinite, unbounded vision. By this [epithet] it is implied: "Even though you dwell on Kailāsa, my salutation here is within the field of your vision." Again, of what nature [is he]? To Mīḍhuṣe — the granter of desires to those who prostrate before him. By this [epithet], the capacity to bestow as the fruit of salutation the fulfilment of all desired objects is indicated. And by [the epithet] Nīlagrīva — meaning "one who bears the blue throat caused by drinking the Kālakūṭa poison" — the well-known glory of the Lord is implied: "The wise one takes credit for the virtue and does not become pained by the fault; he first honours it on his head and then holds the latter at the throat." By this, the Lord Supreme, who contains the entire mass of faults (like the kālakūṭa poison) within himself without allowing even a fraction to escape, yet honours every little virtue on his head, is revealed as one who is easy to worship.
