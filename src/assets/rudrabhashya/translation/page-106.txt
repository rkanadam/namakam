104                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

His supremacy over all the gods — His status as their divine patron — is confirmed. And through this, the Lord's supremacy over the entire universe (viśvādhikatva) is indicated, as well-attested in the Śruti: "He who is the source and the dissolution of the gods," "Rudra, the great sage, is supreme over the universe" (viśvādhiko rudro maharṣiḥ), and "The other deities are partial aspects" — and so forth.

      Homage to Hrasva and to Vāmana.

      Now the eighth [mantra]: "Namo hrasvāya" — He who is of small measure in bodily form is Hrasva (the small one). Through this name, the glory of upāsanā (meditative worship) through the Dahara Vidyā (the meditation on the infinite Brahman within the tiny space of the heart) is indicated, as well-attested in the Śruti: "Even though [the ātman] is tiny, it is free of sorrow, pervading the sky." Vāmana is the Great Gaṇapati, whose limbs such as the feet are contracted — the meaning being: "to Him in the form of Gaṇapati." This is because the Incarnation of the Supreme Lord as the Great Gaṇapati is well-attested in the Mantraśāstra (tantric scriptures), Purāṇas, and other authorities. Through this name, the glory well-attested in the Śruti — "We call upon You, O Gaṇapati, lord of the hosts (gaṇānāṃ tvā gaṇapatiṃ havāmahe)" — is indicated.

      Homage to Bṛhat and to Varṣīyas.

      Now the ninth [mantra]: "Namo bṛhate" — Freedom from the threefold limitation of space, time, and object is what is established in Brahma Mīmāṃsā (Vedānta) as "bṛhattva" (the nature of Bṛhat, the immeasurably vast); hence, the meaning is: "to the Supreme Brahman (Parabrahman), who is Bṛhat." The Atharvāśiras Śruti declares: "Why is it called the Supreme Brahman? Because It is vast (bṛhati) and makes all things vast (bṛṃhayati) — that is why It is called the Supreme Brahman." Through this, the status of the Lord as the Supreme Brahman is indicated, as declared in the Śrutis: "Brahman is truth, knowledge, infinite" and "Sat-Brahman, knowledge, infinite is Brahman" — and so forth.
