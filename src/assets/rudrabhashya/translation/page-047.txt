|| Om ||

|| SRI RUDRA BHASHYAM ||

———*———

Om, salutation to You, O Rudra, to Your anger, and also to Your
arrow — salutation. Salutation to Your bow and to Your two arms
— salutation to them.

        I bow in constant adoration to the blissful one, the source of bliss,
          the gracious one,
        Of the nature of the Self, endowed with self-knowledge;
        The king of yogis, worthy of praise, the physician of the disease of
          worldly existence —
        To Shrimad Rudra, the eternally auspicious. || 1 ||

        Salutation to Dakshinamurti,
        Who is the moon-like nectar filling the lake of the assembly of the
          wise,
        Protector of all the worlds. || 2 ||

Now we shall explain the meaning of the Rudropanishad. Here there are
eleven anuvaka-s (sections/chapters). In the first [anuvaka], [the devotee]
seeks the grace of the Lord. In the second through sixth, there is salutation
to the Lord in [His aspects as] the Self of all beings (sarvatmaka), the
indwelling controller of all (sarvantaryami), and so forth. Then in the tenth
[anuvaka] there is a prayer to the Lord. In the eleventh —
