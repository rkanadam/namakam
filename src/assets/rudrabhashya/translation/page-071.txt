Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                   67

---

[continuing the fourteenth mantra commentary]

and repeating "ubhābhyām" (to both) — "and also salutation to your bow (dhanvane)." Thus [ends] the fourteenth [mantra].

    "Let the weapon launched from your bow protect us from all sides. And that quiver of yours — place it here, far away from us, in the midst of our enemies."

Now, after offering salutation, [the devotee] again prays: "Pari te dhanvana iti" — O Blessed Lord, the weapon (heti) — meaning an arrow — belonging to your bow (dhanvanaḥ), and [thus] understood as having been placed upon the bow — let such a weapon (heti) protect us from all distress (viśvataḥ — from all sides), making us well-guarded. But how, when there is a mass of sins present, can protection from distress [caused] by you come about? That is addressed: "Atho — and also, your quiver (iṣudhi) in which your arrows rest — place that (tam dhehī) in our enemies' quarter (āre — in the multitude of adversaries), in the mass of our sins, and destroy [them there]." Thus [ends] the fifteenth mantra. For the pair of verses beginning "Yā te heti" and so forth, the metre is Anuṣṭup; the seer and deity are the Blessed Lord himself. [The dhyāna-śloka is:] "May one perpetually meditate upon the Father of the worlds, the holder of the Pināka (bow), whose lotus feet are reddened with the pollen of the garlands of flowers worn on the crowns of the hosts of prostrating immortals, seated with his consort." This mantra is primarily [efficacious] for the destruction of enemies.
