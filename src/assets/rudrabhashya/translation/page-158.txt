---
156                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

— the "namaḥ" (salutation) at the beginning of the mantra is the verbal salutation (vācika). Thus we who bow with all three faculties (body, mind, and speech) — that which we hate (yaṃ dviṣmaḥ) and that which hates us (yaś ca no dveṣṭi) — that enemy, who embodies our sinful deeds and their fruits and the instruments thereof — O Rudras, into your open mouth (jaghme, the open jaws) we place (dadhāmi) him — meaning: destroy him. The same connection applies to the second and third mantras. The Rudras who dwell in the atmosphere (antarikṣe) — whose arrows are wind (vāta, their arrow being a weapon of affliction): they afflict sinners by producing various diseases through wind-disorders, and through generating violent storms and the like — this is the meaning. The Rudras who dwell in the heavens (divi) — whose arrows are rain (varṣima, rain-related) — by excessive rainfall, drought, and the like they afflict sinners — the rest is to be understood everywhere as it pleases. The entire collection of yajus (Yajurvedic verses) beginning with "namo rudrabhyaḥ" forms a single mantra. Here the deity and seer are both Rudra. The meditation verse is the same as what was stated under "sahasrāṇi sahasraśaḥ" — that is to be used here as well. This mantra is primarily for warding off evil influences and harmful acts (añcamasya-di-prada). The method of puraścaraṇa (ritual practice for mantra-perfection), the variations in the results such as neutralizing hostile acts (kṛtyotpādana), pacification (śānti), subjugation (stambhana), and the like — these are to be found elaborated in the Rudra-Kalpa and other texts.

    tryambakaṃ yajāmahe sugandhinṃ puṣṭi-vardhe-
    nam | urvārukam iva bandhanān mṛtyor mukṣīya
    mā 'mṛtāt | yo rudro agnau yo apsu ya
    oṣadhīṣu yo rudro viśvā bhuvanā viveśa
    tasmai rudrāya namo astu | tasmai stuhi yaḥ khi-
