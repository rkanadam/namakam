148                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

— or perhaps the dual refers to the two arms that hold the bow and arrows, while the Lord possesses countless arms. O Blessed One, in your arms the weapons (hetayaḥ, missiles) are thousands upon thousands — sāhasrāṇi (in the thousands) and sahasradhā (in a thousandfold manner). The word "sahasra" (thousand) here denotes infinity. This means: there are infinite kinds by the distinctions of bow, sword, and spear in form; and within each single type, there are infinite varieties. As the Śruti declares: "From a single spear (śūla) thousands of spears flash forth by its own energy." Of all those weapons, turn the faces (mukhāni) away — make them face the opposite direction (parācīnā, turned away from us) — since you are the Īśāna (the Lord, the Ruler) who has the power to recall even weapons already discharged. The seer of this mantra is Nārada. The deity is Śrī Rudra. The meter is Anuṣṭubh. Meditation verse: "One should meditate on the God who removes all sins, who is adorned with all ornaments, who holds all weapons, who is the great lord of all worlds." This mantra is primarily for warding off adversities (upadrava-parihāra). Mantra-siddhi is attained by reciting one thousand times, preceded by the Ekakṛcchra (purificatory) observance.

Thus ends the Tenth Anuvāka (chapter).
