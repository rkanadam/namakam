---
Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    151
---

Shall we bind Indra together with the gods? Bring Viṣṇu the eternal! Shall we bring the wrathful Śankha (the demon), together with the Daityas (demons) and Dānavas? For whose adversity shall we create this terrible calamity by your command? For whose festival of triumph, O God, filled with the wishes of the gods, shall we act?'" The statement from the Mahābhārata "tasyā pāriṣadā divyā nānā-rūpa-dharāḥ prabhoḥ" (the divine attendants of the Lord bearing varied forms) refers to the Rudras described in the Śruti as "divyā gaṇā bahu-rūpāḥ purāṇāḥ" (the divine ancient hosts of many forms). In the Sāmaveda too, in the Sāman (chant) known as Devavrataḥ, the Rudras who are stationed in that divine realm — described as "ye divi-ṣado" (those who dwell in the heavens) — are set forth. These are further elaborated in the Śaiva Purāṇa: "They indeed are the dwellers in heaven (divi-ṣadaḥ), the dwellers in the atmosphere (antarikṣa-sadaḥ), and so too the dwellers on earth (pṛthivī-sat) — thus are they praised by the Devavrataḥ hymn." The divine abodes of these Rudras who are lords of their respective realms — established by the Śvetāśvatara Upaniṣad and similar texts with the phrase "the sons of the immortal [Brahman] dwelt in the divine abodes" — those abodes, those innumerable divine realms, the multitudinous histories of their attendant hosts (gaṇa-koṭi) — all these are elaborated in detail in the Śiva Purāṇa. For fear of length, they are not written here. This anuvāka is composed with reference to these Rudras. In this anuvāka there are thirteen mantras. Of these, the first is — "sahasrāṇi" (thousands). They exist in innumerable thousands; and they exist in groups (sahasraśaḥ, a thousandfold) stationed in formations. Such are those Rudras who dwell above the earth (adhiḥ bhūmyām, over the earth) as its sovereign lords — "adhi" (over) indicating their lordship. For them — their bows (dhanvāni) — at a distance of a thousand yojanas (sahasra-yojane), at a very remote distance from us — we unstring (avatatanmasi); meaning: by praises and salutations, [we cause them to unbend their bows —]
