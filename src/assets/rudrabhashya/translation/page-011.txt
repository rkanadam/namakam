[Smriti texts on the expiatory power of Rudra recitation:]

The Shatatapa Smriti declares: "One who has drunk liquor, visited the abode of a guru's wife, committed theft, or caused the death of a brahmin — if he is covered with sacred ash and sleeps on a bed of ash, and recites the Rudradhyaya, he is freed from all sins."

The Atri Smriti declares: "A knower of dharma who has recited even eleven Rudras — even if he has been touched by great sins — is certainly freed from them; of this there is no doubt."

The Yajnavalkya Smriti declares: "A drinker of liquor, a thief of gold — if he recites the Rudra while standing in water, or recites the Sahasrashirsha (the text beginning 'sahasrashirsha') — he is freed from all transgressions."

A Smriti text declares: "One who recites the Veda even once is purified that very day. One who recites eleven Rudras is purified at that very instant."

Similarly, there are many other passages that could be cited. Thus, through the declaration that the Shataraudriya averts all sins — "He who studies the Shataraudriya becomes purified as if by fire... purified from things done and left undone" — its supremacy over the karma-kanda (the ritual section of the Veda) is demonstrated. For the karma-kanda is helpful toward knowledge by virtue of generating the purity of mind (chitta-shuddhi) required for knowledge; and such purity of mind — which consists of the removal of sins generated by performing all the rites prescribed in the karma-kanda — is achieved at once through a single recitation of the Rudra. Thus its supremacy over the entire karma-kanda is established.

Nor should one object: "Since the Shri Rudra produces purity of mind, which is the fruit of the karma-kanda, does it not also produce the fruits of the karma-kanda such as heaven? How then can it be superior to those?" The Suta Samhita explains: "Just as a tree's branches flourish when the root is nourished, so when Shiva is pleased through Rudra-recitation, all his associated deities are equally pleased." Thus for the devoted reciter of the Rudradhyaya who pleases the Supreme Lord, the fruits of pleasing Indra, Agni, and other deities — namely heaven and other results — no longer need to be separately sought, because those very fruits, in an eager competition as it were, present themselves of their own accord. This is established by declarations such as: "Whatever one desires, one attains it — of this there is no doubt," and "For one desirous of the other world, or one wishing to be freed from disease, or desirous of prosperity, or desirous of peace, or desirous of long life, or desirous of health — by performing this [Rudra recitation] he attains all these." Furthermore, Shaiva Purana passages such as "One who recites the Rudra with the mind fixed on Maheshvara — [gains results] far surpassing [a gift of] land extending to the oceans, adorned with mountains and forests, filled with wealth, gold, and healing herbs; such is the result of a single Rudra recitation" — by these, the Shri Rudra's capacity to produce results exceeding the specific fruits of each karma [in proportion] is established. Therefore it is conclusively proven that the Shri Rudra is supreme over the entire karma-kanda by virtue of generating all its fruits. This is precisely why the ritual acts of Agnihotra, Darsha-purnamasa, Panchayajna, Chaturmasya, Jyotishtoma, Sattrasavana, and Samvatsara — all karmas — are brought within the scope of the Shri Rudra through their results, and their status as ancillaries of the Shri Rudra is asserted in the Shri Rudra Laghu-nyasa through [formulas] such as "Aghihotratmane hridayaya namah" (salutation to the heart, which is the essence of the Agnihotra) and so on. But let that pass for now. Let us follow the main topic.
