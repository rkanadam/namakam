---
                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    97
---

[Mantras 8 through 16 — a continuous group:]
Obeisance to those with chariots, to those without chariots — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to those in chariots, to the chariot-lords — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to the armies, to the army-leaders — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to the chariot-drivers (kshattrih), to those who hold the reins (sangrahitrih) — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to the carpenters (taksha), to the chariot-makers (rathakara) — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to the potters (kulala), to the blacksmiths (karmara) — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to the weavers (punjishtha), to the forest-dwellers (nishada) — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to the arrow-makers (ishu-krit), to the bow-makers (dhanva-krit) — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to the hunters (mrigayu), to the dog-keepers (shvani) — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to the dogs (shvabhyah), to the dog-lords (shvapatibhyah) — obeisance to you all.

Now the eighth [commentary]: — "Namo rathibhyah" (Obeisance to those with chariots). "Rathinah" means those who have chariots; "arathah" means those without chariots. The nine mantras from the ninth onward, ending with "shvapatibhyah" (dog-lords), are clear in their meaning. Among these: "kshattrih" are the chariot-drivers (sarathi — charioteers); those who hold back and control the chariot are the "sangrahitarih"; there is a distinction between these two as subtypes of chariot-controllers; "karmarah" are iron-smiths (lohakara); the "punjishtha" are those who gather sand-mounds (valuka); "nishadah" are forest-hunters; those who carry nets with dogs tied to them are the "shvan" (dog-keepers).
