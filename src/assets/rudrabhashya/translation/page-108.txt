106                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

"O Rudra, we shall sing of You to strengthen You with our praises; like a bull bellowing at the cattle-call, You have expanded in both directions through Your own help (ubhayī-virāse) and You have surpassed through descendants"; "I hear You as the most healing among healers (bhiṣaktamaṃ tvā bhiṣajāṃ śṛṇomi)"; and "To one whose deeds are evil and whose deeds are equal, we shall not offend You in any act — O You who are saluted by all the gods" — thus is displayed from Śruti and Purāṇa the Lord's unique status as the sole object of hymns and salutations from all deities without exception.

      Homage to Agriya and to Prathama.

      Now the eleventh [mantra]: "Namo agriyāya" — He who stands first, prior to the arising of the world (jagadutpatti), is Agriya (the primordial one). Now this "primordialness" (agriyatva) belongs to Hiraṇyagarbha (the cosmic creative principle), as established by the Śruti: "Hiraṇyagarbha was born first." Hence the text further specifies: "Prathamāya" — Hiraṇyagarbha too is first [in the manifest order], but even he is the offspring of a cause (kāraṇasūti), meaning: "to Him who is the cause even of Hiraṇyagarbha." The Śvetāśvatara Śruti declares: "He generated Hiraṇyagarbha first." Through this name, the Lord's status as the ultimate primordial cause (agra-kāraṇatva) is indicated, well-attested in the Śruti: "When there was darkness, there was neither day nor night, neither being nor non-being — Śiva alone existed" — and so forth. Although this was already implied by the mantra "namo bhavāya," the present mantra emphasizes the Lord's role as the generator of Hiraṇyagarbha who himself first brings forth the world.

      Homage to Āśave and to Ajira.

      Now the twelfth [mantra]: "Nama āśave" — He who pervades (āvṛṇoti vyāpnoti, pervades) is Āśu
