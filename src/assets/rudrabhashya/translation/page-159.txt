---
Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    157
---

    ṣuḥ sughanvā yo viśvasya kṣayati bheṣajasya |
    yakṣvāmahe saumanasāya rudraṃ namobhir devam-
    agharaṃ duvasya | ayam me astu bhagavān ayam me
    bhagavatīdaḥ | ayam me viśvam eṣajo 'yaṃ
    śivābhimarśanaḥ | ye te sahasramanta pā-
    śā mṛtyo marttyāya hantave | tāny añjhya
    māyayā sarvān ava yajāmahe | mṛtyave svā-
    hā mṛtyave svāhā | oṃ namo bhagavate
    rudrāya viṣṇave mṛtyumme pāhi ||

    We worship the three-eyed one (tryambakam), the fragrant one (sugandhinm),
    the nourisher of all (puṣṭi-vardhenam).
    Like the cucumber freed from its vine — may I be freed from death (mṛtyu),
    not from immortality (amṛta).
    He who is Rudra in fire, who is in the waters,
    who is in the herbs and plants — Rudra who has entered all the worlds —
    to that Rudra, salutation.
    Praise him who is swift-arrowed and beautifully armed,
    who is the lord of all medicine and healing.
    We worship with salutations Rudra the gracious god,
    the sinless one, that we may attain his benevolence.
    May this blessed one be mine; may this one be my bounty.
    May this one be my universal healer,
    may this one touch me with his auspicious hand.
    O Death (mṛtyo), those thousands of snares (pāśā) of yours
    set out to strike down the mortal —
    all of those we worship away with sacred rites, by your māyā (divine power).
    Svāhā to Death! Svāhā to Death!
    Oṃ. Salutation to the Blessed Lord Rudra-Viṣṇu — protect me from death.

    Thus is complete the Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam (Commentary on Śrī Rudra),
    composed by the most venerable Śrīmat Paramahaṃsa Parivrājaka Sārvabhauma
    (the supreme wandering renunciant),
    the establisher of the Advaita (non-dual) Vidyā (wisdom),
    the revered Abhinava Śaṃkara Bhagavatā.
