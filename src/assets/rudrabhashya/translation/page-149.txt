---
Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    147
---

O Vilohita (the Red One) — you who are of red color, which means white as camphor (karpūra-gaura) in essence. Therefore Tantric practitioners (tāntrikāḥ) in the meditation on the five-syllabled mantra (pañcākṣara-dhyāna) remember — "One should meditate upon him whose glow is like ambrosia drawn from a mountain of pearls" — meaning, one whose nature is pure luminous sattva (the quality of clarity and goodness). O Bhagavan (the Blessed One), possessed of many virtuous excellences and lordship, salutation (namaḥ) be to you. These forms of address contain within them the rationale for what has been said. Having thus offered salutation, the devotee prays: "yās te" — may those of your weapons (hetayaḥ), which number in thousands, all of them, fall (nivaapantu) upon some other (anyam) — upon sin — and destroy it; not upon us — this is the meaning.

The seer (ṛṣi) of this mantra is Pulastya. The meter is Triṣṭubh. The deity is Śaṃsu. Meditation verse: "One should meditate upon him with a serene and pleasant face, who is peaceful, adorned with the sacred thread, who is accompanied by Ambā (the Goddess), and who is praised by hosts of gods." The results of this mantra include protection from great obstacles, victory over enemies, and removal of fear. The method of puraścaraṇa (preparatory ritual practice): a fast of three days followed by recitation of ten thousand times, and thereafter ten thousand circumambulations of Śiva. This is the puraścaraṇa procedure for this mantra.

    sahasrāṇi sahasradhā bāhuvor tava hetayaḥ |
    tāsām īśāno bhagavaḥ parācīnā mukhā kṛdhi ||

    O Blessed One (bhagavaḥ), the weapons (hetayaḥ) in your arms
    are thousands upon thousands (sahasrāṇi sahasradhā).
    Being their Lord (īśānaḥ), O blessed one,
    turn their faces away (parācīnā mukhā kṛdhi) from us.

Now the twelfth mantra — beginning "sahasrāṇi" (thousands). The dual "bāhuvoḥ" (in the arms) is used without insisting on the strict dual meaning — the sense is "in the innumerable arms." O Bhagavan —
