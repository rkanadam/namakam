                    Fifth Anuvāka (pañcamo'nuvākaḥ)

                         ———

      Homage to Bhava and to Rudra; homage to
    Śarva and to Paśupati.

      Thus in the fourth anuvāka the Lord was saluted as the Soul of all. Now, keeping in mind the Lord's exclusive distinguishing attributes, the text begins His adoration. From this mantra onward through "drāpe," the salutations are directed toward various aspects of the Lord; each mantra from the opening salutation onward stands as a single unit, as for instance "namo bhavāya ca rudrāya ca" and so forth. The same should be understood for the others as well.

      Here, in the first two mantras, homage is offered to the Lord while contemplating His exclusive, unsurpassed glory as the cause of the birth, sustenance, dissolution, bondage, and liberation of all beings — particularly His lordship over the herd of souls (paśukulayakāritva). "Namo bhavāya ca" — He from whom this world comes into being is Bhava (bhavaty asmāt jagad iti bhavaḥ); through this name is indicated the Lord's role as the cause of world-creation (jagaj-janmahetutva), well-attested in the Śruti by "From Him indeed all these — Brahma, Viṣṇu, Rudra, and Indra — are born." "He who drives away (drāvayati) the suffering of saṃsāra (worldly existence) is Rudra"; through this name, the role of Rudra as the bestower of liberation (mokṣahetutva) is stated, as well-attested in the Śvetāśvatara Upaniṣad: "When men shall roll up space as though it were a piece of hide, then shall the end of suffering come without knowing Śiva," and in "Rudra is the liberating (tāraka) Brahman who carries [the dying] across."
