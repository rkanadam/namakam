[Mantra 4:]
Obeisance to the various-nation assemblies (vratas), to the lords of assemblies — obeisance to you all.

Now the fourth [commentary]: — "Namo vratebhyah" (Obeisance to the assemblies). "Vratah" means assemblies of various kinds of people (nana-jatiya samgha); "vratapatayah" means their lords.

[Mantra 5:]
Obeisance to the ganas (hosts of attendants), to the ganapatis (lords of ganas) — obeisance to you all.

Now the fifth [commentary]: — "Namo ganebhyah" (Obeisance to the ganas). "Ganah" means the Pramathas (Shiva's attendant hosts); their lords (patayah) are Nandikeshvara and others of similar rank.

[Mantra 6:]
Obeisance to those of distorted form (virupas), to those of universal form (vishvarupas) — obeisance to you all.

Now the sixth [commentary]: — "Namo virupebhyah" (Obeisance to those of distorted form). "Virupah" means those of deformed or irregular form (vikrita-rupa); "vishvarupah" means those of many forms (nana-rupa). Or alternatively: since Brahman (the Absolute) is ultimately without form — truly speaking, having no qualifying properties — those from whom form has truly departed are "virupa" (formless); and by the logical principle "that which is superimposed upon [Brahman] becomes the object of form," — just as silver [is falsely seen] in mother-of-pearl — the universal form (vishvarupa) is superimposed upon Brahman; hence [Brahman's manifestations] are called "vishvarupa" (of universal form).

[Mantra 7:]
Obeisance to the great ones, to the small ones — obeisance to you all.

Now the seventh [commentary]: — "Namo mahadbhyah" (Obeisance to the great ones). "Mahantah" means those endowed with the divine powers beginning with anima (the power of becoming infinitesimally small) and the other eight great powers; "kshullakah" means those lacking such powers.
