---
Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    155
---

    namo rudrabhyo ye pṛthivyāṃ ye 'ntarikṣe
    ye divi yeṣām annaṃ vāto varṣim aṣavas te-
    bhyo daśa prācīṃ dik dakṣiṇā daśa pratī-
    cīṃ diśo udīcīṃ diśo dhvāṃs tebhyo namas te no
    mṛḍayantu te yaṃ dviṣmo yaśca no dveṣṭi taṃ
    vo jagme dadhāmi ||

    Salutation to the Rudras who [dwell] on earth, in the atmosphere,
    in the heavens — whose arrows are food, wind, and rain.
    To them, ten [salutations] toward the east, ten toward the south,
    ten toward the west, ten toward the north, ten upward —
    salutation to them! May they bring joy to us.
    That which we hate and that which hates us —
    that I place into your open mouth, O Rudras.

Now, by these mantras beginning "namo rudrabhyo ye pṛthivyām" — having bowed to the Rudras stationed in earth, atmosphere, and the heavenly worlds — the devotee prays for his own happiness and for the suffering of his enemies. The mantra is to be divided into three: "namo rudrabhyo ye pṛthivyāṃ yeṣām anniṣavaḥ tebhyo daśa prācīṃ dik ... dadhāmi" is the first mantra. "Namo rudrabhyo ye 'ntarikṣe yeṣāṃ vāta iṣavaḥ tebhyo daśa prācīṃ dik ... dadhāmi" is the second mantra. "Namo rudrabhyo ye divi yeṣāṃ varṣim aṣavaḥ tebhyo daśa prācīṃ dik ... dadhāmi" is the third mantra.

The meaning of the first verse: Salutation (namaḥ) be to the Rudras who dwell on the earth — whose arrows (iṣavaḥ, instruments of smiting) are food (annam). [The meaning of] "arrows being food" is this: by causing heedless eating (apradhyāna-bhakṣaṇa) and excessive eating (atimātrāśana), they afflict some with disease; they torment others by destroying their wealth and causing hunger; they cause others yet to commit theft and such crimes for the sake of wealth, leading to punishment including death — thus food is their arrow, the instrument of their harm against sinners. To those Rudras, I make ten folded-hand salutations (añjalīḥ) facing the east (prācīḥ, eastward-facing salutations) — with all ten fingers spread out together. In making añjalīs (cupped-hand salutations) while facing east, ten such salutations come from facing east. The same is to be understood for [salutations facing] the south and the other directions. Thus I perform salutations in all directions with full folded hands — this is the meaning.
