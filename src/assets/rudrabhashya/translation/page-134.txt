Tenth Anuvaka (Chapter Ten)

---*---

   Drape andhasaspate daridran nilalohita
   esham purushanamasham pashuna ma bhe-
   mo ro mo esham kim ca namamat

   Thus, having established the foundation through the first anuvaka, the Lord was saluted through the second through ninth anuvakas by means of His qualities of being the Lord of all (sarveshvaratva), being supremely excellent (sarvottamatva), and being the inner controller of all (sarvantaryamimatva) and so forth. Now, through the tenth anuvaka, a prayer is offered. In this anuvaka there are twelve mantras in all. The first among them is: "Drape" etc. The word drapa is a term of censure, meaning one who leads creatures to wretched states; thus "Andhasaspati" means the lord of food (andhasa, which is food, and pati, lord). Daridra means one who is utterly without possessions (nishkimcana). Because half the body is dark blue and half is reddish, He is called Nilalohita (the blue-red one). Being thus addressed, the prayer is made: "Of these our people (esham) — our sons, grandsons and the rest — and of these animals — our cows, horses and the rest — do not cause fear; such is the meaning. Let not even one single thing (kimcana ekam api vastu) of ours perish or be destroyed — such is the meaning. And may none of them be made ill (mo amamatu); may none suffer from disease."
