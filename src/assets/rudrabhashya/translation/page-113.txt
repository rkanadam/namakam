                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam                    111

Varūtha is the protective shelter of the charioteer; Bilma is the helmet — a head-covering used in battle, consisting of pieces (bila-upeta); the one who wears that is Bilmī. Kavaca is the bodily protective armour (kavacaṃ śarīra-rakṣaka-kañcukam); the one who possesses that is Kavacī. Śruta is the renowned one; Śrutasena is the one whose army is renowned. Among gods, demons, humans, and others — whoever abides as Āśuṣeṇa, Āśuratha, Śūra, Avabhindan, Varmī, Varūthī, Bilmī, Kavacī, Śruta, or Śrutasena — to Him who is the essence of all those forms.

      The ṛṣi (seer) of this sixth anuvāka is the Lord Himself; He is also the devatā (presiding deity); the chandas (metres) are, in sequence, Mahāvirāṭ and Mahāpaṅkti. The dhyāna (meditation verse) is: "One should meditate on the moon placed on Gaurī's lotus-hands, on the golden mountain-bow, on the male-female form (ardhanārī), on the one of half-female nature." This mantra bestows victory over enemies, long life, wealth, [material] prosperity, sons, and the desired fruits of [all] offerings. Perfection (siddhi) of the mantra is attained by three days of fasting and eating only once on alternate days, combined with continuous repetition.

                    Thus ends the Sixth Anuvāka.
