66                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

---

    "and your bow. With that weapon of yours, free from disease (ayakṣmayā), and with your bow too — protect us from all sides."

Having thus prayed through three mantras for the withdrawal of the weapons, [the devotee] now through these [mantras] prays for self-protection: "Yā te heti riti" — O Mīḍhuṣṭama (you who grant desires to the highest degree), this address carries within it the implicit reason [for the prayer]: "the weapon (heti) of yours, whatever bow you have in hand, and with that weapon which is free from disease (ayakṣmayā — the cause of health for devotees), and with your bow too" — this is to be understood as included. "You, protect us from all sides (viśvataḥ), from all distress." Thus [ends] the thirteenth [mantra].

    "May there be salutation to your weapon (āyudhāya) which is stretched out and firm (anatatāya dhṛṣṇave). And likewise may there be salutation to both your arms and to your bow."

Now, having thus prayed, he again offers salutation: "Namaste iti" — O Blessed Lord, to your weapon (āyudhāya) may there be salutation. Of what nature [is the weapon]? Anatatāya — not stretched out, not strung [for attack]; the opposite of stretched (ānata) is unstrung (anānata), which is this. Dhṛṣṇave — capable of striking with force. And furthermore, to both your arms (ubhābhyāṃ bāhubhyāṃ) may there be salutation.
