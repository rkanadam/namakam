Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    131

---

Those who afflict and trouble the whole world are called Vikshina kas (the diminishers). Those who examine — distinguishing "these are sinners, these are virtuous" by way of scrutiny — are called Vicinvat kas (the examiners). Those who, having determined that certain persons are most sinful, proceed to execute punishment upon them, are called Anihritatah (those charged with punishment); they are the source of affliction (duhkhakara). Those who wander and spread in all directions among the sinful, pervading everywhere, are called Amivat kas (those who spread disease and affliction). To all such as these — "this salutation of yours" (namo vah), be offered. The collection of mantras from "Namo vah kirikebhyah" up to this point, constituting a single Yajus mantra in the form of various salutations, forms one mantra. Its Rishi is Bhimi. The meter is Trishtup. Rudra is the deity. The meditation verse is: "Contemplate Shiva surrounded by the blazing trident, with hands extended everywhere, with eyes and heads and faces in all directions, blazing with radiance." Mastery of this mantra is achieved through forty thousand repetitions (japa), preceded by the performance of a Prakrichhra penance. This mantra yields as its fruit — sovereignty over the elements, the quality of being irresistible, the vision of Shiva and direct experience.

           Thus ends the Ninth Anuvaka.
