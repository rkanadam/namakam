34                        Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

[Description of the Five-Part Nyāsa (Pañcāṅga-nyāsa)]

The first is the thirty-one-member aṅga-nyāsa (limb-placement) extending from the crown tuft (śikhā) to the eye (akṣa); the second is the twenty-eight-member aṅga-nyāsa extending from the crown of the head (mūrdha) to the feet (pāda); the third is the five-member aṅga-nyāsa from the feet upward to the crown of the head; the fourth is the five-member aṅga-nyāsa from the secret region (guhya) up to the top of the head (mastaka); the fifth is the five-member aṅga-nyāsa from the heart (hṛdaya) to the eye (akṣa).

The first nyāsa (placement) is described as follows: "'Yā te rudra' at the crown tuft (śikhā); 'asmin mahati arṇave' at the head (śiras); 'sahasrāṇi' at the forehead (lalāṭa); 'haṃsaḥ śuciṣat' between the eyebrows (bhruvos madhye); 'tryambakaṃ yajāmahe' at the eyes (netrayoḥ); 'namaḥ sutyāya ca' at the ears (karṇayoḥ); 'mānas toka' at the nose (nāsikāyām); 'avatanye' at the mouth (mukhe); [the two] 'nīlagrīva' at the throat (kaṇṭhe); 'namas te astvāyudhāya' at the upper arms (bāhvoḥ); 'yā te hetir' at the forearms (upabāhvoḥ); 'ye tīrthāni' at the hands (hastayoḥ); the five anuvākas beginning with 'sadyojātim' at the five fingers (pañcasvāṅguliṣu); 'namo vaḥ kirikebhyaḥ' at the chest (hṛdye); 'namo gaṇebhyaḥ' at the back (pṛṣṭhe); 'namo hiraṇyabāhave' at the sides (pārśvayoḥ); 'bindayaṃ dhanuḥ' at the belly (jaṭhare); 'hiraṇyagarbhe' at the navel (nābhau); 'mīḍhuṣṭama' at the hip (kaṭyām); 'ye bhūtānām' at the secret region (guhye); 'ye agniṣvat' at the testicles (aṇḍayoḥ); 'saśirā jātavedā' at the perineum (apāne); 'mā no mahānti' at the thighs (ūrvoḥ); 'eṣa te rudra' at the knees (jānvoḥ); 'saṃsṛṣṭajidam' at the calves (jaṅghayoḥ); 'viśvaṃ bhūtam' at the ankles (gulphayoḥ); 'ye pathām' at the feet (pādayoḥ); 'adhyavocat' as the armor mantra (kavacam); 'namo bilmine' as the sub-armor mantra (upakavacam); 'namo astu nīlagrīvāya' as the third-eye mantra (tṛtīyanetram); 'praśuśca dhanvana' as the weapon mantra (astram); and 'ya etāvantaśca' as the directional binding (digbandhaḥ).'"

Its meaning: After reciting the mantra "Yā te rudra śivā tanūḥ" (O Rudra, that auspicious form of yours...), one should say "namaḥ" [to the śikhā] and touch the śikhā and the [other listed] limbs in order. Regarding "nīlagrīvau dvau kaṇṭhe" — two nīlagrīva mantras are to be placed successively on the northern and southern parts of the throat. Regarding "yā te hetiḥ iti upabāhvoḥ" — the word 'upabāhu' refers to the region above the wrist. Regarding "sadyojātim iti pañcānuvākān pañcasvāṅguliṣu" — after reciting with the mantra for sadyojāta and saying 'namaḥ', one places [the mantra] on the two thumbs. In the same manner, one places Vāmadeva on the index fingers, Aghora on the middle fingers, Tatpuruṣa on the ring fingers, and Īśāna on the little fingers — that is the meaning.
