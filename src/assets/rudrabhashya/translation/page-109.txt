(pervader) — from the root "aśva" meaning "to pervade," with the suffix u, formed as a Uṇādi derivative with the augment ā at the beginning; the meaning is: "to Him who pervades the entire universe." The reason for this pervasion is then stated — "Ajirāya": to Him who is supremely swift in movement (gamana-kuśala), meaning: "to the swift-mover." Through these two names, the Lord's pervasion of the entire universe (nikhila-jagad-vyāpitva) is indicated, well-attested in the Śruti: "Rudra who is in fire" — and so forth.

      Homage to Śīghriya and to Śībhya; homage
      to Ūrmya and to Avaṣṭhanya; homage to Śrotasya
                  and to Dvīpya.

      Now the thirteenth, fourteenth, and fifteenth [mantras]: "Namaḥ śīghriyāya ca" — He who dwells in swift-flowing waters (śīghragā) is Śīghriya; meaning: "to Him who dwells in such rapid streams." He who dwells in auspicious water currents (śubhe udaka-pravāhe) is Śībhya. He who dwells in waves (ūrmiṣu taraṅgeṣu) is Ūrmya. He who dwells in still water devoid of sound (avaṣkhana — quiet water) is Avaṣkhanya. He who dwells in flowing streams (śrotasi pravāhe) is Śrotasya — Śībha and Śrotas are distinguished by their large and small scale respectively. He who dwells in islands (dvīpe) is Dvīpya.

      Although in a later section [of the Rudra-Namaka] similar expressions such as "namaḥ nāthāya ca vaiśantāya ca" (homage to Him of shallow waters and of small pools), "namaḥ sikatāya ca pravāhāya ca" (homage to Him of sandy beds and flowing streams), and "nama ūrmyāya ca sūryāya ca" (homage to Him of waves and the sun) will describe the Lord's immanence in the various forms of swift, rippling, and wave-filled waters — nonetheless, all the glory of water, declared in the Śruti "Waters, indeed, are everything" (āpo vā idaṃ sarvam) — and so on — belongs entirely to the Supreme Lord who is present as the inner dweller (antaryāmin) of those waters.
