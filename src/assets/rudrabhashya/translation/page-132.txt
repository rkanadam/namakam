130                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam

---

He who stands with weapon raised in a threatening posture (apaguramana) — striking those sinners (abhighnan) — afflicts some lightly in accordance with their sins (attikhaddan) — and others severely (prakhikhaddan) — such is the meaning intended. This entire group — from "Namah somaya" onward, the collection of twenty-one inner mantras in the form of salutations constituting a different type of Yajus (prose Vedic text) — forms one single mantra. Its Rishi is the Lord Himself. The meter is Anushtup. The deity too is the Lord. The meditation verses are those declared in the third anuvaka, beginning with "rupa, yavvana" etc. This mantra yields as its fruit — the state of equality with the Sages and Lords of creation, the Shiva state and direct experience. Mastery of this mantra is achieved through five hundred repetitions (japa), preceded by the performance of two krichhra penances.

           Namo vah kirikebhyo devanam hridaye-
           bhyo namo vikshina kebhyo namo vicinva-
           tkebhyo nama anihritebhyo nama amiva-
           tkebhyah

           (Salutation to you, O destroyers, who dwell in the hearts of the
           gods; salutation to those who diminish all things; salutation to
           those who examine; salutation to those who have been charged
           with punishment; salutation to those who spread disease)

With five mantras beginning "Namo vah kirikebhyah," the Rudraganas (Rudra's attendant hosts) who serve as the world's regulators are saluted in the form of a group (gana). Their hiddenness is understood from the two qualifications "to those in the hearts of the gods" — meaning, they dwell only in the hearts of the gods, and we (ordinary people) do not know them. This qualification applies to all five categories: the Karikas and the rest. Those who destroy whatever they wish are called Karikas (the destroyers).
