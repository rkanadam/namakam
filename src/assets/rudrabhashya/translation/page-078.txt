76                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

---

[continuation of Kūrma Purāṇa citation regarding those outside the Vedas being cursed to remain as great sinners (mahāpāpins)] — Thus, with those who are outside the Vedas established as great sinners, [the Purāṇa continues to explain how the Lord in his compassion composed Tantras for their gradual uplift, so that even those outside the Vedic fold would have some form of spiritual discipline rather than being entirely without recourse.]

The point is that the compassionate Lord, seeing beings who are beyond the reach of the Vedic path, instituted the Tāntrika paths as a means of partial uplift. Since it is the all-knowing (sarvajña) Supreme Lord who instituted these paths for the welfare of those outside the Vedas, he is indeed their lord (pati). Hence the epithet "pathīnāṃ pataye" encompasses both the Vaidika (Vedic) and Tāntrika paths.

By "Tviṣīmate" (the effulgent one), the light of knowledge is indicated — suggesting that the Lord's lordship over both paths is through his omniscience (sarvajñatva) and not through any deficiency or error. Thus the objection that the Lord, being associated with censured Tāntrika paths, cannot be worshipped is thoroughly refuted. The Lord, in his omniscient wisdom, has appointed paths suitable for all grades of beings — those within the Vedic fold following the Vaidika path, and those outside it being gradually elevated through Tāntrika disciplines — all ultimately for liberation (mokṣa).

Thus the third mantra salutes the Lord as the omniscient, radiant (tviṣīmat) lord of all paths — both Vedic and Tāntrika — and in the form of Saspiñjara (the golden-red one, bright like young grass illumined by the sun). This form too is a manifestation of his brilliance and creative activity throughout the universe.
