74                    Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

---

Just as cattle, horses, buffaloes and the like are, by their respective bonds, brought under the sway of their owners and are employed — either for the purpose of serving them in worship or for their practical utility — in various activities suited to the nature of those things, within those very things and in activities dependent on the capacity of those things: such is the intended import of the pāśutva (bound nature) of beings from Brahmā to the stationary, and of the Supreme Lord's patitva (lordship) over them.

Regarding what is elsewhere heard in the Vedic text — "He chose [to be]: 'Let me alone be the lord of cattle (paśūnāmadhipatirasānī).' Hence Rudra is the lord of cattle by virtue of this boon" — meaning the Lord's status as Paśupati was obtained as a boon — this [statement should be understood thus]: the world, bewildered by ignorance, being incapable of distinguishing the paśu, the bond (pāśa), and the Paśupati (lord of the bound), and hence unable to liberate itself, was shown [the Lord's Paśupatya] through the acceptance of a boon, out of compassion, making manifest [what was already his] in the world — such is the intended interpretation, as explained in the Śiva Purāṇa itself: "My lordship (patitva) and your paśu-nature — these are self-established (svataḥ-siddha). This is made clear in the world through the play of granting boons." It is also logically fitting that since the paśu-nature consists in the bond of saṃsāra, and since the paśupati-nature consists in being the binder of that [bond], both must be without beginning (anādi) — hence the statement that they were obtained as a boon is inappropriate. For without a binder, no bond is possible. In the face of the Lord's Supreme Self-nature, well-proclaimed by Śruti, Smṛti and other authorities, the proposition that [Paśupati-ship] was obtained as a boon, or that someone bestowed that boon upon him, is logically untenable in every way — let that suffice for now.

    "Salutation to Saspiñjara (the golden-red one), to the brilliant one (tviṣīmate), salutation to the lord of paths (pathīnāṃ pataye namaḥ)."
