such is the meaning intended by these salutations. Atara means the act of returning (punar-agamana); Atarya means He who abides at the return crossing. Alada means the condition of a creature (jiva) in which its actions are not yet complete, so that it receives the fruits of those actions accordingly; Aladya means He who abides in that state. Shashpa means tender young grass; Shashpya means He who abides among young grass. Phenya means He who abides in foam (phena). Sikatya means He who abides in sand (sikata). Pravahya means He who abides in flowing currents (pravaha).

           Thus ends the Eighth Anuvaka (chapter)
