36                        Śrī Rudra Bhāṣyam

[Explanation of the first nyāsa, continued]

Its meaning: After reciting the mantra "Yā te rudra śivā tanūḥ" (O Rudra, that auspicious form of yours...), one should say "namaḥ to the śikhā (crown tuft)" and touch the śikhā and succeeding [limbs]. Regarding "nīlagrīvau dvau kaṇṭhe" (the two nīlagrīva [mantras] at the throat) — the two nīlagrīva mantras are to be placed successively on the northern and southern parts of the throat. Regarding "yā te hetiḥ iti upabāhvoḥ" (the mantra 'yā te hetiḥ' at the forearms) — the word 'upabāhu' denotes the region above the wrist. Regarding "sadyojātim iti pañcānuvākān pañcasvāṅguliṣu" (the five anuvākas beginning with sadyojāta at the five fingers) — one recites with the words "namaḥ" along with the Sadyojāta mantra, and places [the mantra] on the two thumbs. In the same manner, one places Vāmadeva on the index fingers (tarjanī), Aghora on the middle fingers, Tatpuruṣa on the ring fingers (anāmikā), and Īśāna on the little fingers (kaniṣṭhikā) — that is the meaning.

Regarding "saśirā jātavedā" — one tradition (sampradāya) holds that the verse-mantra "saśirā jātavedā akṣaraṃ padaṃ vedānāṃ sāramuddhṛte jātavedase śirase svāhā" (the imperishable word, the essence of the Vedas, is offered to Jātavedas, the head — svāhā) is the mantra referred to by this phrase. A second tradition holds that the six mantras — "jātavedase," "soma-marātīyataḥ," "agne tvaṃ pāraya," "agne naya supathā rāye," "viśvāni no durgahā," and "pūrtanājitaṃ saha" — are what is meant by "saśirā jātavedāḥ." The practice is to be determined according to one's own tradition (ācāra).

The mantra 'adhyavocat' is to be placed with the utterance 'hum' for the armor (kavacāya hum). The mantra 'namo bilmine' is to be placed with 'hum' for the sub-armor (upakavacāya hum). The mantra 'namo astu nīlagrīvāya' is to be placed with 'namaḥ' for the third eye (tṛtīyanetram). The mantra 'pramukhca dhanvanaḥ' is to be placed with 'phaṭ' for the weapon (astram).

This completes the first nyāsa (ritual placement), which extends from the crown-tuft (śikhā) to the eye (akṣa), together with the directional binding (digbandha).
