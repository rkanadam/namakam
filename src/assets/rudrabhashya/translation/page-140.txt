---
138                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

[The verb] "rīriṣaḥ" (do not harm) applies throughout. "Do not harm our ukṣantam" — the son capable of sustaining the family, possessing the power to nourish — "and our ukṣitam" — the son yet in the womb — "and do not harm our pitaram" (father); also do not harm "naḥ priyāḥ tanavaḥ" (our dear bodies). The particle "ā" placed at the beginning serves as a negative (niṣedha, prohibitive) particle, functioning as an alternative to "mā." The plural "naḥ" (our/us) is used with reference to one's own people. Thus the meaning is: do not harm the great ones and the lesser ones among our kinsfolk.

The seer (ṛṣi) of this mantra is Bhṛgu. The deity (devatā) is Rudra in the form of Nandiśvara (the Lord Nandi). The meter (chandas) is Jagatī. Meditation verse: "One should meditate upon Nandiśvara — the god wearing a crescent moon as his crown, whose form blazes like the newly risen sun, and who is upheld by the lord of the gaṇas (attendant hosts)." This mantra is primarily oriented toward peace (śānti). Mantra-siddhi (perfection in the mantra) is attained by reciting it five thousand times, preceded by a three-day fast.

    mā nas toke tanaye mā na āyuṣi mā
    no goṣu mā no aśveṣu rīriṣaḥ | vīrān
    mā no rudra bhāmito 'vadhīr viṣmanto
    namasā vidhema te ||

    Do not harm our offspring (toke), our son (tanaye),
    do not harm our life span, our cattle, or our horses.
    O Rudra, do not in your anger slay our heroes.
    We, bearing oblations (havis), would worship you with obeisance.

Now the sixth mantra — beginning "mā nas toke" (do not harm our offspring). O Rudra, you who have become angry (bhāmitaḥ) on account of transgressions — do not harm (mā rīriṣaḥ) in the domain of our offspring (toke), whether progeny in general or specifically our son (tanaye, the male child). Likewise, do not harm in our lifespan (āyuṣi), our cattle (goṣu), or our horses (aśveṣu).
