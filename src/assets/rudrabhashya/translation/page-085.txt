[Mantra 11:]
Obeisance to Suvantaya, to the one who pervades [devotees] like repeated sprinklings, to the Lord of herbs — obeisance.

Now the eleventh [commentary]: — "Namas suvantaya" (Obeisance to Suvantaya). "Suvantih" means one who extends (tanoti) well-being (suvam); obeisance to Him; "varivaskritaya" — "varivah" means repeated acts of attendance or service, those who are pervaded [by Him] through such repeated attendance are the "varivaskritah" — devotees — "varivaskrita" means one who dwells [especially] in them; even though the Supreme Lord is all-pervading, this [special presence] arises from the excess of His closeness to devotees. It is precisely for this reason that the following Puranic statements are heard: "Just as fire, though it has no specific form, becomes manifest [in a vessel], so too my devotees alone, through my special proximity, are pure. What is done by Shiva's devotees is as if done by Shiva Himself — what indeed is performed for the completely full Shiva by men? The body of a devotee becomes Shiva's own body." The meaning is: obeisance to the Lord of herbs, of plants such as trees and medicinal plants — obeisance to Him.
