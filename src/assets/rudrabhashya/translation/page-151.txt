---
Eleventh Anuvāka
---

——*——

    sahasrāṇi sahasraśo ye rudrā adhi-
    bhūmyām | teṣāṃ sahasrayojane 'va dhanvāni
    tanmasi ||

    The Rudras who exist in thousands upon thousands
    upon this earth — their bows we unstring
    at a distance of a thousand yojanas (leagues).

The Lord has now been prayed to in the Tenth Anuvāka. In the Eleventh, the Rudras who are his emanations (aṃśa-bhūtāḥ) are prayed to. For, of the omnipresent Supreme Brahman who is Śiva — from whom all this [universe] proceeds — there are Rudras established as lords of their respective realms, placed by the Lord himself to govern, protect, and chastise the beings of those realms, residing throughout the entire circle of all universes (brahmāṇḍa-maṇḍala) as their presiding sovereigns, possessed of unsurpassed majesty and glory. These Rudras are heard of in the Śruti (sacred scripture): "He who is spoken of as the one Rudra — innumerable thousands [of Rudras exist]." This is amplified in the Mahābhārata: "That God is the Mahādeva (Great God), the eternal [lord] of all beings. Of Rudras there are innumerable thousands — this is certain." And in the Śaiva Purāṇas: "He who is called the one Rudra — he pervades all the worlds. Becoming Rudra in innumerable thousands, he [sustains] the entire universe." Similarly, as stated in the Nirukta (etymological text): "Without moisture (nirapas), without Indra (nindra), without the Earth (nirapabhūvāḥ) — moving in all directions (sañcakayaḥ), they roam everywhere; saluted by all worlds (sarvaloka-namaskṛtāḥ).
