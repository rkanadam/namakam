Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    123

---

"From the Sun (Aditya), from whom it arose, the bilva tree sprang up" — thus the glory arising from the leaf-nature (of the bilva), just as from its sacredness, is proclaimed in the Puranas. Likewise other trees may also be cited as examples.

           Namastara ya
           (Salutation to the Deliverer)

Now, to declare that the Lord alone is to be taken refuge in not only as the means to heaven but also as the means to liberation (moksha) — this is the eighth mantra: "Namastara ya." The Tara is the Pranava (the sacred syllable Om); salutation to Him who is of that form. For it is heard in the Atharvashiras (Upanishad): "He is the Om, the Deliverer (tarah)" — having introduced this — "He alone is Rudra, He is Ishana (the Ruler)." Similarly, in the Taittiriya (Upanishad): "May Brahman be auspicious (shiva) to me; Om, ever-auspicious one (sadashiva)." The Lord's being of the form of the Pranava amounts to the same as His being the subject matter to be conveyed by the Pranava; as the Shivapurana states: "Shiva indeed is the Pranava, or the Pranava is called Shiva. The difference between the denoted object (vachya) and the denoting word (vachaka) is nowhere absolute." And from the Shruti: "He who is beyond that which has merged into its primordial cause — He is the great Lord (Maheshvara)."

           Namah shambhave ca mayobhave ca
           (Salutation to the source of blessedness and to the source of joy)

Now, just as the drops of water in the ocean of the supreme bliss of the Self (paramatman) — which transcends the limit of the highest sacrifice — compare to all the worldly joys of creatures, so too the Lord is to be taken refuge in by all devotees. This is shown in the ninth mantra: "Namah shambhave" etc. Shambi-
