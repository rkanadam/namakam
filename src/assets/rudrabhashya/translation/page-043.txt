Regarding these mantras, one view holds that the prostration is indicated
simply by reciting them; another view holds that the prostration with all eight
bodily parts (sashtanga, like a staff falling flat) is to be performed while
reciting these mantras.

The action to be performed thereafter is stated: "Then one should meditate
upon oneself as the beautiful form of Shri Rudra —

[Dhyana-shloka, verse for meditation on Rudra's form:]

Pure as crystal, three-eyed, with five faces,
Bearing the Ganga, with ten arms, adorned with serpents as ornaments;
With a blue throat, crescent-mooned, wearing a sacred thread of serpents,
Draped with a tiger-skin upper garment, bestowing blessings and the gift of fearlessness;
Accompanied by a water-pot and a cluster of flowers, holding a trident;
Blazing with tawny matted locks, causing the topknot to emit radiance;
Purified by nectar, joyful, sharing the body with Uma;
Seated on a divine throne, endowed with divine enjoyments;
Attended by the deities of the directions, saluted by gods and demons;
Eternal, everlasting, pure, immovable, imperishable, immutable;
All-pervading, the lord Isha (ruler), Rudra, of universal form —

Having thus meditated, one who has undertaken the vow should properly
commence worship."

Now the commentary on the procedure of abhisheka (ritual bathing) is given:
"Now we shall explain the procedure of the bathing and consecration (abhisheka)
of Rudra. First, having bathed at a sacred ford, having put on freshly-washed
clothes brought in the approved manner, pure and restrained, observing
brahmacharya (celibacy), and dressed in white — having made an image of
Ishana, one should stand to the south of it, facing it, and establish the deities
within oneself: 'May Brahma stand in the generative organ. May Vishnu stand
in the feet. May Shiva stand in the heart. May the Earth stand in the belly.
