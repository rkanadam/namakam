---
90                    Sri Rudra Bhashyam
---

[Mantra 6:]
Obeisance to those who protect themselves with a srkava (a mechanical device), to those who desire to kill, to the Lord of grain-thieves (sushnatam) — obeisance.

Now the sixth [commentary]: — "Namah srkavibhya" (Obeisance to those with the srkava). "Srkavinah" means those who protect themselves with a mechanical device called srkava; "jighamsamtah" means those who desire to kill living beings; obeisance to the one of both these forms; "sushnatam" means the Lord of grain-thieves who steal grain from fields (kedara) and the like — obeisance. Here also the two qualifying epithets [serve to establish the reason for lordship over grain-thieves]: the lordship of a [person over] grain-thieves at a certain time belongs to one who is both skilled in protecting grain and who desires to oppose [thieves]. The plural [form] is Vedic usage (chandasa); otherwise there would be no [grammatical] relation between the qualifying words and what they qualify. The same applies to the next mantra also. Thus ends the sixth.

[Mantra 7:]
Obeisance to those with swords, to the night-wanderers, to the Lord of predatory thieves (prakuntanah) — obeisance.

Now the seventh [commentary]: — "Namo'simanjya" (Obeisance to the sword-bearers). "Asimantas" means sword-bearers (those carrying swords); "naktamcharantah" means night-wanderers (those who roam at night) — night-robbers; obeisance to such a form. "Prakuntah" means thieves who steal while [fleeing] — obeisance to their Lord. Here the two qualifying epithets [are given] to show the form appropriate to lordship over predatory thieves. Thus ends the seventh.

[Mantra 8:]
Obeisance to the turban-wearer (ushnishthin), to the mountain-roamer, to the Lord of land-thieves (kulunjchas) — obeisance.
