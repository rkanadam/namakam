Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    125

---

"All demons and gods who exist become equal before Me; because You are Shiva (auspicious), therefore all beings have auspiciousness, and therefore the gods are Mine." The Bharata also gives another explanation of the word shiva: "Because He desires the well-being (shiva) of all human beings, therefore the Lord is called Shiva." This explanation too ultimately arrives at the meaning of shivamkara (maker of auspiciousness), since desire (icchai) alone — mere volition — is the cause by which the Lord who is capable of creating the entire world does so. Similarly, the explanations found in other Puranic passages — "The word shiva is derived from the root 'hinsi' combined with the prefix 'vi,' and means one who subdues, or alternatively, He who is the sole treasure-house of all auspicious qualities is called Shiva by the wise who know the meaning of this truth" — all these ultimately converge upon the meaning of shivamkara. This quality of shivamkara (being the maker of auspiciousness) is also seen in a different context: "O Pavaka (Fire), be auspicious (shiva) to us" and even in ordinary speech: "May your paths be auspicious (shivah)." The mantra then qualifies this further: "and shivataraya" (to the most auspicious). Compared to all those who may be called auspicious in ordinary life and in the Vedas, the Lord is supremely and beyond all measure more auspicious — He is shivatara, the most auspicious. Hence, the Lord alone is worthy of worship — such is the meaning. This very meaning was fully established in the Atharvashiras (Upanishad) at the sixth point. For there, in answer to the question "What is the true knowledge? Who is the true teacher? And who is to be meditated upon?" it was declared: "Shiva alone is to be meditated upon — the maker of auspiciousness — relinquishing everything else." Having thus shown through the derivation of the word shiva as shivamkara that Shiva alone is to be meditated upon to the exclusion of all else, it then reveals His quality as supremely auspicious beyond all comparison —
