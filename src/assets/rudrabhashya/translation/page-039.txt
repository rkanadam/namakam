                        Upoddhāta (Introduction)                             37

Now the second nyāsa is described: One is to place the salutation 'Oṃ namo bhagavate rudrāya' as follows:

"Place the Oṃkāra syllable on the crown of the head (mūrdhni);
Then the syllable 'na' at the nose (nāsikā);
The syllable 'mo' at the forehead (lalāṭa); and 'bha' at the center of the face (mukha-madhya);
The syllable 'ga' at the throat region (kaṇṭha-deśa); place 'va' at the heart (hṛdi);
Place 'te' at the right hand (dakṣiṇa-hasta); place 'ru' on the left side (vāmataḥ);
Place 'drā' at the navel region (nābhi-deśa); place 'ya' on the two feet (pādayoḥ)."

Its meaning: After the first nyāsa with the directional binding described above, one recites the mantra 'Oṃ namo bhagavate rudrāya' and makes obeisance. Then one places:
'Oṃ — namaḥ to the crown of the head (mūrdhne namaḥ)' at the crown;
'naṃ — namaḥ to the nose (nāsikāyai namaḥ)' at the nose;
'moṃ — namaḥ to the forehead (lalāṭāya namaḥ)' at the forehead;
'bhaṃ — namaḥ to the face (mukhāya namaḥ)' at the face;
'gaṃ — namaḥ to the throat (kaṇṭhāya namaḥ)' at the throat;
'vaṃ — namaḥ to the heart (hṛdayāya namaḥ)' at the heart;
'teṃ — namaḥ to the right hand (dakṣiṇahastāya namaḥ)' at the right hand;
'ruṃ — namaḥ to the left hand (vāmahastāya namaḥ)' at the left hand;
'drāṃ — namaḥ to the navel (nābhyai namaḥ)' at the navel;
'yaṃ — namaḥ to the two feet (pādābhyāṃ namaḥ)' at the feet.

This is the second nyāsa.

Now the third nyāsa is described:

"Place Sadyojāta (sadyam) at the two feet (pādayoḥ);
Place Vāmadeva (vāmam) at the middle of the belly (udara-madhya);
Place Aghora (aghora) at the heart (hṛdi);
Place Tatpuruṣa (tatpuruṣam) at the face (mukhe);
Place Īśāna (īśānam) at the crown of the head (mūrdhni) — [this is] 'Haṃsaḥ', named Sadāśiva.
Having performed this nyāsa procedure, one then worships [the deity] with palms joined (sapuṭam ārcayet)."

[The following mantras are then to be used:] 'trātābhimantrya', 'tvaṃ no agne', 'sugaṃ naḥ panthām', 'asunvantam', and 'tattvā-[dhāvana]'...
