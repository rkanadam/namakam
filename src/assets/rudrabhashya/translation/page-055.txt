SRI RUDRA BHASHYAM.                       54

— Even though in this way [the Lord has been] propitiated through salutation
and praise, [one might wonder:] how would the averting of misfortunes [caused
by] Yama (the god of death), the unrelenting and fierce ones, and other
terrible [beings] and other gods [who are] instruments of [fulfilling the results
of] the wicked person's sins — how would their [influence] be removed? Not
knowing this, [the devotee] now desires to know the path for its removal,
[realizing] that all these [other gods] are helpless and without any capacity
[to act] independently — this is stated:

"Adhy abocat" — adhivacana means speaking on behalf of, championing
[someone's cause], as [confirmed by] the Brahmana: "yad brahmano 'shajjagad
prathameyatam brahmanaya chivruyat" (whenever a brahmin goes before the
world, he should declare [the cause of the brahmin]). The meaning here is as
follows: adhivakta — He who is of the nature of championing the devotees'
cause, that is, foremost and supreme; daivya — He who exists among the gods,
in the sense of the indwelling controller of the gods (devantaryami-artha);
bhishak — the physician of all suffering, the healer of all the afflictions of
worldly existence such as disease and others. [This is confirmed by] the Shruti:
"bhishaktamam tva bhishajam shrinomi" (I hear that You are the greatest
physician among physicians). Such a Lord adhy abocat — has declared [His
stance] on behalf of [His devotees], "chandishi lungi" — in the optative [the
aorist is used in the sense of optative], meaning He makes a declaration
of partisanship for His devotees. What does He do [while declaring this]?
While destroying (sambhayan, annihilating) all ahi-s (snakes, and other visible
harms such as thieves and tigers), and all yatudhanya-s — classes of yatudha-
ja (evil spirits); rakshas, pishachas, and other invisible assailants — adhy
abocat, He has declared [His protection].

Here, the objection might be raised: "How can the Lord, who is impartial
toward all, make a partisan declaration in your favor? Doesn't this lead to
the charge of partiality (vaishanya) on His part?" To counter this, the word
adhivakta is used — the declaration on behalf of devotees is motivated by
their act of devotion; therefore it does not bring any defect upon the Lord.
"Vaishanya-nairghrnye na sapekshatvat" (partiality and cruelty do not apply
to the Lord, because His actions are conditioned [by devotees' karma]) —
such is the principle, as stated in the Vedanta.
