[Smriti texts on the expiatory power of Rudra recitation — continued:]

The Shatatapa Smriti declares: "One who has drunk liquor, visited the dwelling of a guru's wife [in violation of dharma], committed theft, or perpetrated brahminicide — if he is smeared with sacred ash and sleeps on a bed of ash, and recites the Rudradhyaya, he is freed from all sins."

The Atri Smriti declares: "A knower of dharma who has recited even eleven Rudras — even if touched by great sins — is certainly freed from them. Of this there is no doubt."

The Yajnavalkya Smriti declares: "A drinker of liquor, a thief of gold — if he stands in water and recites the Rudra, or recites the Sahasrashirsha (the Purusha Sukta beginning 'Sahasrashirsha Purushah') — he is freed from all transgressions."

A further Smriti text declares: "One who recites the Veda even once is purified that very day. One who recites eleven Rudras is purified at that very moment."

Similarly, there are many other passages that could be cited. Therefore, through the declaration that the Shataraudriya averts all sins — "He who studies the Shataraudriya is purified as if by fire... purified from things done and left undone" — its supremacy over the karma-kanda (the ritual section) is demonstrated.

For the karma-kanda helps toward knowledge by generating the purity of mind (chitta-shuddhi) that knowledge requires; and such purity of mind — which consists of the removal of sins accrued through failure to perform the rites of the entire karma-kanda — is achieved all at once through a single recitation of the Rudra. Thus the Shri Rudra's supremacy over the entire karma-kanda is established.

Nor should one object: "The Shri Rudra produces the purity of mind that is a fruit of the karma-kanda — but since it does not directly produce karma-kanda fruits like heaven and so on, how can it be said to be superior to the karma-kanda?" The Suta Samhita resolves this: "Just as the branches of a tree flourish when its root is watered, so when Shiva is pleased through Rudra recitation, all his associated deities are equally pleased. Therefore, through Rudra recitation alone, both liberation and all material goals become accomplished." Thus, for the devoted reciter of the Rudradhyaya — who pleases the Supreme Lord — when the Lord himself is pleased, the attendant fruits of pleasing Indra, Agni, and other deities (namely heaven and similar results) need no separate pursuit, for those very fruits, competing as if in eagerness, present themselves spontaneously. This is established by Shruti and Smriti passages such as: "Whatever one wishes, one obtains — of this there is no doubt," and "For one desiring another world, or freedom from disease, or prosperity, or peace, or long life, or health — by performing this [Rudra recitation] one obtains all of these." Furthermore, by Shaiva Purana passages such as: "One who constantly recites the Rudra while meditating on Maheshvara attains a result greater than [donating] land extending to the oceans, adorned with mountains and forests, filled with wealth, gold, and medicinal plants — by a single recitation of the Rudra he obtains more than that" — the capacity of the Shri Rudra to produce fruits surpassing the specific results of each individual karma is established. Therefore it is conclusively proven that the Shri Rudra is supreme over the entire karma-kanda by virtue of generating all its fruits.

This is precisely why all the ritual acts — Agnihotra (the twice-daily fire oblation), Darsha-purnamasa (new and full moon rites), Panchayajna, Chaturmasya (four-monthly rites), Jyotishtoma (the soma sacrifice), Sattrasavana (year-long soma session), and Samvatsara (annual rite) — all karmas — being subsumed within the Shri Rudra through their fruits, are inserted as ancillaries of the Shri Rudra in the Shri Rudra Laghu-nyasa (the brief nyasa of the Shri Rudra) through formulas such as "Agnihotratmane hridayaya namah" (salutation to the heart, which is the essence of the Agnihotra) and so on. But let us leave this for now and follow the main topic.
