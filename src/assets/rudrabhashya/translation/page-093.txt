[Mantra 2:]
Obeisance to those who string the bow, to those who take aim — obeisance to you all.

Now the second [commentary]: — "Nama atanvanebhyah" (Obeisance to those who string the bow). "Atanvanah" means those who are stringing (aropa) the bowstring onto the bow; "pratidadhana" means those placing an arrow on the bowstring of the drawn bow; and all who exist in the world in such forms — obeisance to you — as before.

[Mantra 3:]
Obeisance to those who draw [the bow], to those who release [the arrow] — obeisance to you all.

Now the third [commentary]: — "Nama ayacchattva" (Obeisance to those who draw). "Ayacchantah" means those who are performing the drawing of the bowstring; "visrijantah" means those who release the arrow after drawing [the bow]; the rest here is to be understood as before.

[Mantras 4–8 — a continuous group:]
Obeisance to those who shoot, to those who pierce — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to those who are seated, to those who are lying down — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to those who are asleep, to those who are awake — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to those who stand, to those who run — obeisance to you all.
Obeisance to those who are assembled, to those who lead assemblies — obeisance to you all.
