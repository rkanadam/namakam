                    Sixth Anuvāka (ṣaṣṭho'nuvākaḥ)

                         ———

      Homage to Jyeṣṭha and to Kaniṣṭha; homage
      to Pūrvaja and to Aparaja; homage to Madhyama
      and to Apagalbha; homage to Jaghanya and to Budhnya;
      homage to Sobhya and to Pratisarya;
      homage to Yāmya and to Kṣemya; homage
      to Urvaryā and to Khalya; homage to Śrokya
      and to Avasānya; homage to Vanya and to Kakṣya;
      homage to Śrava and to Pratiśrava; homage
      to Āśuṣeṇa and to Āśuratha; homage to Śūra
      and to Avabhindat; homage to Dharmiṇe and to Varūthine;
      homage to Bilmine and to Kavacine; homage
      to Śruta and to Śrutasena.

      In the fifth anuvāka, the Lord was saluted as the possessor of diverse glories; in the sixth, He is saluted by virtue of His being the Soul of all (sarvātmakatva) and His inner presence in all things (sarvāntaryāmitva) — "Namo jyeṣṭhā-
