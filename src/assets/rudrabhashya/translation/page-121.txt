Sri Rudra Bhashyam                    119

---

"bhaya ca arunaya ca" (to the fearsome and to the ruddy one) was declared. How supremely compassionate is this Rudropanishad (the Upanishad pertaining to Rudra)!

           Namah shanghaya ca pashupataye ca
           (Salutation to the bringer of happiness and to the Lord of all beings)

Now, to show that the Supreme Lord alone is worthy of worship by all who seek happiness, on account of His being the bestower of happiness — this is the third mantra: "Namah shanghaya" etc. Shangha means one who leads to or bestows happiness (sham sukham gamayati prapaayatiiti shanghah). The reason for this is stated by the word pashupataye: He is the master and protector (pati, palaka) of all creatures (pashu), from Brahma down to the immovable. Thus the Lord alone is the cause of happiness for the entire world, because whatever happiness belongs to those who are to be nurtured (palaniya) depends upon the one who nurtures (palaka) — such is the meaning.

           Namo ughraya ca bhimaya ca
           (Salutation to the supreme one and to the terrifying one)

Now, to show that the Supreme Lord alone, being superior to all the worlds, is worthy of worship by all — this is the fourth mantra: "Namo ughraya" etc. Ughra means the highest or supreme. By the Unadi grammatical rule "kaprendradu..." with the suffix ran, the derivation yields the meaning of one who is supremely excellent. Indeed, in the mantra "You are ughra (supreme); may I be ughra among my kinsmen," the word ughra is employed in the sense of highest praise. Since no other being of that same category (sajata) possessing comparable supremacy exists, by the principle of the absence of restriction (sankocabhava-nyaya), the nature of being supremely above all — namely being superior to the entire universe — is established. The reason for this is stated by: "bhimaya" (the terrifying one). Bhima means fearsome, from the Shruti: "Out of fear of Him, Vata (wind) blows" — and so forth. Thus, even toward the great Indra, Agni and others, the Lord's fearsome quality establishes His supremacy over all — such is the meaning.
