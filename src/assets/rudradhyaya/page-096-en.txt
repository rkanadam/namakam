[Na. Na. A.] Rudradhyaya | 83

Salutations to the non-decaying ones (Vikshinakebhyah). || 16 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Vikshinakah' is the opposite of 'kshina' (decayed). It means those who are never subject to decay. || 16 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Vikshinakebhyah'. To those who cause various types of destruction ('kshina') to non-devotees through diseases, thieves, hell, etc. 'Kshinu' means violence/destruction. Those who destroy in various ways are 'Vikshinakah'. (Grammatical derivation with Vedic lengthening). Others say: 'Kshiyu' means violence; from that, 'Vikshinakah' refers to those who destroy diverse beings. The Vajasaneyins read this as 'Vikshinakebhyah'. || 16 ||

Salutations to those who distinguish (Vichinvatkebhyah). || 17 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Vichinvatkah' are those who distinguish or provide the required objects. || 17 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Vichinvatkebhyah'. To those who distinguish between the virtuous and the sinful among beings in order to reward or punish them. || 17 ||

Salutations to those who kill with certainty (Anihritebhyah). || 18 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Anihritah' are those by whom sins have been completely destroyed/removed. || 18 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Anihritebhyah'. 'Hatam' means violence. 'A' indicates limit, 'ni' indicates certainty. Those who kill within limits or with certainty are 'Anihritah'. They do not act unjustly, nor do they ignore offenders. Or: Those who kill within the limit and with certainty. Others say: This refers to those who have been separated ('ni-gamita') from other deities. It means those who have been kept separate from other gods because of the fierce nature of their shared sacrifice. There is another mantra: "O Rudra, do not be angry...". The Brahmana says: "One repels Rudra in his own direction." || 18 ||
