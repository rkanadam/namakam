64  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Sa. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Dundubhyah'—the sound (shabda) arising in the drum (dundubhi). 'Ahananah'—the stick used for striking the drum; he who is born in the form of striking (beating) that is 'ahananyah'. (1)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: For this anuvaka starting with 'Namo dundubhyaya', the Rishi is Hemakosha. Sri Rudra is the Deity. The Metre is Mahapankti. 'Namo dundubhyaya'—to him existing in the drum, of the nature of the drum-sound. It means him whose glory is well-known. Or: to him in the form of musical instruments like the drum etc. As is said: "That same speech speaks in the trees which is in the drum, in the flute (tunava), and in the lute (veena)." [Grammatical note]. 'Ahananyaya cha'—the drum is struck (ahanyate) with this, hence it is the 'ahanana' (bamboo stick); to him existing there. (1)

Salutation to the brave and to the discerning. (2)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | dhrishnave | cha | pramrishaya | cha || 2 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Dhrishnuh'—one who does not flee in battle. 'Pramrishah'—one who examines (paramarshaka) the essential reality of the enemy's army. (2)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo dhrishnave cha'—to him whose nature is to overpower. Or: to him who is skilled in protecting the entire universe. [Grammatical note]. Even though powerful, he is without partiality towards any particular field—'pramrishaya cha'—to the one skilled in discerning (examining) everything, the wise (pandita). [Grammatical note]. Another says: 'pramrishah' means one of distinguished character. For all indeed worship the God for their own desires, or for well-being, or for supreme liberation; the God, considering that as his own purpose, protects them. (2)

Salutation to the messenger and to the envoy. (3)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | dutaya | cha | prahitaya | cha || 3 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Dutah'—one skilled in conveying information (news). 'Prahitah'—a person sent (preshi-tah) by his Master. (3)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: What more? He protects his devotees even by performing tasks like being a messenger. 'Namo dutaya cha'—a 'duta' is a messenger (sandesha-hara); to him who sometimes exists in that form out of love for his devotees, or to him who acts as the messenger for the gods in the form of Agni (fire). As is heard: "We choose Agni as the messenger," "Agni was the messenger of the gods," "You are the messenger, the seer, the sentient." 'Prahitaya cha'—to the one who is sent. Out of love for his devotees alone, he sometimes takes that form as well. Or: with reference to Agni, as the sacrificer is sent (prahita) towards the gods (through him). [Grammatical note]. Or: 'prahitah' means one who is extremely beneficial (prakrishtena hitah). (3)

Salutation to the one with a sword and to the one with a quiver. (4)
