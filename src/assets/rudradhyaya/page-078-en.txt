69  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Sa. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Nishangine'—equipped with a sword (nishanga). 'Ishudhimate'—equipped with a container for arrows (quiver). (4)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo nishangine cheshudhimate cha'. Explained already. The distinction is that previously (in the third anuvaka) these two attributes were mentioned in the context of committing theft; now, however, they are in the context of protection (rakshana). The God takes up weapons even for the sake of protecting beings. (4)

Salutation to the one with sharp arrows and to the one with (many) weapons. (5)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | tikshna-ishave | cha | ayudhine | cha || 5 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Tikshneshuh'—one whose arrows (ishu) are sharp (tikshna). 'Ayudhi'—one who possesses many weapons. (5)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah tikshneshave cha'—to him with sharpened arrows. 'Ayudhine cha'—to him with many or with excellent (praiseworthy) weapons. [Grammatical note]. (5)

Salutation to the one with good weapons and to the one with a good bow. (6)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | su-ayudhaya | cha | su-dhanvane | cha || 6 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Svayudhah'—one whose weapon (ayudha) is beautiful (shobhana), in the form of a trident (trishula) etc. 'Sudhanva'—one whose bow (dhanus) is beautiful, in the form of the Pinaka. (6)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah svayudhaya cha'—to him with a beautiful weapon, or to him with an unfailing (amogha) weapon. [Grammatical note]. Or: 'svayudhah' is one who possesses weapons of a beautiful form. Furthermore, because you alone are worthy of them, they are beautiful. As it is said: "You shine with arrows and the bow." 'Sudhanvane'—to him with an excellent bow. It is mentioned separately because of its importance, or to show its extreme excellence—because it is made of gold. [Grammatical note]. (6)

Salutation to the one in the foot-paths and to the one in the main roads. (7)
