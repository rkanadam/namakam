34  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Dvi. Am.]

He speaks the twelfth Yajus:
Salutation to the one with a loud sound, to the one who makes (enemies) cry out, salutation to the lord of foot-soldiers. (12)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | uchaih-ghoshaya | akrandayate | pattinam | pataye | namah || 12 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Uchairghoshah' is he whose sound (ghosha) or noise is loud/raised (uchrita) during war. 'Akrandayate'—to him who makes enemies wail/cry (rodayita). Salutation be to that Rudra. Salutation to the protector of 'pattis'—the warriors who walk on foot (foot-soldiers) are called 'pattis'. (12)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah uchairghoshaya'—to him with a loud sound or loud hymns of praise. Or to him in the form of loud sounds like clouds, etc. 'Akrandayate'—to him who causes 'akrandana'—the loud shouting/calling in war. Or to him who makes enemies cry everywhere. It means the one who brings victory in wars. 'Pattinam pataye namah':
"One chariot, one elephant, five foot-soldiers, and three horses—these together are called a 'patti'."
To their Master. Or: to the Lord of foot-soldiers; to him who is capable of conquering all three worlds himself in the form of a foot-soldier. (12)

He speaks the thirteenth Yajus:
Salutation to the one who surrounds the whole (army), to the one who runs, salutation to the lord of the good/attendants. (13)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | kritsna-vitaya | dhavate | sattvanam | pataye | namah || 13 ||

|| Thus ends the Second Anuvaka. ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Kritsna-vitah' is he by whom the whole (kritsnam) army is enveloped or covered (vitam). Salutation be to that Rudra. 'Dhavate'—to him who runs (dhavat) behind the fleeing enemy armies. Salutation to the protector of the 'sattvanas'—those who are helpful or have taken refuge. (13)

|| Thus in the Vedartha-prakasha, the commentary on the Yajur-samhita composed by Sayanacharya-Madhava, in the fourth Kanda, fifth Prapathaka, the commentary on the Sri Rudram, ends the Second Anuvaka. ||
