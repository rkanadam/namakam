38  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Tri. Am.]

He speaks the fourth Yajus:
Salutation to the deceiver, to the double-deceiver, salutation to the lord of the thieves who hide. (4)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | vanchate | pari-vanchate | stayunam | pataye | namah || 4 ||

Sayana's Commentary: Having become a trusted person of the Master, and in his dealings like buying and selling, stealing some wealth whenever possible—this is 'vanchana' (cheating). Deception in all dealings is 'parivanchana'. Salutation to the one in both those forms. Secret thieves are of two types: those who come from a distance and steal wealth by opening doors at night without being known are 'stenas'; those who are known to the owner (insiders) and steal either at night or by day are 'stayavah' (stayus). Salutation to the protector of those 'stayus'. (4)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo vanchate'—to the one whose nature is to deceive or cheat. 'Parivanchate'—to him who goes everywhere deceiving and is not obstructed by anyone. 'Stayunam pataye namah'—'stayus' are those who walk in disguise and steal wealth, such as those in the guise of false holy men. As seen in: "He gave it to him and not to the thief (taya)." [Grammatical note on the word 'stayu'].

He speaks the fifth Yajus:
Salutation to the one who moves about secretly, to the one who moves about in public, salutation to the lord of forest-thieves. (5)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | nicherave | paricharaya | iti | pari-charaya | aranyanam | pataye | namah || 5 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Nichera' is one who has the nature of moving about (charana-shila) constantly and carefully within the Master's house itself with the intention "when shall I steal?". 'Parichara' is one who has the nature of moving about in marketplaces, streets, gardens, etc., with the intention of stealing. Salutation to him in both those forms. 'Aranyas' are thieves who stay constantly in the forest to harass travelers on the path by stealing their wealth; salutation be to their protector. (5)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo nicherave'—to him whose nature is to move about (charana-shila) secretly or constantly. [Grammatical note].
