[Na. Ma. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 21

[Word-for-word]: Ava-tatya | dhanuh | tvam | sahasra-akshah | shata-ishudhe || nishirya | iti | ni-shirya | shalyanam | mukha | shivah | nah | su-manah | iti | su-manah | bhava || 11 ||

Sayana's Commentary: He who has a thousand eyes in his form as Rudra is 'sahasraksha'. He who has a hundred quivers (cases for arrows) is 'shateshudhe'. O such Rudra, having unstrung (avatatya) the bow, making it one from which the string has been removed; having dulled (nishirya) the points (mukha) of the arrows (shalyas)—the tips of the iron pieces attached to the arrows—by keeping them hidden downward in the quivers; become 'sumana', endowed with grace toward us, and 'shiva', peaceful. (11)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: Now the second. 'Avatatya'. The third foot ends with 'mukha'. O Sahasraksha—explained already. O Shateshudhe—the idea is that one whose arrows are all over the earth must have many quivers. Having unstrung (making it unstrung) the bow named Pinaka, and having made the points of the arrows extremely broken or blunt, become 'shiva' and 'sumana' for us. By forgiving even our offenses, become 'shiva', peaceful, and you should always be one with a 'good mind' (sumana) towards me for the sake of granting auspiciousness. Or: being thus prayed for by us, and having become 'sumana', and having withdrawn the causes of fear like the bow etc., become 'shiva', free from the causes of fear.

|| He speaks the twelfth verse ||

May the bow of the matted-haired one be stringless, and his quiver be without (pointed) arrows;
May his arrows be unable to strike, and his scabbard be for the sake of (containing) the sword. (12)

[Word-for-word]: Vi-jyam | dhanuh | kapardinah | vi-shalyah | bana-van | uta || aneshan | asya | ishavah | ashuh | asya | nishangadhih || 12 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Kapardi' is he who has a matted tuft of hair (kaparda). May the bow of that Rudra be 'vijyam', without a string. And also, let the 'banavan'—the quiver where the arrows stay—be 'vishalya'. May it be without projecting arrowheads, because of the shalyas (heads) of the arrows being hidden in its interior. May the arrows of this Rudra, hidden in the quiver, be 'aneshan'—unable to pierce.
