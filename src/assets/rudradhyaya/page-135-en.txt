[Na. E. A.] Rudradhyaya | 121

He prescribes the holding of the ladle at different heights (knee-deep etc.) during the three offerings, through hand gestures:

He sacrifices at this height (knee-deep), then at this height (waist-deep), then at this height (face-deep). Three are these worlds. From these very worlds he pacifies Him. || 7 ||

He prescribes the Homa to be performed with the three Yajus "Namo rudrebhyo ye prithivyam" etc.:

He offers three subsequent oblations. Six they become. Six are the seasons. By the seasons he pacifies Him. || 8 ||

Commentary: (None provided).

He prescribes the Homa while walking around (anuparikramam) using a sequence of previous and subsequent views (purva-paksha and siddhanta):

If he were to sacrifice while walking around, he would make Rudra one who roams inside (the altar area). But they say, "In what direction is Rudra, and in what direction is He not?" One should sacrifice walking around; thus he pacifies Him without exclusion (aparivargam). || 9 ||
