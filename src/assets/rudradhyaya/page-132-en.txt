[Na. E. A.] Rudradhyaya | 119

"Everything was heard as it is, through Rudra's grace.
The eleven donations for the eleven Rudras, O sinless one.
The five-cow-ear donation, and the Shambhu-donation.
The Earth-donation for universal sovereignty, and the placement of pots.
The ritual for broken hair-tufts, and the donation of pieces.
The worship of the eleven Rudras, as heard from Soma and Vasuki.
The holy jati-smara ritual, and the family-donation.
Everything to be done by a king has been stated in full.
Again, speak of the destruction of worldly existence through the Rudra-mantra.
The ritual to conquer untimely death, especially for kings.
With the procedures, instructions, time, and place.
Everything is accomplished through this alone, as stated by Shambhu."

(Taittiriya Samhita 5.4)
He now employs this Rudradhyaya of eleven Anuvakas in the ritual:

This Agni is indeed Rudra; he is born when he is fully piled up. Just as a newborn calf desires the breast, so he (Agni-Rudra) at this time desires his share. If one does not offer an oblation to him, he might harm the Adhvaryu and the sacrificer. One offers the Shatarudriya; by that share, one pacifies him. The Adhvaryu and the sacrificer do not suffer harm. || 1 ||

Commentary: When the fire (altar) is being piled up and is completed with all layers, at that time this Fire is 'born'. This born Fire is called 'Rudra'. (Derivations of the word Rudra). Just as a newborn calf immediately desires the mother's breast, similarly this Fire named Rudra, born at the completion of the piling, desires his share. If no oblation is offered to this Fire, He might 'think' to consume the Adhvaryu and the sacrificer. Therefore, to avoid that, one should sacrifice the 'Shatarudriya'. 'Shatam' (hundred) indicates 'innumerable'. The chapter in which innumerable Rudras are described is the 'Shatarudriya'. By sacrificing with it, Rudra is satisfied by His share, and the Adhvaryu and sacrificer do not perish. || 1 ||
