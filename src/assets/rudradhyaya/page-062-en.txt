49  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Cha. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: From the ninth Yajus up to the seventeenth, he speaks Yajus whose meanings are clear.

He speaks the ninth Yajus:
Salutation to the chariots and to the lords of chariots, salutation to you. (9)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | rathebhyah | rathapatibhyah | iti | ratha-pati-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 9 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah rathebhyah'—to those in the form of chariots, or to those who are the parts of the chariot.
As is said in the Mahabharata: "The four Vedas are the horses, the chariot consists of all the gods."
And in the Bhavishya Purana:
"With the Sun and Moon as wheels, the Vedas as horses, the chariot consisting of the Maruts (winds); that chariot of the Enemy of the Cities (Purari) was fashioned by the gods, with Brahma as the charioteer."
'Rathapatibhyashcha vo namah'—to the masters of the chariots. (9)

He speaks the tenth Yajus:
Salutation to the armies and to the leaders of armies, salutation to you. (10)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | senabhyah | senanibhyah | iti | senani-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 10 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah senabhyah'—to those who play having become armies. 'Senanibhyashcha vo namah'—to the leaders of the armies. [Grammatical note]. (10)

He speaks the eleventh Yajus:
Salutation to the attendants/masters and to the charioteers (holders of reins), salutation to you. (11)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | kshattrubhyah | iti | kshatru-bhyah | samgrahitribhyah | iti | sam-grahitru-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 11 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah kshattrubhyah'—to those who train the chariots (or those who supervise). 'Samgrahitribhyashcha vo namah'—to the charioteers (sarathi) who gather/hold the reins (rashmi). (11)
