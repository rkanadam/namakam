42  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Tri. Am.]

He speaks the twelfth Yajus:
Salutation to those who throw, to those who pierce, salutation to you. (12)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | asyadbhyah | vi-dhyadbhyah | cha | vah | namah || 12 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Asyantah' are those who perform the action of making the released arrow travel to the target. 'Vidhyantah' are those who perform the action of making the arrow enter (pierce) the target once it is near it. (12)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo asyadbhyah'. From the root 'asu' (to throw). To those who, having released the arrows, make them reach the body of the enemy. 'Vidhyadbhyashcha vo namah'—to those who strike the body of the enemy. (12)

Sayana's Commentary: Starting from the thirteenth Yajus up to the seventeenth, he speaks Yajus whose meanings are clear:

Salutation to those who are sitting, to those who are lying down, salutation to you. (13)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | asinebhyah | shayanebhyah | cha | vah | namah || 13 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah asinebhyah shayanebhyashcha vo namah'. Both are well-known. [Grammatical note]. (13)

Salutation to those who are sleeping, to those who are awake, salutation to you. (14)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | svapadbhyah | ja-gradbhyah | cha | vah | namah || 14 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah svapadbhyo jagradbhyashcha vo namah'. They are indeed well-known. The accent is as stated before. (14)

Salutation to those who are standing, to those who are running, salutation to you. (15)
