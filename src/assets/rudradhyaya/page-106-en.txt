[Na. Da. A.] Rudradhyaya | 91

(Grammatical explanation continued). Now the 'prabharanam' (offering) is qualified: In such a way that by this offering, there may be peace ('sham') for our own bipeds (sons etc.) and quadrupeds (cattle etc.). (Grammatical note on 'ashat'). "Asmin grame": In this village where we live, let all living beings—even those that do not belong to us—be well-nourished and healthy. What kind of thought should we offer?

The Puranic sages say:
"In a village or city where a chanter of Rudradhyaya lives, there is no hunger, thirst, famine, or disease."

Or, in such a way that in our village, there may be peace for bipeds and quadrupeds, and every object like wealth and grain may be abundant and healthy. || 1 ||

He states the fourth and fifth [Note: 4th is here]:

O Rudra, make us happy; grant us happiness. We serve you, the destroyer of enemies, with salutations. May we attain that peace and separation from suffering which father Manu sought through sacrifice; may we reach that under your guidance, O Rudra. || 4 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: O Rudra, make us happy ('mridaya') in this world. And also ('uta') grant us happiness ('mayas') in the next world. We serve ('vidhema') you, the destroyer of our sins, with salutations. Father Manu (Prajapati), the protector, sought peace and happiness and separation from suffering; all that, O Rudra, may we attain under your guidance/love. || 4 ||
