[Na. Na. A.] Rudradhyaya | 77

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Kapardi' is one with matted hair (jata-bandha). 'Pulastih' is one who stands in front of devotees. || 3 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Kapardine'. (Explained before). To 'Pulastaye'. 'Pulastayah' means having long hair (kuntala); salutations to the one who has that. The possessive suffix is dropped. At the time of grace, the Lord sometimes comes with matted hair and sometimes with long hair. Others say: 'Pulastih' means one who always stands in front of everyone. The form is derived from 'pru-shodara' etc. This shows that even though He is of such great majesty, He is always easily accessible. || 3 ||

Salutations to the one in the cowshed (Goshthyaya) and to the one in the house (Grihyaya). || 4 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Goshtham' is the place for cows; He who is in that is 'Goshthyah'. 'Grihyah' is He who is in the house. || 4 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: To further elaborate on the Lord's accessibility, He says: Salutations to 'Goshthyaya'. 'Goshtham' is the place where cows stay. To the one who exists there. To 'Grihyaya', the one who exists in the house. || 4 ||

Salutations to the one on the couch (Talpyaya) and to the one in the mansion (Gehyaya). || 5 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Talpyah' is one lying on a bed or couch (khatva). 'Gehyah' is one who exists in a house or palace (prasada). || 5 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Talpyaya'. 'Talpam' is a bed/couch etc.; He who exists there. To 'Gehyaya'. 'Geham' is a palace etc.; He who exists there. || 5 ||

Salutations to the one in the dense forest (Katyaya) and to the one who stands in the mountain cave (Gahvareshthaya). || 6 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Katam' is a thorny forest or a place full of creepers, making it difficult to enter; a dense and inaccessible forest is 'katam', and He who exists in that is 'Katyah'. 'Gahvareshthah' is one who stands in uneven places like mountain caves. || 6 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Katyaya'. This is explained. Or He graces the world in the form of water in a well ('kata'). To 'Gahvareshthaya'. To the one who stands in mountain caves. (Grammatical explanation for 'shthah' and 'shatvam'). || 6 ||
