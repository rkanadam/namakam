(Pacification continued): Similarly, with "Namaste rudra manyave", if cows are afflicted, light a fire in the cowshed and sacrifice 10,000 Vakankata sticks. By doubling the ritual for animals, great peace is achieved.

[Ritual for killing/destroying an enemy]:
One desiring the destruction of an enemy, having performed nyasa as before and offered red-rice sacrifice to the direction-guardians—performing sixteen-fold worship with "Stuhi shrutam", chant the Rudra-Ekadashini eleven times. Then chant "Stuhi shrutam" 1,000 times daily. Within seven nights, the enemy is destroyed. One desiring to create a 'Kritya' (magical entity) should worship the deity wearing red clothes, red turban, and red garlands. In a cemetery, sacrifice 100,000 small red sticks smeared with thick oil. "Arise, red-bodied, red-eyed, red-haired girl!"—quickly satisfy her with a pot of blood and flesh. Otherwise, she might harm the performer. She, being ready, will ask "Whom shall I crush? Tell me, and if he is in the three worlds, I will destroy him." This ritual is for a single body, a village, a city, or a house. (Detailed instructions on fasting, vows like Tula-purusha, and chanting follow).

With "Pramuncha dhanvanah" and the next six Riks, enchant the weapons; enemies brought to battle will flee. They will not stand in front. Sacrifice 10,000 thorny sticks. If a powerful enemy from the Kshatriya class etc. appears—for a Kshatriya, sacrifice for 1/3 of the day; for a Vaishya, 1/6; for a Shudra, 1/4. Thus the work is unhindered. With "Vikikirida vilohita", sacrifice in the mouth of a sleeping enemy after many rounds; all will be confused and flee. With "Namo hiranyabahave" Anuvaka, sacrifice mustard seeds smeared with bitter oil for fear of a king; the whole circle of enemies is expelled. With the sound 'Phat', hang a garland upside down on the Lord, use a blood-smeared cloth with Trikatu powder, and using the Lord's hand-finger to draw lines, chant the enemy's name; the enemy with his army will be expelled. Thus an entire village can be expelled in one day. A king is finished in a day and night, a Kshatriya in half a day, a Vaishya in 1/3, and a Shudra in 1/4. This is stated by Shaunaka in Hemadri.
