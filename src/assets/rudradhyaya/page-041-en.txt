26  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Dvi. Am.]

[Word-for-word]: Namah | vrikshebhyah | hari-keshebhyah | pashunam | pataye | namah || 2 ||

Sayana's Commentary: Trees whose hair-like leaves are green (harita) in color are 'harikesha'. Salutation be to those trees which are the tree-shaped forms of Rudra. Salutation to Rudra who is the protector of cattle (pashu). (2)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: Salutation to those who are in the form of trees, or who reside in trees. The word 'tree' points to the resident by the container, as in "the platforms are shouting" (meaning people on them). 'Harikeshebhyah'—those who have leaves in the place of hair that are green, or those whose hair is green. Or: 'Vriksha'—all beings from Brahma down to a blade of grass, grown in the Cosmic Egg (Brahmanda), are indeed 'trees'. Derived from the root 'vriksh' (to cut/destroy); the meaning is those who are subject to destruction. Salutation to him who is the self of those. 'Harikeshebhyah'—to him with green-colored hair. Salutation to the Lord of cattle (pashu); to him who exists as the Master of those cattle belonging to him, whether two-legged or four-legged. As it is said: "Of which cattle Pashupati is the Lord, the four-legged and the two-legged."
And in the Purana: "All from Brahma down to the stationary things are proclaimed as 'Pashu' (tethered beings). Because he is their Lord, Bhavah is remembered as Pashupati."
In the Mahabharata: "Because he always protects the 'Pashus' and delights with them, and because he is their Overlord, he is remembered as Pashupati." (2)

He speaks the third Yajus:
Salutation to the one who is golden-red like young grass, to the radiant one, salutation to the lord of paths. (3)
