[Na. Cha. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 45

...with twenty-five stars and a crown made of peacock feathers; with a face like the full glory of the spotless autumn moon. With matted hair (kaparda) tied at the ends, wearing a soft skin; adorned with garlands held on both the left and right. Decorating the navel-region with a cluster of Dhara-Kadamba flowers; visible up to the thighs, he is to be seen even by his enemies. His wife, beautiful in all her limbs, adorned with forest ornaments; a spotless image of all forest beauties. Having given the bow and one spotless arrow into her hand; resting the second arrow on his shoulder and the rest with his left arm. Smelling a bouquet of fragrant flowers again and again with his hand; being fanned slowly with a branch of young leaves. Accompanied by boys and charming sages (or mendicants); the Guru of the worlds should be meditated upon as going ahead of the gods. Thus is the Lord of great brilliance meditated upon in the form of a hunter (Kirata)."

|| Thus in the Yajurveda commentary composed by the illustrious Bhatta Bhaskara, in the Sri Rudra-prashna Namakam, ends the Third Anuvaka. ||
|| This Third Anuvaka is completed. ||

Sayana's Commentary: In the third anuvaka, those Yajus with salutations at both ends were spoken. Now, in the fourth anuvaka, other such Yajus with salutations at both ends are being stated.

There, he speaks the first Yajus:
Salutation to the female strikers, and to you who pierce in various ways, salutation. (1)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | a-vyadhinibhyah | vi-vidhyantibhyah | iti | vi-vidhyantibhyah | cha | vah | namah || 1 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Avyadhinyah' are female forms (stri-murtayah) of Rudra capable of striking from all sides. 'Vividhyantyah' are those capable of piercing in a special or diverse way. Salutation to them. (1)
