74  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Aa. Am.]

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: Now he states that the God graces the world through specific bases (adhara). 'Namah tirthyaya cha'—existing in holy fords (tirtha) like Prayaga etc. [Grammatical note]. 'Kulyaya cha'—existing on the banks (kula) of rivers like the Kaveri etc. (12)

Salutation to the one on the far shore and to the one on the near shore. (13)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | paryaya | cha | avaryaya | cha || 13 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Paryah'—he who exists on the far shore (para-tire) of the ocean of worldly existence (Samsara), as the object of meditation for those seeking liberation. 'Avaryah'—he who exists on the near shore (avara-tire), in the midst of Samsara, as the granter of desired fruits. (13)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah paryaya cha'—to him existing on the far shore (para-tire) of rivers etc. [Grammatical note]. 'Avaryaya cha'—to him existing on the near shore (avara-tire). Someone says: "Tirthas and the like are with respect to Samsara." Tirthas are the holy assemblies (samavayas) like the Shaivas etc.; he exists in those. Staying there, he graces the world. To him existing on the banks, during times (of transition) etc. The far shore (para) is pure; the near shore (avara) is impure. (13)

Salutation to the crosser and to the deliverer. (14)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | pra-taranaya | cha | ut-taranaya | cha || 14 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Prataranah'—the cause of crossing over (tarana) sins through excellent (prakrishta) means like mantra-chanting. 'Uttaranah'—the cause of crossing out of (uttarana) this worldly existence through the form of essential knowledge (tattva-jnana). (14)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah prataranaya cha'. 'Taranam' is living or crossing. Excellence is birth in higher wombs. [Grammatical note]. He is the means for crossing over Samsara. To the cause of crossing over with excellence in this world. 'Uttaranaya cha'—to the cause of crossing out of (exiting) this world. [Grammatical note]. (14)

Salutation to the one on the stepping-stones and to the one on the stairs. (15)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | a-taryaya | cha | a-labyaya | cha || 15 ||
