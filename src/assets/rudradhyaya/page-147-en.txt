134 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Cha. Pra. A.]

(First Anuvaka continued):
...overpowering strength, life-span, old age, the soul, the body, happiness, armor, limbs, bones, joints, and bodies (organs)—may all these be mine (be granted to me) through sacrifice. || 1 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Vajah' is food. The word 'cha' (and) is for including the subsequent items. 'Me' (to me). The word 'kalpatam' (may they be granted/accomplished) mentioned in the tenth Anuvaka is to be added to all items. 'Prasavah' is the permission for food—may it be given, may it be easily digested. 'Prayatih' is purity. 'Prasitih' is the eagerness for wealth, objects, etc. 'Dhitih' is the holding/sustaining of food. 'Kratuh' is the sacrifice which is the cause of food. 'Svarah' is the accent (udatta etc.) in the chants. 'Shlokah' is praise. 'Shravah' is the ability to make others hear (one's fame). 'Shrutih' is the ability to hear. 'Jyotih' is light. 'Suvah' is heaven. 'Prana', 'Apana', 'Vyana' are the specific functions of the vital air (wind). 'Asuh' is the vital air connected to those functions. 'Chittam' is knowledge born from the mind. 'Adhitam' is the object/substance always grasped by that knowledge. The four starting from 'speech' (Vak) are well-known. 'Dakshah' is the skill in the sense-organs. 'Balam' is the strength in the motor-organs. 'Ojas' is the eighth humor, the cause of strength. 'Sahah' is the power to overpower enemies. 'Ayuh' is well-known. 'Jara' is the stage where the life-span reaches its peak... ||
