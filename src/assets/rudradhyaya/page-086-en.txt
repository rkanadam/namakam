73  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Aa. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Mayaskarah'—the maker of delight (liberation). The distinction is that these two words (Shankara and Mayaskara) express the direct cause of happiness, while the previous two (Shambhu and Mayobhu) express being the source of it through some medium. (10)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shankaraya cha'. He who habitually (tachchhilyena) produces (karoti) happiness (sham) for all beings is 'Shankara'. [Grammatical note]. Or: from the root 'shim' used for names. The God Shankara is indeed of that nature and has that name. As it is said: "The root 'shim' is used for names." Or: he who only produces happiness is 'Shankara'. 'Mayaskaraya cha'—'mayas' has been explained; to him who does that habitually. [Grammatical note]. (10)

Salutation to the auspicious and to the more auspicious. (11)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | shivaya | cha | shiva-taraya | cha || 11 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shivah'—of the form of welfare (kalyana); meaning he is himself stainless. 'Shivatarah'—excessively auspicious/stainless. (11)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shivaya cha'. Shiva is the stainless one, the one without qualities (nirguna). Salutation to the very famous Shiva who is welfare in all ways. 'Shivataraya cha'—the word 'shiva' is also used figuratively for things that cause welfare such as prosperity and liberation. For example: "Auspicious (shiva) is the path, auspicious is the means." Likewise, the God is called 'Shiva' because he is the maker of welfare (shivankara). As the Atharvashikha Upanishad says: "Shiva alone is to be meditated upon, the maker of welfare (shivankara), abandoning all else."
And in the Mahabharata:
"Because he always accomplishes all goals in all actions, desiring the welfare (shiva) of men, therefore the God is remembered as Shiva."
He who is excessively auspicious is 'shivatarah' or 'shivankarah'. Salutation to him who produces welfare in various ways. (11)

Salutation to the one in the holy fords and to the one on the banks. (12)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | tirthyaya | cha | kulyaya | cha || 12 ||

Sayana's Commentary: The meaning is that he also makes his devotees stainless. 'Tirthyah'—one present in holy fords (tirtha) like Prayaga etc. 'Kulyah'—one residing on the banks (kula) of rivers in the form of installed Lingas. (12)
