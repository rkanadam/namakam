114 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. E. A.]

Those who are the protectors of the paths (pathirakshaya), providers of food (ailabruda), and the fighters (yavyudha). || 8 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Those Rudras who are 'pathirakshayah', the protectors of both worldly and Vedic paths. Not just some paths, but all paths. 'Ailabruda': 'Ira' is food; a collection of it is 'airam' or 'ailam'. Those who bear/provide that are 'ailabhruta' or 'ailabruda'. Meaning, they are the nourishers through the grant of food. And they are 'yavyudha': 'Yu' is an enemy (one who opposes); those who fight (yudhyanti) with enemies are 'yavyudha'. Meaning, those who remove our unwanted troubles. (The rest as before). || 8 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The eighth. Those who are the protectors of both types of paths—worldly and Vedic. Those Rudras who are the lords of those paths, capable of blocking or sending beings through them. (Grammatical notes on 'pathirakshaya'). 'Ailabrudah': 'Ira' is food. They bear 'ila' (earth/food) to give to those on the right path. (Grammatical derivation and Vedic variations). 'Yavyudha': (Various interpretations of 'yu' and 'yavyudha'). Some say 'yava' refers to life/breath; those who gather/protect life. The meaning is: Among those on worldly and Vedic paths, they grant life/food to some and take it from others according to their deeds. Others say: They strike exactly what is to be struck, never missing. (Grammatical notes). || 8 ||

He states the ninth Rik:

Those who roam in the holy places (tirthani), holding sharp weapons (srikavanta) and swords (nishangina). || 9 ||
