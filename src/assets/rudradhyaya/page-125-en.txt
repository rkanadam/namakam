112 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. E. A.]

[Purashcharana for these four Mantras]: Having performed a Chandrayana penance, chant the mantra 5,000 times to purify the body. This completes the Purashcharana. One should perform black magic rituals (Abhichara) with these mantras. A king should have the Homa performed by Brahmins born in his own country. Sacrifice 100,000 sticks of Shalmali, Sala, Vetra, and Benu, wrapped in a single thread and smeared with blood. Here, having made an image of the enemy out of smooth flour, having invoked life into it, then cutting it in order, sacrifice 8,000 offerings. Then sacrifice 8,000 bundles (kurcha) smeared with blood. By these rituals, enemies perish. Countering black magic with these same mantras: sacrifice 5,000 Durva grasses smeared with curd, honey, and ghee. The defects of black magic affecting the king or the performer will perish. Meditation:

"Meditate on the one with a fierce fanged face, with blazing fire as hair, holding a trident and a lamp, and adorned with snakes."

He states the fifth Rik:

Those who are in the trees, golden-yellow like young grass, blue-necked and red... || 5 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Just as Rudras are situated in the worlds, they are also situated in the trees. Some of them are 'saspinjarah', having the golden-yellow color of young grass. 'Nilagrivah', having blue-colored necks. Some have blue color in the neck region. Others are 'vilohitah', especially red in color. Those who are of this nature (the rest is as before). || 5 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The fifth mantra. Those Rudras who are in the trees—either in the trees themselves or in all moving and stationary beings. Yellow like young grass. Those who age/destroy the tormentors of the world. Blue-necked. Specially red or free from impurity. Those who are superior as owners and protectors. (The rest is as before). || 5 ||

He states the sixth Rik:

Those who are the lords of spirits, shaven-headed or with matted hair... || 6 ||
