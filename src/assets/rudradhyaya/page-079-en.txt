66  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Sa. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shrutih' is a small path suitable only for walking; he who is worthy of that is 'shrutyah'. 'Pathah' is a wide road suitable for the movement of chariots etc.; he who is worthy of that is 'pathyah'. (7)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: Now he establishes that (the God) exists everywhere for the purpose of protecting his servants (devotees). 'Namah shrutyaya cha'—'shrutayah' are small paths; to him existing there. [Grammatical note]. 'Pathyaya cha'—'panthanah' are main roads (highways); to him existing there. (7)

Salutation to the one in the wells and to the one in the waterfalls. (8)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | kavyaya | cha | nipyaya | cha || 8 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Kaatah' is a place like a small canal where water flows slightly or in a difficult way (kutshitam). He who exists there in the form of water is 'kavyah'. 'Nipa' is a place where water falls downward from the top of a mountain; he who is worthy of that and resides there in the form of water is 'nipyah'. (8)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah kavyaya cha'. 'Kaatah' is a well. As is seen: "O Indra, having performed deeds... in the well." Or: 'kaatah' is a hole or ditch. As is seen in "not the horse... in the dusty ditch." To him existing there; meaning him who is always present in ditches. 'Nipyaya cha'—'nipa' is a place where water has flowed out or down. [Grammatical note]. To him who has become that. [Grammatical note on the accent]. (8)

Salutation to the one in the marshy land and to the one in the lakes. (9)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | sudyaya | cha | sarasyaya | cha || 9 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Sudah' is a muddy place; he who exists in the form of the water there is 'sudyah'. 'Saras' is the well-known lake; he who exists in the form of the water there is 'sarasyah'. (9)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah sudyaya cha'—where the absence of water is never seen is 'suda', i.e., mud; to him existing there. 'Sarasyaya cha'—'saras' is an artificial reservoir; to him existing as the water of that place. (9)

Salutation to the one in the rivers and to the one in the ponds. (10)
