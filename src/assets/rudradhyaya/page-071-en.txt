58  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah srotasyaya cha'—to him existing in the current. 'Dvipyaya cha'—an island (dvipa) is elevated land from the water, a region in which there is water (apas) on both (dvi) sides. [Grammatical note]. To him existing there. These are the distinctions of the presiding deity's visions. (15)

|| Thus in the Yajurveda commentary composed by Sri Bhatta Bhaskara, in the Sri Rudra-prashna, ends the Fifth Anuvaka. ||
|| This Fifth Anuvaka is completed. ||

Sayana's Commentary: In the fifth anuvaka, some Yajus with salutation at one end were spoken. Now, in the sixth, fifteen other such Yajus are being stated.

Salutation to the eldest and to the youngest. (1)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | jyeshthaya | cha | kanishthaya | cha || 1 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Jyeshthah'—greater in power, lordship, etc. 'Kanishthah'—smaller/lesser, being without those. (1)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: For the anuvakas 'Namo jyeshthaya cha' and 'Namo hantre cha' (6th and 7th), the Rishi and Deity are as before. 'Namo jyeshthaya'—to the eldest or to the most praiseworthy. [Grammatical note]. 'Kanishthaya cha'—to the youngest, to a child, or to the smallest. [Grammatical note]. (1)

Salutation to the first-born and to the last-born. (2)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | purva-jaya | cha | apara-jaya | cha || 2 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Purvajah'—one who was born at the beginning (purva) of the universe in the form of Hiranyagarbha. 'Aparajah'—one born at the end (apara) of the universe in the form of Kalagni (the Fire of Time/Destruction). (2)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah purvajaya cha'—to him born in former times. It means the Unborn (ajata). That existence which precedes all beings is indicated here by 'birth', for there is no primary birth (of God). For it is heard: "Being unborn, some fearful one takes refuge in you." 'Aparajaya cha'—to him born in later times, or existing even after all beings. (2)

Salutation to the middlemost and to the immature. (3)
