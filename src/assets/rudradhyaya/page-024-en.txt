[Na. Pra. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 11

...a person (purusha) of righteous conduct. Indeed, he alone deserves the designation of 'Purusha'. Furthermore, 'jagat'—the moving world like cattle, buffaloes, etc., which are other than humans—do not harm those either, by implication. Do not harm even the stationary things like trees and creepers. Thus, by excluding humans etc. and making the arrow target only those to be killed [the wicked], that constitutes the arrow's auspiciousness (shivatva). O Giritra—he who protects (trayate) the mountain (giri) Kailasa, holding it in high regard as his residence. Or, the protector of the cloud (giri) because he causes it to rain. Or, the protector of the chariot which is like a mountain, by being the protector of the gods who are its parts. Or, the protector (palaka) of words (giraam), i.e., words of prayer, by granting boons as desired.

|| He speaks the fifth verse ||

With auspicious speech, O dweller on the mountain, we speak toward you;
So that our entire moving world may be free from disease and of good mind. (5)

[Word-for-word]: Shivena | vachasa | tva | girisha | achha | vadamasi || yatha | nah | sarvam | it | jagat | ayakshmam | sumanah | iti | su-manah | asat || 5 ||

Sayana's Commentary: O Girisha, you who reside (shete) on the mountain (giri) Kailasa—we pray to you (tvaam) with auspicious (shiva), welfare-causing words in the form of praise. So that (yatha) our entire moving world (nah sarvam-it jagat)—the word 'it' is for emphasis—all beings including humans and animals, may be free from disease (ayakshmam) and endowed with a good mind (sumana). Do thus. (5)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: Now the second (of this pair). 'Shivenavachasa'. O Girisha—he who resides (shete) on the mountain (giri) Kailasa. Or, he who has the mountain as his excellent residence. Or, he who is the Lord (Isha) of the mountain (Kailasa), or the cloud (giri), or of speech (giraam) in the form of the Veda and Pranava. With auspicious speech (shivena vachasa)—with words of the nature of a hymn of praise, which consists of the recitation of your qualities, or which is welfare-causing, or which is pleasing to you—we speak (vadamasi) toward you (tvaam) who are endowed with all lordly qualities. We praise you—this is the meaning. 'Vadamasi' is the Vedic form for 'vadamah'. In the text, the word 'achha' has a long 'a' due to Vedic rules. The indeclinable 'achha' means 'toward'. Some say it is in the sense of 'to attain'.
