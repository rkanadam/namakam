60  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Yamyah'—one born in the realm of Yama in the form of the teacher/punisher of the sinful. 'Kshemyah'—one who is worthy of 'kshema' (liberation/well-being). (6)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah yamyaya cha'. 'Yama' is cessation, the end-time, or the restraint of life-force (prana). To him existing there. Or: the city of Yama is 'yamyam'. [Grammatical note]. To him existing there. 'Kshemyaya cha'—'kshema' is protection, or a hidden place. To him existing there. [Grammatical note]. (6)

Salutation to the one in the fertile land and to the one on the threshing floor. (7)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | urvaryaya | cha | khalyaya | cha || 7 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Urvara' is land possessing all crops; he who is worthy of that in the form of specific grains is 'urvaryah'. 'Khala' is the place for threshing/cleaning grain; he who is worthy of that in the form of the threshing-floor etc. is 'khalyah'. (7)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah urvaryaya cha'—to him existing in the fertile land (urvara). 'Khalyaya cha'—to him existing on the threshing floor (khala), the place for cleaning crops. (7)

Salutation to the one in the hymns and to the one in the conclusion. (8)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | shlokyaya | cha | ava-sanyaya | cha || 8 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shlokas' are Vedic mantras; one who exists in them as their subject matter is 'shlokyah'. 'Avasana' is the conclusion, i.e., the Vedanta (Upanishads); one who exists in them as their subject matter is 'avasanyah'. (8)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shlokyaya cha'. 'Shloka' is an assembly of letters bound in verse (pada-baddha). To him existing there. 'Avasanyaya cha'—'avasana' is the withdrawal/conclusion of all meanings, or the end of life. To him existing there. Another says: 'shlokyaya' means existing in the Vedas; 'avasanyaya' means existing in the Upanishads, which are the end (conclusion) of the Vedas. (8)

Salutation to the one in the forest and to the one in the thickets. (9)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | vanyaya | cha | kakshyaya | cha || 9 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Vanyah'—one born in the forest in the form of trees etc. 'Kakshyah'—one born in a thicket (kaksha) in the form of creepers etc. (9)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo vanyaya cha'—existing in the forest in the form of trees etc. 'Kakshyaya cha'—'kakshas' have been explained as forest-thickets; existing there. Creepers etc. are 'kakshya'. These are the distinctions of the presiding deity of the Earth. (9)
