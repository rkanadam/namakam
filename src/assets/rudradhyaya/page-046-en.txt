[Na. Dvi. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 33

...the auspicious Upanishads that should be spoken. To him who possesses those by being their teacher. Or: to the one who consists of mantras; the idea is that seven crores of mantras belong to him. 'Vanijaya'—the merchant (vanik) himself is 'vanijah'. [Grammatical note]. It means one who wears the guise of a merchant, as such a thing is possible at any time according to his will. Or: the designation is due to similarity. Just as a merchant assesses the business affairs of all people, so he is the witness of the actions (karma) of all beings. Or: 'vani' is speech; he who is born in speech as its expressed meaning is 'vanijah'. [Grammatical note]. Salutation to the Lord of thickets (kaksha)—to the Master of thickets consisting of mountain-rivers, caves, bushes, etc., or to the protector of beings residing there. Or: 'kaksha' are deep/impenetrable (gahana) regions—deep regions of language, deep regions of Dharma and Adharma; to him who is established in them. (10)

He speaks the eleventh Yajus:
Salutation to the expander of the earth, to the creator of wealth, salutation to the lord of plants. (11)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | bhuvantaye | varivah-kritaya | iti | varivah-kritaya | oushadhinam | pataye | namah || 11 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Bhuvanti' is he who extends (tanoti) the earth (bhuvam). Salutation be to that Rudra. 'Varivah' is wealth; its maker is 'varivaskrita'. Salutation be to that Rudra. Salutation to Rudra who is the protector of medicinal plants (oushadhi), both of the village and the forest. (11)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo bhuvantaye'. He in whom the universe exists (bhavati) as the material cause, predominating in being, is 'bhuvantah'. Or: he who exists (bhavanti) always and everywhere. [Grammatical note]. Or: 'bhuvanti' is he who extends (tanoti) the earth by being the support for grass, bushes, herbs, and trees. 'Varivaskritaya'—'varivah' is wealth; its maker is 'varivaskara'. [Grammatical note]. Or: he who is connected as a helper to those who earn wealth. Another says: 'varivah' is service/worship; its performers are 'varivaskritah'—the devotees; he is 'varivaskrita' for them. It means he is 'bhakta-vatsala' (fond of his devotees). Or: to him who has made his residence (vasa) in water (vari)—to him in the form of Vishnu or Varuna. [Grammatical note]. Salutation to the Lord of medicinal plants (oushadhi)—to the Master of both village and forest varieties; in the form of Soma (the moon), being the protector of those produced in the form of beings. 'Oshadhayah' are those which grow/stay until they ripen (osha-antam dhiyante). (11)
