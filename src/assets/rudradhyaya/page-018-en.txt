[Na. Pra. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 5

...this is the meaning of the collection of sentences. Some say: having pleased the God Rudra with the first anuvaka, then, through the next eight anuvakas, not knowing what seat the God might be presiding over, one salutes everything moving and unmoving with the suspicion that it might be his seat, and then prays for fearlessness in the final ones. Indeed, the God is the ground of absolute freedom; presiding over various things, he performs punishment and grace for the world. For instance—appearing in the form of a Yaksha, he deluded the gods. Taking the guise of a cheat, he deluded the seven sages. Adorned in the guise of a hunter (Kirata), he played with the son of Pandu (Arjuna). With the body of Bhairava, along with the Goddess who took a certain guise, he generated Gajanana (Ganesha). In the form of Indra, he desired to lead some devotee to the attainment of penance. In the form of the Arka tree, he resided in the lap of the Goddess. With the body of an owl, he taught knowledge to someone. Adorned in the guise of a madman (unmatta), he showed the organ of procreation to the best of gods as the decorated cause of the universe. To prevent some devotee who wished to kill his sinful self, he appeared from the laundry-stone of a washerman. He partook of the oblation placed in a hole in the ground by someone. Thus, this universe is the play of the God of gods who accepts various guises according to his will. Therefore, one salutes him by praising his universal form. As it is said:
'Your deeds are diverse, secret, and deep; O Shankara, you are hard to be known by all including Brahma.'

|| Here, in the first anuvaka, he speaks the first verse ||
Harih Om.

Salutations to your anger, O Rudra; and salutations to your arrow.
Salutations be to your bow, and salutations to your two arms. (1)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | te | rudra | manyave | uto | iti | te | ishave | namah | namah | te | astu | dhanvane | bahubhyam | iti | bahu-bhyam | uta | te | namah || 1 ||

Sayana's Commentary: O Rudra, let there be salutation to your anger (manyu), your wrath. May that anger spread only among our enemies, not against us. And (uto) also salutations to your arrow (ishu). Similarly, salutations to your bow (dhanvan). And also salutations to your two arms (bahu) which are equipped with the bow and arrow. The intention is: let all these operate only against enemies, not against me. (1)

Bhatta Bhaskara's Commentary: Om. For 'Namaste rudra manyava', the Rishi is Kashyapa. The Deity is Rudra. The Metre is Anushtup. These first five mantras are directly addressed (pratyakshakrita). Om. Svaha, Svadha, Vashat, Namah—these are the five names of Brahman. To achieve selfhood (atmatva) and Brahman-hood through worship with speech, mind, and body...
