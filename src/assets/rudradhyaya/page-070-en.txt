57  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Salutation to the one in the swift-flowing (water) and to the one in the rapids. (13)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | shighriyaya | cha | shighryaya | cha || 13 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shighriyah'—one who moves quickly. 'Shighryah'—one who resides in 'shighra', which refers to the state of flowing out/onwards. (13)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shighriyaya cha'—to him who exists in waters that flow quickly. [Grammatical note]. 'Shighryaya cha'—to him who exists in waters that flow quickly. 'Shighra' is a sub-division of the quick. [Grammatical note]. (13)

Salutation to the one in the waves and to the one in the still water. (14)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | urmyaya | cha | ava-svanyaya | cha || 14 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Urmyah'—one associated with waves (urmi). 'Avasvanyah'—one residing in still water (avasvana) where noise is absent. (14)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah urmyaya cha'—to him who exists in waves. [Grammatical note]. 'Avasvanyaya cha'—'avasvana' is silent/still water; either where sound is diminished or from where sound has departed. 'Ava' is used in the sense of 'absent', referring to still water. To the one existing there. (14)

Salutation to the one in the currents and to the one in the islands. (15)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | srotasyaya | cha | dvipyaya | cha || 15 ||

|| Thus ends the Fifth Anuvaka. ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Srotasyah'—residing in the current (srotas/flow). 'Dvipyah'—residing on an island (dvipa) in the middle of water. Here, in one single Yajus, two 'cha' particles are recited for the sake of conjunction, to connect the word 'Namah' (salutation) separately to the two words ending in the fourth case. (15)

|| Thus in the Vedartha-prakasha, the commentary on the Yajur-samhita composed by Sayanacharya-Madhava, in the fourth Kanda, fifth Prapathaka, the commentary on the Sri Rudram, ends the Fifth Anuvaka. ||
