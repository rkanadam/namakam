16  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pra. Am.]

...or he who bears (bibharti) the universe through the granting of knowledge etc., is 'babhruh'. 'Sumangalah'—endowed with all auspiciousness because he is the cause of it. In the Bahuvrihi compound, the accent is on the final syllable of the last member. Such is this Aditya (Sun). The meaning is: Rudra who is of the form of such a Sun. And those Rudras of similar nature to Rudra, with similar appearance and glory, created by the God and always his attendants—who are spread in the directions around this earth. They exist in the thousands (sahasrashah), becoming groups of thousands. (Their purpose is) to grant the fruits according to the merits and sins of the worlds. Some say: the Rudras in the form of rays of this Sun-formed Rudra, who exist all around the earth—of this Rudra and these attendants of Rudra, we remove (av-imahe) their 'heda'—anger or disrespect. (We do this) with hymns and salutations—this is the implied remainder. Yaska says 'hedah' means anger. Both are taken here: we remove the disrespect toward us arising from the violation of injunctions and the anger arising from the performance of prohibited acts. The root 'ii' (to go) alone is a verb for 'requesting'. As seen in "We always pray (imahe) for a portion," etc. The 'shap' suffix is dropped in the Veda. Combined with 'ava' (av-imahe), it means to remove. Or, we pray in such a way that it (the anger) becomes diminished (avachinam). (7)

|| He speaks the eighth verse ||

That one who moves along, blue-throated and specially red;
And him the cowherds saw, and the water-carrying women saw;
And him all beings saw—he, being seen, makes us happy. (8)
