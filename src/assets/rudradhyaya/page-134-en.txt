122 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. E. A.]

(Sayanacharya continued): By sacrificing with the Arka leaf, since both the leaf and the milk (from the goat/plant) have a common origin, the quality of 'same source' is achieved. || 4 ||

He prescribes a specific detail for the time of Homa:

He sacrifices standing facing the North (udan). This is indeed Rudra's direction. In His own direction, he appeases Rudra. He sacrifices on the last brick (charamaya ishtakayam). He appeases Rudra at the very end. || 5 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The last brick is the one at the edge/end. It is shown by the Sutra-maker: "On the Vikarni brick at the northeast corner of the north wing...". By the Homa at the end/edge, he expels/appeases this Rudra at the very end. || 5 ||

He prescribes the division of the mantras used in the Homa:

He sacrifices dividing it into three parts. Three are these worlds. These very worlds he makes well-ordered. || 6 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The three-fold division should be done thus: (1) From "Namaste rudra" to "Sabha-patibhyashcha vo namah" is the first part. (2) From "Namo ashvebhyah" to "Avayancha va" is the second part. (3) From "Namah prataranaya cha" to the end including the Rik "Ya etavantashcha" is the third part. || 6 ||
