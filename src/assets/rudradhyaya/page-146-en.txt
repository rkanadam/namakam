[Cha. Pra. A.] Rudradhyaya | 911

|| BEGINNING OF THE CHAMAKAM BHASHYA ||
|| Salutations to Shri Ganesha ||

I salute Vidya-tirtha-Maheshwara, whose breath are the Vedas, who created the entire world out of the Vedas.
The main purifications and rituals like 'Parishechana' were stated in the sixth (Prapathaka). The remaining rituals like 'Vasordhara' etc. are being stated in the seventh.

(The Ritual Rule): Having offered a fourfold offering (chatur-grihita) with "Agnavishnu sajoshasa", fill a ladle made of Udumbara wood, which is black-colored and smeared with mud, and has a spout at the back, with ghee. Offer the continuous 'Vasordhara' (stream of wealth) with "Vajashcha me prasavashcha me" until the fire is completed. Here the seventh Prapathaka is completed with eleven Anuvakas.

[The mantra for the fourfold offering]:

Om, O Agni and Vishnu, may you both be of one mind. Let these my words/praises increase you. Come with wealth and swift horses. || 1 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: O Agni and Vishnu, be of equal affection (sajoshasha). Let these my praises (girah) in the form of hymns increase you. You both, come here (agatam) accompanied by wealth (dyumnaih) and horses (vajibhir). || 1 ||

Now the mantra consisting of eleven Anuvakas is stated. The division into Anuvakas is for the purpose of performing separate rituals associated with each.

[The first Anuvaka]:

May food, permission (to eat), purity, desire (for wealth), meditation, sacrifice, sound, praise, fame, hearing, light, heaven, prana, apana, vyana, life, mind, thought, speech, spirit, eyes, ears, skill, strength, power... || 1 ||
