72  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Aa. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Harikesha'—those trees like the wish-fulfilling tree (Kalpataru) etc., whose hair-like leaves are green (harita). The meaning is: Rudra is in that form. (7)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah vrikshebhyo harikeshebhyah'. These two were explained in the anuvaka with salutations at both ends. The God indeed graces the world by appearing in whatever form he desires, even in the form of trees etc. (7)

Salutation to the savior. (8)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | taraya || 8 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Tarah'—the subject matter expressed by the Pranava (Om). (8)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah taraya'. He who carries (tarayati) beings across the ocean of worldly existence is 'tarah'. [Grammatical note]. As the Jabala Upanishad says: "Here indeed, when the life-breath of a being is departing, Rudra explains the saving (taraka) Brahman." Salutation to him. Or: to him in the form of the Pranava. (8)

Salutation to the source of welfare and to the source of delight. (9)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | sham-bhave | cha | mayoh-bhave | iti | mayah-bhave | cha || 9 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shambhuh'—he who manifests or produces (bhavayati) happiness (sham). 'Mayobhuh'—he who manifests happiness (mayas). (9)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shambhave cha'. He from whom happiness (sham) arises (bhavati) is 'Shambhuh', the cause of happiness. Or: he who exists (bhavati) always as happiness alone. Or: he who manifests (bhavayati) happiness of a unique form. The observer of the various types of happiness. [Grammatical note]. 'Mayobhave cha'—'mayas' is also happiness. The distinction is between worldly and other-worldly happiness, or between prosperity (abhyudaya) and the supreme good (nihshreyasa). Or: happiness in the form of the pacification of sorrow is called 'sham'; other (positive) happiness is 'mayas'. (9)

Salutation to the maker of welfare and to the maker of delight. (10)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | sham-karaya | cha | mayah-karaya | iti | mayah-karaya | cha || 10 ||

Sayana's Commentary: One is the happiness of the objects of the senses, the other is the happiness of liberation; this is the distinction between the two.
