124 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Pa. A.]

Sayanacharya Bhashya: If he were to sacrifice walking around (anuparikramam)—meaning, circumambulating in order—then he would make that Rudra roam inside (antaravacharinam) the area of the Fire-altar. And since He is fierce after entering, He causes trouble; therefore, one should not circumambulate—this is the 'purva-paksha' (initial view). The word 'atho' is to start the 'siddhanta' (conclusion). Experts say: (the word 'khali-ahuh' is to refute the previous view). Who is capable of knowing where this Rudra, who roams everywhere, is at any given time and in which direction He might or might not be? Therefore, one should sacrifice exactly while circumambulating in order. In that case, having reached Rudra wherever He may be situated around, he pacifies Him. It is because He is pacified that this Rudra, having entered inside, does not cause trouble while roaming. || 9 ||

He prescribes the recitation by the sacrificer of the mantras "Namo rudrebhyah" etc.:

These deities are indeed heavenly (suvarya). Those which are the highest—he makes the sacrificer recite them. By those very deities, he makes him go to the heavenly world. || 10 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The deities to be described by these Yajus are the causes of Heaven, therefore they are 'highest' (uttama), or 'highest' because they are described by the mantras of the highest life-stage (Ashrama). The Adhvaryu should make the sacrificer recite them. Meaning, he should make him read the Yajus that describe them. Thus, by those very deities, he makes the sacrificer reach Heaven. || 10 ||

He prescribes the place for disposing of the Arka-parna (milkweed leaf):

Him whom he (the sacrificer) hates—he should throw the Arka-leaf in the path where his (the enemy's) cattle roam. The first animal that steps on it will suffer harm. || 11 ||
