54  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Sharvah'—because he destroys (shrinati) or kills sins. 'Pashupatih'—because he protects (palayati) human beings who are ignorant like cattle (pashu). (2)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah sharvaya cha'. He who breaks/destroys (shrinati) everything at the end of time is 'Sharvah'. [Etymology]. He is the presiding deity of Fire (Agni). 'Pashupataye cha'—to the one who protects beings. This is recited again (from the earlier anuvaka). He is the presiding deity of Earth. [Grammatical note]. (2)

Salutation to the blue-throated one and to the white-necked one. (3)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | nila-grivaya | cha | shiti-kanthaya | cha || 3 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Nilagrivah' is he who has a part of his neck blue in color due to holding the Kalakuta poison. 'Shitikanthah' is he who has the remaining neck region white (shiti). (3)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo nilagrivaya cha'—explained already. 'Shitikanthaya cha'—because 'shiti' means both white and dark according to the Nighantu. Indeed, only a small part of the God's neck became blue due to the fire of the Kalakuta poison. Or: the repetition is for the sake of respect, as in "Salutation to the Lord of Ambika, the Lord of Uma, the Lord of Beings." [Grammatical note on the word 'shitikanthaya']. (3)

Salutation to the one with matted hair and to the one with shaven hair. (4)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | kapardine | cha | vyupta-keshaya | cha || 4 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Kapardi' is he who has matted hair (kaparda). 'Vyuptakeshah' is he whose hair is shaven (munidita). The matted hair refers to the guise of a Pashupata ascetic. Shaven hair refers to the guise of a Sannyasi (yati). (4)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah kapardine cha'—clear. 'Vyuptakeshaya cha'—to the shaven-headed one, in his form as the Sacrificer (Yajamana). Others say 'vyuptakesha' means half-shaven hair. [Grammatical note]. (4)

Salutation to the thousand-eyed one and to the one with a hundred bows. (5)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | sahasra-akshaya | cha | shata-dhanvane | cha || 5 ||
