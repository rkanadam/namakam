[Na. Pra. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 21

|| He speaks the thirteenth verse ||

That weapon of yours, O most bountiful one, and that bow which was in your hand;
With that, which is non-injurious, protect us from all sides. (13)

[Word-for-word]: Ya | te | hetih | midhuh-tama | haste | babhuva | te | dhanuh || taya | asman | vishvatah | tvam | ayakshmaya | pari | bhuj || 13 ||

Sayana's Commentary: O most bountiful one (midhustama), greatest shedder of desires—that weapon (heti) of yours in the form of a sword etc., and also that bow which was in your hand; with that weapon and with that kind of bow, which is 'ayakshmaya'—not causing injury or disturbance—you protect (paribhuj) us from all sides (vishvatah), guard us entirely. (13)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: Now 'Ya te hetih' and the following three are in Anushtup. The Rishi and Deity are the Lord Himself. O Midhustama—'midhvan' has been explained; the greatest shedder. That weapon (heti) belonging to you, named the bow, which exists (babhuva) in your hand. 'Heti' is derived from the root 'han' (to kill/strike). With that weapon named the bow, which is 'ayakshmaya'—not a cause of disease or injury to devotees. From all causes of fear (vishvatah), you protect (paribhuj) us entirely. Guard us at all times, in all ways, and from all sins. The root 'bhuj' has a Vedic variation. Or: protect all of us (vishvatah), including sons and grandsons. Some say: 'heti' means violence; that violence performed by you, for the sake of which the bow, the means of violence, stays in your hand—protect us from that, etc., is the same. (13)

|| He speaks the fourteenth verse ||

Salutation be to your weapon which is unstrung and powerful;
And also salutation to your two arms and to your bow. (14)
