[Na. Dvi. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 29

...salutation be to such a Rudra-form. Salutation to Rudra, the lord/protector of the paths—the northern, southern, and second paths mentioned in the scriptures. (3)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo saspinjaraya tvishimate'. 'Saspi' is young grass; to him whose color is red like that. Or: 'saspin' refers to those like cows who desire young grass; he who makes them age/perish (jarayita) is 'saspinjara'. To him who destroys the demons who kill young grass. 'Pinjara' is red-yellow; to the one who is golden-red like young grass. [Grammatical note on the form]. 'Tvishimate'—'tvishi' is radiance; to him who possesses it to a great degree, who has an extremely radiant body, or who is of the nature of self-luminous knowledge. [Grammatical note on the suffix]. 'Pathinam pataye namah'—to the promoter of good paths, both worldly and Vedic. Or: to the Lord of the paths like the path of light (archiradi-marga), the great leader (ativahika) on them. Or: 'pathayah' means seers/observers like Brahma etc.; to their Master. [Grammatical note on the word 'pathinam']. Some say: 'psa' means to eat. Those who are eaters, like Rakshasas etc., who hate the good path. As is seen in: "While each section is being made, the Rakshasas desire to kill." He who makes them age/perish is 'saspinjara'. Salutation to the radiant one, and salutation to the one who is the promoter of those paths. (3)

He speaks the fourth Yajus:
Salutation to the one who sits on a bull, to the one who pierces in various ways, salutation to the lord of foods. (4)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | babhlushaya | vivyadhine | iti | vi-vyadhine | annanam | pataye | namah || 4 ||

Sayana's Commentary: A bull is called 'babhluh' because he bears (bibharti) Rudra; here 'l' and 'r' are interchangeable. He who sits (shete) on that (bull) is 'babhlushah'. And he who pierces (vidhyati) his enemies in various ways is 'vivyadhi'. Salutation be to such a 'vivyadhi'. Salutation to Rudra, the protector of foods (annanam). (4)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo babhlushaya'. 'Babhluh'—one who has a color like a brown monkey (kapishala-pinjala). Or: 'babhluh' is the bull who bears (vabharti). One who has that. Or: one who sits on a bull is 'babhlushah'. Salutation to him. 'Vivyadhine'—to him who pierces or afflicts in various ways, such as in the form of hunger etc.
