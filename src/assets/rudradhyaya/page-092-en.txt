[Na. Na. A.] Rudradhyaya | 79

Salutations to the one on the earth (Urdhyaya) and to the one in the waves (Surmyaya). || 11 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Urdhyah' is He who exists on the 'urdhya', i.e., the earth. A river having beautiful waves ('urmi') is 'surmi'; He who exists in that is 'surmyah'. || 11 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Urdhyaya'. To the one on the 'urdhya', the earth. (Grammatical note on long vowel). It refers to the entire ground. To 'Surmyaya'. 'Surmi' is a hollow metal image or a flowing stream. Salutations to the one who exists in that. Cites "Surmim jvalantim...". || 11 ||

Salutations to the one in the leaves (Parnyaya) and to the one in the heap of dry leaves (Parnashadyaya). || 12 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Parnyah' is He who exists in leaves (patra). A heap of dry leaves is 'parnashad'; He who exists in that is 'parnashadyah'. || 12 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Parnyaya'. To the one in the leaves. In these three (Urdhya etc.), the initial accent is absent as before. To 'Parnashadyaya'. A heap of dry leaves is 'parnashad'; to the one who is in that. This shows how accessible He is. These are the ways the Lord graces the world. || 12 ||

Salutations to the one who raises weapons (Apaguramanaya) and to the one who strikes (Abhighnate). || 13 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Apaguramanah' is one with weapons raised. 'Abhighnan' is one who strikes or kills. || 13 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Now He describes how the Lord acts towards the wicked souls who are to be restrained: Salutations to 'Apaguramanaya', the one who raises weapons to strike those to be punished. To 'Abhighnate', the one who strikes or kills the enemies who are in front. || 13 ||
