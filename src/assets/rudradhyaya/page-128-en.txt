[Na. E. A.] Rudradhyaya | 119

Those who roam in the holy places (tirthani) like Kashi, Prayaga, etc., to protect them. What kind of Rudras? 'Srikavanta': 'Srika' is a sharp-pointed weapon like a knife held in the hand. Others are 'Nishangina', holding swords. (The rest as before). || 9 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The ninth. Those Rudras who roam in the holy places to grant the fruit of pilgrimage to some and block others. Holding weapons (srikavanta) and swords (nishangina). (Grammatical notes). || 9 ||

[Purashcharana for these two (8 and 9)]: Having performed a Kricchra penance, chant the mantra 5,000 times to complete the Purashcharana. On the day of intended conception (ritu-gamana), sacrifice 8,000 offerings of cow-milk mixed with ghee in the fire; then perform the act. A learned and lineage-continuing son will be born. For going to a great pilgrimage, sacrifice 10,000 offerings of pure rice smeared with ghee. If one cannot reach the pilgrimage, having bathed and come back, worship the fire and keep silence; he obtains the fruit of the pilgrimage. Meditation etc. as before.

He states the tenth Rik:

Those Rudras who are so many, and even more, who have pervaded all directions... || 10 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Those Rudras who are 'etavantah'—as many as mentioned in the previous Riks—and 'bhuyansah'—even more than that—who have pervaded (vitasthire) all directions. (The rest as before). Thus the ten Riks are stated. || 10 ||
