[Na. Pra. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 15

...having thrown (ginger) into milk and boiled it, one should offer eight thousand or eight hundred oblations for oneself and drink the remainder. All diseases vanish.

Meditation (Dhyanam):
"One should meditate on the three-eyed Lord, who has the crescent moon on his crown, who is the enemy of Time (Kala), who has serpents as his sacred thread, and who is like blazing fire." (6)

|| He speaks the seventh verse ||

That one who is copper-red, crimson, and also tawny, and very auspicious;
And those Rudras who are spread in the directions around this (earth) in the thousands—of all these, we appease the anger. (7)

[Word-for-word]: Asau | yah | tamrah | arunah | uta | babhruh | su-mangalah || ye | cha | imam | rudrah | abhito | dikshu | shritah | sahasrashah | iti | sahasra-shah | avai | esham | hedah | imahe || 7 ||

Sayana's Commentary: That Rudra who is in the form of the Sun (Aditya) in the solar disc—he is 'tamrah', extremely red at the time of sunrise. 'Arunah'—slightly red after rising. 'Uta'—and also 'babhruh'—tawny/brown further after that. Other colors occurring at different times should also be understood. 'Sumangalah'—being of various colors, he is extremely auspicious as he removes darkness etc. at various times. And those others, the Rudras in the form of rays, who are spread around (abhito) this earth, situated in all the directions (dikshu) like the East etc., and who are in the thousands (sahasrashah)—many thousands in number—of all these Rudras in the form of the Sun and his rays, we remove (av-imahe) their 'heda'—the sharpness which is like anger—through devotion, salutation, etc. (7)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Asau yastamrah' and the following is in Aastara-pankti metre. "If the last two feet are of twelve syllables, it is Aastara-pankti," according to the rule. Some say it is a Pankti of five feet. 'Asau yo-vasarpati' is in Jagati metre of six feet. For these two, the Rishi is Kaala (Time). Some say Marutvan. The Deity is Rudra of the nature of Aditya (the Sun). "With 'Asau yastamrah', one waits upon the Sun," says the Shatapatha Brahmana. Since Aditya is included among the eight bodies of the God which perform the withdrawal of the universe. Through these two (mantras 7 and 8), he prays for his grace regarding devotees. 'Asau'—he points with his finger. That Sun in the upper world. What kind of a one? 'Tamrah'—extremely red at the time of rising. He shines thus because of the contact with heat. Some say: because of the contact with the dust of the earth. Then 'Arunah'—slightly red after rising. And also 'Babhruh'—tawny after that. Some say fair (gaura).
