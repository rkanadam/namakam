44  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Tri. Am.]

...with a mixture (of rice and ghee), one should offer eight thousand oblations. Their disease perishes. In case of a major disease (pradhana-vyadhi), one should offer a hundred thousand (lakh) oblations of pure rice soaked in ghee in a temple of Shambhu. In case of diseases of ministers also, one should offer eight thousand oblations each of Durva grass and Apamarga; their disease perishes. Now for the King's disease: it should be performed by twenty-one perfected Brahmins. One of them is the Brahma (supervising priest). Having made a fire-altar of the king's measure (height) in all directions, and having established the churned fire there, they should offer oblations with a firm mind. The offering materials are: rice, sesame, shyamaka, nivara, apamarga, durva, lotus filaments, pure cooked rice, and ghee—all should be mixed with cow's ghee. The Brahma priest should touch a water-filled pot to the North-West of the fire-altar and chant. The offering should be continuous for seven 'nadikas' (units of time). Thus the homa is performed from one new moon to the next new moon. If the King's disease becomes severe, the homa should be performed from sunrise. The Dakshina for the nadika-homa is half a Nishka per person per day. For daytime homa, double that. For the Acharya, double that. One should sprinkle the King's objects of enjoyment with the water from the pot. When this is done, the King's disease vanishes. Another method is told: for the destruction of the King's disease, eight thousand oblations of white rice mixed with ghee and sesame should be offered daily with this anuvaka. By this, the King becomes permanently free from disease and long-lived. If others are diseased, with this anuvaka, one should offer eight thousand oblations of sesame, rice, and barley soaked in ghee and sprinkled with milk for each salutation; their disease perishes.

Malevolent rites (Abhichara) are told with this anuvaka: A king desiring many diseases in another kingdom should have a homa performed with meat such as boar etc. mixed with oil. "I perform abhichara against the enemy nation, Phat Svaha!"—thus (one should say). By this meat-offering, the enemy nation and its subjects wither away. Now, offering a lakh (100,000) of Vaikankata wood-sticks wrapped in red thread; by this, a great fever arises in the enemy. Having touched or looked at objects like water intended for use by the people of the other kingdom, one should perform a lakh of chants. Whoever uses that object perishes within a year. Offering Arka wood-sticks in the cremation fire (for a long time); by that the enemy king's city perishes by disease and becomes a wilderness. With this very (mantra), in a Chamunda temple, facing South, offering eleven thousand black grams and sesame soaked in oil; the enemy nation is afflicted by smallpox (masurika). Another thing is told: in a Shiva temple with a West-facing door, touching the fire of the God's pedestal, one should chant seven thousand times. Having remembered the enemy kingdom, at the end of the mantra, one should say "Blaze, O flame of epilepsy!" By this, the king becomes epileptic. Thinking of the (enemy) king, with a lakh of oblations of pure rice and sesame paste in a fire of mustard seeds, the royal consumption (tuberculosis/rajayakshma) arises; he perishes by that.
