55  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Madhyamah'—one born in the middle time in the form of gods, animals, etc. 'Apagalbhah'—a child whose senses are not yet fully developed. (3)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo madhyamaya cha'—to him who exists in the middle time. 'Apagalbhaya cha'—to the extreme infant. [Grammatical note]. (3)

Salutation to the one born in the rear and to the one at the root. (4)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | jaghanyaya | cha | budhniyaya | cha || 4 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Jaghanyah'—born in the form of a calf etc. from the rear part (jaghana) of cattle. 'Budhniyah'—born in the form of branches etc. from the root (budhna) of trees etc. (4)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo jaghanyaya cha'—to him existing in the rear/hips (jaghana). [Grammatical note]. 'Budhniyaya cha'—to him existing at the root (budhna). [Grammatical note]. (4)

Salutation to the one in the human world and to the one in the protective thread. (5)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | sobhyaya | cha | pratisaryaya | cha || 5 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Sobhah' is the human world, which exists along with both (sa-ubha) merit and sin. Therefore the followers of the Atharvaveda state: "By merit one wins the world of merit, by sin the world of sin, and by both (the two together) the human world." He who exists there is 'sobhyah'. 'Pratisarah' is the protective thread (raksha-bandha) tied on the hand during weddings etc.; he who is worthy of that is 'pratisaryah'. (5)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah sobhyaya cha'. 'Sobha' is the name of the city of the Gandharvas. As seen in the Mahabharata: "Shalva, presiding over Sobha." This is an indicator (upalakshana) for all cities. To him existing there. [Grammatical note]. Some say: 'Sobha' is that which is with both (sa-ubha) heat and cold, in the form of Indra. To him existing there. 'Pratisaryaya cha'—'pratisara' means contrary movement, or the protective thread. To him existing there. [Grammatical note]. (5)

Salutation to the one in Yama's realm and to the one in the world of security. (6)
