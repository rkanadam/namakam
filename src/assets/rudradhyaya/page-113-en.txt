100 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Da. A.]

[Purashcharana for this Mantra]: Having fasted for three nights, one should chant the mantra continuously for three nights. This completes the Purashcharana. This is only for those desiring long life, not others. As the Shruti says: "Man is called a hundred-year-being." Thus, for those whose life is being taken away by deeds causing decay in this or other births, this ritual is for increasing their life. Daily sacrifice 8,000 or 108 offerings of ghee in the fire. Sacrifice 8,000 Durva grasses smeared with curd, honey, and ghee. Daily chant 8,000 times on one's birth star. Specifically, on the birthday, sacrifice 10,000 each of Durva, Apamarga, Shami, and sesame seeds smeared with curd, honey, and ghee. At night, chant 5,000 times and perform circumambulations. A king should sacrifice 10,000 each of Durva, Apamarga, etc., on his birthday; he will be long-lived. For those unable, sacrifice in a flowing stream; for those extremely weak, only chanting. Sacrifice 10,000 offerings of pure rice smeared with honey and ghee; his life increases. Worship the Lord with 10,000 Arka flowers; his prosperity increases. Staying in a temple with a west-facing door, eating only Charu, sleeping on the ground, being celibate, and eating only unsalted food—chant 1,000 times daily near the Lord; he will live for a hundred years in seven births. For the weak, living on alms is recommended. Meditation:

"Meditate on the Lord dancing the Tandava of bliss in front of the Goddess (Devi), focused on the sacrificial fire, brilliant like molten gold."

He states the eighth Rik:

Praise the famous, the one who resides in the heart, the youthful, fierce like a lion, the destroyer, the fierce one. O Rudra, being praised, make us happy in this aging body. Let your armies strike someone other than us. || 8 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: O my speech, praise the famous, well-known Rudra. What kind? 'Gartasadam', always residing in the lotus of the heart. As the Smriti says: "The Lord dwells in the heart of all beings." 'Yuvanam', ever-youthful. 'Upahatnum ugram', having a fierce form to destroy the entire world at the time of dissolution. An example: 'Bhimam mrigam na', like a terrifying lion. Just as a lion is fierce to tear apart an elephant, so is He. O Rudra, being praised by our words, make this aging body—which decays day by day—happy ('mrida'). Let your armies ('senah') strike ('nivapantu') our enemies, not us. || 8 ||
