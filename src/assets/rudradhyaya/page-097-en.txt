84 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Na. A.]

(Grammatical note on accents). || 18 ||

Salutations to those who expand (Amivatkebhyah). || 19 ||

|| Thus ends the Ninth Anuvaka ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Amivatkah' are those who grow large or pervasive in all directions. Here, to 'Vikshinaka', 'Vichinvatka', 'Anihrita', and 'Amivatka', the phrase "Devaram Hridayebhyah" is added. || 19 ||

From the fifth Anuvaka to the end of the ninth Anuvaka, the Yajus with salutations on one side are mentioned. Through all of them, the all-pervasiveness (sarvatmya) of Parameshwara is established by describing various forms—both stationary and moving—in each Yajus. The division into Anuvakas is according to the different mantras used outside the sacrifice. Each Anuvaka is one mantra. The method for its Purashcharana etc. will be explained in the Rudra-kalpa.
Thus ends the Ninth Anuvaka in the Shri Rudra Bhashya of the Yajur-Samhita, Fourth Kanda, Fifth Prapathaka, in the Madhaviya Vedartha Prakasha composed by Sayanacharya. || 9 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Amivatkebhyah'. To those who expand or yawn in all directions. 'Miv' means to grow large. Or others say, those who strike in all directions. The sense is that there is no space for the wicked to escape. 'Miv' can also mean violence. (Grammatical explanation). Salutations to you who are of this nature. || 19 ||

[Purashcharana for this Mantra]: Having performed the Paraka penance, one should chant the mantra 40,000 times to complete the Purashcharana. To purify the mantra, one should chant it 8,000 times before the Guru; then one becomes fit for the ritual. Nothing is impossible through this mantra; everything is accomplished by its power. The rituals to be performed are: In front of the Dakshinamurti form, sacrifice 100,000 goat-sesame seeds (chaga-tila) in fire; gold will be produced. Sacrifice 10,000 Apamarga seeds, barley, etc., smeared with curd, honey, and ghee; 1,000 gold pieces will be produced. One desiring a bride should make a woman's shape with Guggulu, chant 5,000 times, then cut it and sacrifice 6,000 times; he will obtain a desired bride adorned with all ornaments. Sacrifice 100,000 blue and white lotuses; royal glory (Rajalakshmi) will appear before him. Sacrifice 100,000 Palasha sprouts with milk; Yakshas, Rakshasas, ghosts, spirits, etc., will be visible and under control.
