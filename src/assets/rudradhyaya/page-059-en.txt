46  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Cha. Am.]

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: For this anuvaka starting with 'Namah avyadhinibhyah', the Rishi is Durvasa. The Metre is Ushnik. Shambhu is the Deity. From here on, his lordship over qualities and substances should be seen. In this anuvaka, all mantras are directly addressed (pratyakshakrita), and salutations are offered to groups (samghata). 'Namah avyadhinibhyah'—'avyadhinyah' are the armies of Rudra and specific classes who strike (vidhyanti) from all sides (a-samantat). The word formation and accent are as before. 'Vividhyantibhyashcha vo namah'—to those who strike in various, manifold ways. (1)

He speaks the second Yajus:
Salutation to those in excellent groups and to the fierce ones, salutation to you. (2)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | uganabhyah | trimhatibhyah | cha | vah | namah || 2 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Uganah' are women (female forms) in excellent groups, such as the Seven Mothers (Sapta-Matrikas) and others. 'Trinhatyah' are fierce deities like Durga and others who are capable of killing (injury). Salutation to them. (2)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Uganabhyah'—to those in excellent groups (utkrishta-gana). [Grammatical note].
"By fives and by multiples, the names in order are: army-front (sena-mukha), bush (gulma), and group (gana)."—thus the definition of a group.
This has been said already. 'Trinhatibhyashcha vo namah'—to those who produce special types of violence/injury. From the root 'trihh' (to kill/strike) of the Rudh-class. [Grammatical note on the word 'trimhatibhyah']. (2)

He speaks the third Yajus:
Salutation to the greedy, and to the lords of the greedy, salutation to you. (3)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | gritsebhyah | gritsapatibhyah | iti | gritsapati-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 3 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Gritsah' are those whose nature is greed, i.e., those addicted to sensual objects (vishaya-lampata). 'Gritsapatayah' are their protectors. Salutation to them. (3)
