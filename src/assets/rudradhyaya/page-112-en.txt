[Na. Da. A.] Rudradhyaya | 99

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Let your fierce form which kills cattle ('goghna') and kills men ('purushaghna'), your sons/grandsons, etc.—O Rudra who destroys enemies—let that form be far away ('arat') from us. Let it stay distant. Whatever auspicious, happiness-giving ('sumnam') form is Yours, let that be with us ('asme'). As it is said: "One is terrible, another is auspicious." Between the two bodies mentioned, let the terrible one go far away. Let the auspicious body come here. Furthermore, protect us ('raksha') from all sides. O God (Adhishtatri), speak of us ('adhi bruhi') as superior to other sacrificers among the gods. And also ('adha cha'), You who are the increaser of both worlds ('dvibarhah'), grant us peace ('sharma'). || 7 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The Rishi of "Arat te" is Richiparitri. Chhandas is Trishtubh. The deity is Shambhu. (Grammatical notes). Let Your fierce hunger for killing cattle and men, and the satisfaction You get from it, be far away ('arat') from us. Let the Lord play and sport elsewhere than with us. (Grammatical notes). Whatever happiness ('sumnam') is Yours, the destroyer of enemies, let that be with us. Meaning, let us be happy through non-violence alone. This shows that the Lord should not be blamed for His actions. "Protect us": (Grammatical notes). Or, let your fierce form which kills cattle etc. be far away. 'Kshayadvirah' is explained: one in whom heroes/progeny reside. One who is approachable and gracious. Let the satisfaction arising from Your protection and grace be with us. "Adhi cha deva bruhi": Speak for us with favoritism/support. Meaning, may the Lord Himself say "Do not fear, I will protect you" repeatedly by taking us under His guardianship. Not just common for everyone, but specifically for us. And also ('adha cha'), grant us peace/happiness. 'Dvibarhah' means pertaining to both worlds—this world and the next. (Grammatical derivation). You are the increaser of happiness both here and hereafter. Others say: "Increaser of only two worlds." Or 'Barha' means wing; one who supports both wings/worlds. Grant us peace here and hereafter. || 7 ||
