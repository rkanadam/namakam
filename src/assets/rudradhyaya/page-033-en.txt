...eight hundred measures with a staff, gold for earrings, and each day a quarter-Nishka should be given for making earrings. A bronze vessel for the single fire (Eka-agni) should also be given. Having done this, the one desiring a son becomes the father of a son of good nature. The lineage remains as long as the Dakshina land remains; success comes within a year. Meditation (Dhyanam):
"One should meditate on Shiva, with a body like the autumn moon and a cool radiance, seated on a throne along with Uma (Parvati)." (9)

|| He speaks the tenth verse ||

Loosen the string from both ends of your bow;
And those arrows which are in your hand, cast them away, O Blessed One. (10)

[Word-for-word]: Pra | muncha | dhanvanah | tvam | ubhayoh | artniyoh | jyam || yah | cha | te | haste | ishavah | para | iti | tah | bhagavah | iti | bhaga-vah | vapa || 10 ||

Sayana's Commentary: O Bhagavan (Blessed One), O Lord endowed with worshipful great lordship, O Rudra—you loosen (pramuncha), remove the string (jya) situated on both ends (artniyoh/tips) of your bow. And those arrows which are in your hand, cast them away (paravapa), abandon them. (10)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Pramuncha dhanvanah' and the following three are in Anushtup metre. The Rishi is Narada. Sri Rudra is the Deity. Here, after pleasing the angry God with the nine mantras starting from 'Namaste Rudra', he now begins to request the withdrawal of the means of destruction. There, two mantras are directly addressed. The third is in the third person. 'Pramuncha'—loosen. Loosen the string which is bound on both ends (tips) of the bow. The meaning is: make the bow one with the string unstrung. Similarly, those arrows which are in your hand to destroy the world, cast them away. Make them such that they are not directed towards us. Keep them hidden in the quiver alone. Or, hide them somewhere. Of what use are these bows etc. which cause us fear? O Bhagavo—O Bhagavan (Blessed One). The word has a Vedic change of 'v' to 'r' in the vocative.
"Total Lordship, Dharma, Fame, Wealth, Knowledge, and Detachment—these six are called 'Bhaga'." (10)
