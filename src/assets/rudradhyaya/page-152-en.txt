Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Urk' is food in general. 'Sunrita' is pleasant speech. From 'milk' onwards are specific types of food. 'Payah' is milk. 'Rasah' is essence/juice. Ghee and honey are well-known. 'Sangdhih' (melded with 'Sarpish'?) means eating with relatives. Similarly, 'Sarpitih' (or 'Sapihtih') means drinking together. Agriculture and rain are well-known causes of food. 'Jaitram' is victorious nature, meaning safety/security. 'Auddhidyam' is the emergence of plants, trees, and shrubs. 'Rayih' is gold. 'Rayah' are gems and pearls. 'Pushtam' is the abundance of the previously mentioned gold. 'Pushtih' is the nourishment of the body. From 'Vibhu' to 'Akshiti', these seven are terms for the progressive increase of grains. Not only great grains, but even small ones are mentioned: 'Kuyavah' are inferior/wild barley. 'Annam' is well-known. 'Akshud' is the removal of hunger through food. Rice, barley, beans, sesame, mung beans are well-known. 'Khalvah' are seeds larger than mung beans; in Atharvaveda Bhashya they are explained as chickpeas. Wheat is well-known. 'Masurah' are lentils for soup. 'Priyangu' is well-known. 'Anavah' is fine rice. 'Shyamakah' is a special village grain. 'Nivarah' is wild rice.

Thus ends the Fourth Anuvaka in the Chamaka Bhashya of the Yajur-Samhita, Fourth Kanda, Fifth Prapathaka, in the Madhaviya Vedartha Prakasha composed by Sayanacharya. || 4 ||

He states the fifth Anuvaka:

May stone, clay, hills, mountains, sand, trees, gold, bronze, lead, tin, dark iron, copper, fire, water, creepers, herbs, the cultivated, the uncultivated, domestic animals, wild animals—may all these be fit for sacrifice; may wealth, obtaining wealth, established assets, power of assets, property, residence, action, capability, purpose, path, goal, and attainment be mine through sacrifice. || 5 ||
