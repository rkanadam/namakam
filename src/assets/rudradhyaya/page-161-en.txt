151 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Cha. Pra. A.]

He praises the sentences in the fourth Anuvaka:

He says "Rice (vrihi) and absence of hunger (akshud)...". This is indeed the form of food. By the form itself he gains food for him. || 7 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Whatever is described by the sentences starting with "Vrihi...", all that is the form of food. Because the absence of hunger (akshud) is achieved through food, and the subsequent items like sesame etc. are means of food. || 7 ||

He praises the sentences in the fifth Anuvaka:

He says "Fire (agni) and water (apah)...". This is indeed the source of food. From the same source itself he gains food for him. || 8 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Fire is the source of food because it is the cause of cooking. Water is the source because it produces herbs. By reciting this mantra, he obtains food along with its various causes. || 8 ||

He praises the sentences in the sixth Anuvaka:

He offers 'half-Indra' (ardhendrani) oblations. He gains the deities. Since Indra is half of all, therefore Indra is the most favored among the deities. He mentions Indra later; thus he places the power of senses (indriyam) in him (the sacrificer) at the top. || 9 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Those sentences in which Indra is mentioned along with another (like Agni-Indra) are 'ardhendrani' (half-Indra). In those sentences, because deities like Agni and Soma are mentioned in the first half, he gains the deities. Since Indra is mentioned in every sentence, Indra is the 'most favored' (bhuyishtha-bhaktamah). Because the deity Indra is mentioned in the second half here, therefore He establishes the sense-organs (indriya) like eyes etc. in the head-region (top) of this sacrificer. || 9 ||
