[Na. E. A.] Rudradhyaya | 113

Those who are the lords (adhipatayah) of spirits (bhutanam), some shaven-headed (vishikhasah), some with matted hair (kapardinah). || 6 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The word 'bhuta' refers to classes of followers who remain invisible and cause trouble to humans. The Rudras who are the lords of them—some are shaven-headed (vishikhasah), others have matted hair (kapardinah). || 6 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The sixth mantra. Those Rudras who are the lords of the five great elements or of the groups of followers; who are the causes of activity and withdrawal, and who reside in the seasons. 'Vishikhasah' means without hair (shikha) or with various types of hair or forms. 'Kapardinah' means those wearing crowns of matted hair. (Grammatical notes). || 6 ||

He states the seventh Rik:

Those who strike people through the food and through the vessels while they drink... || 7 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Those Rudras who, residing secretly in the food (anneshu) being eaten, strike (vividhyanti) people especially by causing imbalance of humors etc. Similarly, residing secretly in the vessels (patreshu) in liquids like milk or water while they are being drunk, they strike people. || 7 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The seventh. Those Rudras who, residing in the food being eaten like cooked rice etc., strike people. They torment sinful men in various ways according to their sins through diseases etc. caused by imbalance of humors. Residing in the vessels of liquids like milk etc., they strike those who drink (pibatah). 'Drinking' is an illustration. Those residing in food strike the eaters, and those in vessels strike the drinkers. Or those who reside in vessels and cups. (The rest is as before). || 7 ||

For these three, the Purashcharana and everything else is as before.

Those who are the protectors of the paths, the providers of food, the fighters... || 8 ||
