[Na. E. A.] Rudradhyaya | 127

"First, the Brahmin should perform the nyasa (assignment of mantras to limbs), bathe his own body, and then worship Mahadeva according to the rules. On the bank of a river, bathe a single Linga (without its retinue like the bull etc.) that faces the West. Bathe Shambhu again with Panchagavya and a hundred palas of something else. Then bathing with water again, meditate on the Lord of the universe. Anoint Hara with devotion along with 'Gorochana'. Then worship Shiva with Bilva leaves that are not damaged, not worm-eaten, and not stale, and are sprinkled with water. For eleven days, worship Maheshwara thus. Hear more of this procedure from me.

For eleven days initially, perform Homa after the worship. Daily, facing the South, offer Bilva leaves with ghee. Similarly, for those desiring excessive wealth, with blue lotuses and five fragrant substances, perform the Homa and worship for three times eleven days. Burning Guggulu with ghee daily for the Lord, and performing the worship with supreme devotion—he will thus accumulate wealth. If one desiring wealth embraces trees and chants the Rudras, meditating on Shiva, he will see them turning into gold.

Embracing a blossoming Brahma-tree or a Bilva tree, and chanting them repeatedly many times, that expert will see them turning into gold. Going to the holy place of Kanakhala and chanting the Rudras many times, and then chanting in the middle of the water—even black stones will turn into gold. Taking refuge on a great mountain and staying there day after day, at the end of eleven days he obtains abundant treasure. He who sacrifices white Vetasa sticks smeared with honey—at the end, he will have abundant wealth. Bilva and others, being embraced with devotion on the eleventh day, turn into gold at the end of the chant; this is true without doubt." ||
