192 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Cha. Pra. A.]

He praises the sentences found in the seventh and eighth Anuvakas:

He offers the 'sacrificial weapons' (yajnayudhani). Sacrifice is indeed the sacrificial weapons. He gains the sacrifice. Furthermore, this is indeed the form of sacrifice. By the form itself he gains the sacrifice. || 10 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The means of sacrifice in the form of cups like Amshu, Adabhya, etc., are 'forms of sacrifice' because they directly accomplish the sacrifice. The sacrifice is defined indirectly by fuel, sacred grass, and the altar. But it is directly accomplished by the cups. Therefore, they are the 'form' of sacrifice. Through these internal and external sacrificial weapons, he attains the sacrifice. || 10 ||

He shows the purpose of the final 'Svaga-kara' (invocation):

He says "the final bath (avabhritha) and the Svaga-kara". This is for the sake of making them one's own (svaga-krityai). || 11 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: It means for the sake of bringing them under one's control. || 11 ||

He praises the sentences in the first half of the ninth Anuvaka:

He says "Fire (agni) and Gharma...". This is indeed the form of Brahmic brilliance (brahmavarchasa). By the form itself he gains Brahmic brilliance. || 12 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Since the duties of a Brahmin are accomplished by Fire etc., these are the form of Brahmic brilliance. || 12 ||

He praises the sentences in the second half of that same Anuvaka:

He says "the Rik and the Saman...". This is indeed the form of the meters (chhandas). By the form itself he gains the meters. || 13 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Rik etc. are well-known to be the forms of the meters or the Vedas. || 13 ||

He praises the sentences in the first half of the tenth Anuvaka:

He says "the fetuses and the calves...". This is indeed the form of cattle (pashu). By the form itself he gains cattle. || 14 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Fetuses, calves, etc., are well-known to be the form of cattle. || 14 ||

He praises the sentences in the second half of that Anuvaka:

He offers the 'Kalpas'. This is for the sake of making fit what is not fit (akliptasya kliptyai). || 15 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The mantras "May life-span be fit through sacrifice" etc. are 'Kalpas'. Whatever was previously 'unfit' (not capable of its own function), this Homa is for the sake of giving it that capability. || 15 ||
