70  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Aa. Am.]

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: The section from 'Namah somaya' until 'Namo vah kirikebhyah' is considered one sequence. Its Rishi is the Lord Himself. The Metre is Mahanushtup. The Deity is also the Lord Himself. Here, the graceful forms (anugraha-rupa) of the God are displayed. The repetition of names like Rudra, Pashupati, etc., is to proclaim his graceful nature as well. 'Namah somaya cha'—to 'Soma', the one with Uma. Indeed, at the time of grace, the God resides as Soma. Or: to him in the form of the Moon (Chandra). In that form, the God satisfies the ancestors, the Vedas, and humans. Or: to him in the form of the Soma-sacrifice (Soma-yaga). In that form, he joins men with prosperity (abhyudaya). Since the derivation is not fixed, the lack of separation (avagraha) in the compound-view is not a flaw. As they say: "It is not separated (with avagraha) when the etymology is not determined." 'Rudraya cha'—to him who makes beings weep (rodayita) at the end of time. Out of compassion, the God performs the great destruction (maha-samhara) for the removal of the weariness of worldly existence. He who melts away (dravayati) the sorrow (rut) of Samsara is 'Rudra'; these and other specific meanings were already stated. (1)

Salutation to the copper-red one and to the crimson one. (2)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | tamraya | cha | arunaya | cha || 2 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Tamrah'—the extremely red one at the time of rising in the form of the Sun. 'Arunah'—the slightly red one after rising. (2)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah tamraya charunaya cha'. These two regarding the Sun have been explained. As the Sun, the God graces the world by granting awakening etc., and directly witnesses the actions of beings. (2)

Salutation to the maker of happiness and to the lord of cattle. (3)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | shangaya | cha | pashupataye | cha || 3 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shangah'—he who makes happiness (sham) reach (us) or grants it. 'Pashupatih'—the protector of cattle (beings). (3)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shangaya cha'. 'Shangah' is he who makes happiness (sham) go (reach); the giver of happiness to those desiring it, or the producer of happiness itself. [Grammatical note]. 'Pashupataye cha'—to the Master of cattle (beings). The protector from causes of fear like sin, disease, thieves, etc. [Grammatical note]. (3)

Salutation to the fierce and to the terrifying. (4)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ugraya | cha | bhimaya | cha || 4 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Ugrah'—endowed with anger to destroy opponents. 'Bhimah'—the cause of fear to opponents by his mere sight. (4)
