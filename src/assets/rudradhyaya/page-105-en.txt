92 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Da. A.]

Sayanacharya Bhashya: In such a way that there may be peace ('sham') for our bipeds (man in the form of sons, grandsons, etc.) and for our quadrupeds (cattle in the form of cows, buffaloes, etc.). Furthermore, so that in this village, all living beings may be well-nourished (full of happiness) and free from disease (unafflicted), we offer ('prabharamahe') this thought ('matim')—the intellect focused on worship, meditation, etc.—to Rudra. To what kind of Rudra? To the powerful ('tavase'), capable of doing what we desire. To the one with matted hair ('kapardine'), in the form of an ascetic. To the 'Kshayadvira', the one who destroys the opposing heroes (enemies). Or the cause of destruction of sins in the form of ignorance. || 3 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The Rishi of these two, "Imam rudraya" and "Tavase kapardine", is Mandavya. Both are in Jagati meter. Rudra is the deity. Some say these two Riks together form one mantra in Anushtubh meter.

"Imam rudraya": We offer this thought ('mati'), this kind of intellect meant for mental worship, to Rudra. Meaning, we always worship Rudra with our mind. 'Mati' is also a name for praise (stuti). We offer this thought of mine, which is of the nature of praise and worship, to Rudra. Meaning, we always worship Rudra with praise. (Grammatical notes on 'mati' and 'bhram'). The intent is that we make our thought focused on Him uninterruptedly through praises and offerings of Havis. Or, only Rudra is to be praised, meditated upon, served, seen, heard, thought of, and contemplated. We always carry such a thought regarding Him. To what kind of Rudra? To 'Tavase', the one who causes growth. Or 'Tavas' means power; to the one having that. 'Kapardine' is explained. 'Kshayadviraya': One in whom heroes ('virah') reside ('kshiyanti') or who is their refuge. Or 'Kshaya' means destruction; one whom heroes reach and perish. (Grammatical notes). || 3 ||
