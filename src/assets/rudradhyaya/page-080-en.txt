67  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Sa. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Nadyah'—one in the form of river water. 'Vaishantah'—one in the form of water in a small pond (veshanta). (10)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah nadyaya cha'—to him in the form of river water. [Grammatical note]. 'Vaishantaya cha'—'veshanta' is a small pond; to him existing in the water of that place. 'Vaishantas' are those (ponds) made by the gods (natural), where water enters for the purpose of pleasing the deities etc. [Grammatical note]. (10)

Salutation to the one in the wells and to the one in the ditches. (11)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | kupyaya | cha | avatyaya | cha || 11 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Kupyah'—in the form of well water. 'Avatyah'—in the form of water in a ditch or hole (avata). (11)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah kupyaya cha'—to him existing in a well. 'Avatyaya cha'—'avata' is a hole; to him existing as the water in a ditch. (11)

Salutation to the one in the rain and to the one not in the rain. (12)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | varshyaya | cha | avarshyaya | cha || 12 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Varshyah'—one in the form of rainwater. 'Avarshyah'—one in the form of water independent of rain, such as the ocean. (12)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah varshyaya cha'—to him existing in rainwater. 'Avarshyaya cha'—that which is not rainwater is 'avarshyah'; to him existing in ocean water. [Grammatical note]. Some say: to him existing in water that is not rain, like the ocean. (12)

Salutation to the one in the clouds and to the one in the lightning. (13)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | medhyaya | cha | vidyutyaya | cha || 13 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Medhyah'—residing in clouds (megha). 'Vidyutyah'—moving along with lightning (vidyut). (13)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah medhyaya cha'—to him existing in a cloud. [Grammatical note]. 'Vidyutyaya cha'—to him who moves with lightning; existing there. (13)

Salutation to the one in the clear sky and to the one in the sunshine. (14)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | idhriyaya | cha | atapyaya | cha || 14 ||
