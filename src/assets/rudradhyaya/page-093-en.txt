80 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Na. A.]

Salutations to the one who causes slight pain (Akkhidate) and to the one who causes great pain (Prakkhidate). || 14 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Akkhidat' is one who pains slightly. 'Prakkhidat' is one who pains excessively. || 14 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Akkhidate', who slightly pains some by pressure. To 'Prakkhidate', who pains some excessively according to their offense. 'Khid' means striking or tormenting. Both have Vedic additions of letters. This shows how He restrains the wicked. || 14 ||

[Ritual Procedures (Purashcharana) for the Ninth Anuvaka]
Having performed two Kricchra penances, one should chant the mantra 50,000 times to complete the Purashcharana. First, for kings: when a king desires an empire, he should perform what is described below. Time: Spring. Place: A mountain, a Shiva temple, or a forest. In that temple or in the presence of the Lord, having asked Brahmins to pray for success and having worshipped them, the work begins. Start on a Full Moon or New Moon day. One should take Brahmins who have mastered the mantra. Or, one should ensure immediate success through the Acharya. The Acharya should be one from a renowned lineage. Whatever he says, success follows. The method for immediate success: Brahmins should receive the mantra from that purified Acharya and chant it continuously for 15 days. Then they become fit for the ritual. Thus, once success is achieved, Homa (sacrifice) should be performed. An offering is made for each 'Namaskara'. Homa materials are sesame, rice, barley, etc., with ghee. Homa times: From sunrise to sunset, 11 Brahmins should perform this for 20 days. Performers should be given upper garments etc. The Acharya should be given earrings etc. Above the Homa fire, there should be a vessel of ghee with a continuous stream (dhara) flowing until the ritual is completed. The performer should eat only once a day for 8 praharas (the whole day/night). Feed the same number of Brahmins daily; on the final day, double. The Acharya is to be considered as the Lord Himself. Essential land should be given to the Acharya. 100 nishkas or half of that should be given as dakshina to the Ritvijas. Double that for the Acharya. For Shiva puja: Sandalwood mixed with camphor, lotuses, incense with camphor and guggulu, cow-ghee lamp. Offerings as mentioned. Rudra-aradhana should be done annually. By this worship, a king becomes an emperor. Kings in the middle of the ocean will be under him in fierce forms. Leaving this earth, he goes to Shivapura at the end, graced by Rudra-forms. There, becoming a Rudra-form, he enjoys pleasures in Shivapura for ten thousand kalpas, then becomes a Shiva-devotee king of the seven islands. On his hands, there will be the marks of the Pinaka bow. ||
