132 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Pa. A.]

(Meru Tantra continued):
—His sins born of that day and divine sins will be destroyed. By Sandhyavandana they are weakened, and the rest from previous births will follow. One reading of 'Namaste rudra' (Namakam) and its subsequent Anuvakas, then 'Bhavaya namah' etc., then 'Agnavishnu' (Chamakam)—this is called 'Rudri', the cutter of fierce sins. He who performs the continuous Abhisheka with this daily—sins of many births perish within a year. Eleven 'Rudris' are called a 'Laghu-Rudra'. Those whose Linga is anointed with this will never see the Sun's son (Yama/Death). Eleven of these (Laghu-Rudras) are called a 'Maha-Rudra'. The poor obtain wealth, and it destroys great sins. A Homa performed with this grants the fruit of a Soma-sacrifice. For those not eligible for initiation, this is the rule for three fortnights. Eleven 'Maha-Rudras' are called an 'Atirudra'. Since it removes extreme sins, no other atonement is seen like it."
Thus concludes the procedure stated in Meru Tantra.

Thus ends the Fifth Prapathaka named 'Shri Rudra Prashna' in the Fire-section (Agni-kanda) of the Yajurveda Bhashya composed by Shri Bhatta Bhaskara.
|| Thus ends the Bhashya on the Namakam ||
