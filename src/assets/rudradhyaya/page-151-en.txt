138 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Cha. Cha. A.]

(Sayanacharya continued):
...of all people. 'Mahah' is worship. 'Samvit' is the detailed knowledge of Vedas and scriptures. 'Jnatram' is the power to make others know (teaching). 'Suh' is the power to produce sons etc. 'Prasuh' is the power to impel or create. 'Siram' is the equipment for agriculture like bull and plow. 'Layah' is the removal of obstacles to that. 'Ritam' is the ritual like sacrifice. 'Amritam' is the fruit of that. 'Ayakshma' is the absence of strong diseases like tuberculosis. 'Anamayat' is the absence of minor illnesses like fever. 'Jivatuh' is the medicine for life, for removing illness. 'Dirghayutvam' is the absence of untimely death. 'Anamitram' is the absence of enemies. 'Abhayam' is the absence of fear. 'Sugam' is good movement, meaning conduct accepted by all. 'Shayanam' is the wealth of bedding, pillows, etc. 'Susha' is a beautiful morning, endowed with bathing and morning prayers. 'Sudinam' is the whole day endowed with sacrifice, charity, and study. || 3 ||

Thus ends the Third Anuvaka in the Chamaka Bhashya of the Yajur-Samhita, Fourth Kanda, Fifth Prapathaka, in the Madhaviya Vedartha Prakasha composed by Sayanacharya. || 3 ||

He states the fourth Anuvaka:

May strength, sweet speech, milk, essence, ghee, honey, melted butter, eating together, drinking together, agriculture, rain, victory, power of emerging, gold, gems, nourishment, abundance, pervasiveness, lordship, much, more, full, fuller, imperishability, inferior barley, food, absence of hunger, rice, barley, beans, sesame, mung beans, chickpeas, wheat, lentils, millet, fine rice, wild grains, and wild rice—may all these be mine through sacrifice. || 4 ||
