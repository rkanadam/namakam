[Na. Tri. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 39

...the word 'nichera' is formed thus. [Grammatical note]. 'Parichara' is one who moves about (charati) everywhere (paritah); an open traveler. [Grammatical note]. 'Aranyanam pataye namah'—salutation to the Lord of forest-dwellers, i.e., the robbers (dasyus) who live in the forest, or the Master of those who reside in the forest. (5)

He speaks the sixth Yajus:
Salutation to the ones with weapons, to the ones who desire to kill, salutation to the lord of those who steal (grain). (6)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | srikavibhyah | iti | srikavi-bhyah | jighahmsadbhyah | iti | jighahmsat-bhyah | mushnatam | pataye | namah || 6 ||

Sayana's Commentary: The word 'srika' refers to the thunderbolt (vajra). Those who protect (avanti) their bodies with that are 'srikavinah'. Thieves who desire to kill (jighahmsantah) living beings. Salutation to the one in both those forms. Those who, while being farmers, steal the master's grain are 'mushnantah'; salutation to their protector. (6)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah srikavibhyah'. 'Srikam' is a name for the thunderbolt. By that, any weapon is indicated. To those who possess that, or to the attendants of Rudra. [Grammatical note]. Or: to the protectors of weapons like 'srika' etc. [Grammatical note]. Another says: these are special classes of men who live by their skill with specific weapons. 'Jighahmsadbhyah'—to those who desire to kill. Others say these are special classes of men. 'Mushnatam pataye namah'—the taking away of wealth etc. is 'mosha' (theft). From the root 'mush' (to steal). [Grammatical note]. (6)

He speaks the seventh Yajus:
Salutation to the sword-bearers who move at night, salutation to the lord of those who kill and steal. (7)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | asimadbhyah | iti | asimat-bhyah | naktam | charat-bhyah | iti | charat-bhyah | prakrintanam | iti | pra-krintanam | pataye | namah || 7 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Asimantah' are thieves who carry swords (asi). Those thieves who move at night (naktam charantah) and harass people who have gone out into the streets. Salutation to the one in both those forms. Those who steal only after killing (prakrintana); salutation to their protector. (7)
