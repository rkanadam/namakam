14  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pa. Am.]

...destroying all those who are ready to kill directly, and those who cause pain secretly through magic, like Rakshasas, Pishachas, etc., as well as diseases like epilepsy (apasmara), etc. May the God speak for us, saying "Do not fear, I will protect you."

Now for the Purashcharana of this mantra: having performed half-Krichhra penance, and for five days thereafter eating only vegetables, roots, and barley porridge, and chanting continuously while not opposing necessary daily duties, one completes the Purashcharana. Then, let a king have protection (raksha) performed with this. For the protection of all, one should offer eight thousand oblations of white mustard (shveta-siddhartha), rice, Durva grass, and barley, soaked in ghee, into one's own fire or a churned fire, as in daily rites. Specifically for the king, ten thousand oblations. In that same fire, offering eight thousand oblations of charu mixed with sesame, jaggery, barley, and milk, the king becomes long-lived. At all times, one should give Dakshina (fees). Dakshina is of three types: distinguished by the best, medium, and lowest. Ten thousand gold Nishkas is the best Dakshina. Five thousand is medium. Three thousand is lowest. Or, three hundred is best, two hundred is medium, and a hundred Nishkas is lowest.

"In whatever rite a Dakshina is prescribed as part of the ritual, first one should give that Dakshina, and then the Dakshina for the ritual part."

Thus, one should give Dakshina at all times. Or, sixty-four is best, thirty-two is medium, and twenty-four is the lowest. In rites with many performers, the best should be given to each person. In a rite with a single performer, to that one alone. Furthermore, they cite:

"A sacrifice with too little Dakshina destroys the sacrificer."

Kings and those equal to them should give the best Dakshina. Others should give medium, etc.

"He who, while having wealth, out of delusion performs the ritual without the proper extent (of Dakshina), his mind being overcome by greed—he does not obtain the fruit of that rite."

In a rite with ten performers, a thousand should be given as Dakshina. In a rite with five or four performers, five hundred. In a rite with one performer, three hundred. For the Acharya (preceptor), double. The Acharya should be thought of as the Lord Himself. At all times, such Dakshina should be given. There, one should place a mantra-pot (kalasha) near the fire. Placing the mantra-pot in front of the fire, one should establish a pot made of gold or silver. Having invoked the God in that fire, one should perform the homa in his presence. At the end of the homa, one should sprinkle the king with the water from the pot. The king who always honors his Guru becomes especially protected. With this same (mantra), if one is going on a path near an anthill, having consecrated cotton seeds, salt, and white mustard eleven times, he should throw them in directions other than the one he is going. Then he should proceed while chanting, purified. He is freed from tigers, thieves, Brahma-rakshasas, Yakshas, ghosts, etc. With this same (mantra), another method for destroying disease is told—a sick person, having consecrated white mustard seeds seven times, should tie and keep them in his tuft (shikha); health will ensue.
