140 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Cha. Pan. A.]

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Stone etc. are well-known. 'Shyamam' is dark iron. 'Loham' is bronze, copper, etc. Fire etc. are well-known. May domestic and wild animals be fit (kalpantam), through the sacrifice acting as the cause. 'Vittam' is wealth already obtained. 'Vittih' is the gain of enjoyment. 'Bhutam' are assets like sons endowed with lordship. 'Bhutih' is one's own lordship/opulence. 'Vasuh' is the means of residence like cattle. 'Vasatih' is the support of residence like a house. 'Karma' is Agnihotra etc. 'Shaktih' is the capability to perform it. 'Arthah' is a specific purpose. 'Emah' is the happiness to be reached/attained. 'Itih' is the way or means to attain the desired. 'Gatih' is the attainment of the desired.

Thus ends the Fifth Anuvaka in the Chamaka Bhashya of the Yajur-Samhita, Fourth Kanda, Fifth Prapathaka, in the Madhaviya Vedartha Prakasha composed by Sayanacharya. || 5 ||

He states the sixth Anuvaka:

May Agni and Indra, Soma and Indra, Savita and Indra, Saraswati and Indra, Pusha and Indra, Brihaspati and Indra, Mitra and Indra, Varuna and Indra, Tvashta and Indra, Dhata and Indra, Vishnu and Indra, the Ashvins and Indra, the Maruts and Indra, the All-gods and Indra, Earth and Indra, Atmosphere and Indra, Heaven and Indra, the Directions and Indra, the Zenith and Indra, Prajapati and Indra—may all these be mine through sacrifice. || 6 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Agni and others are well-known deities. Because Indra has an equal share with all of them, he is mentioned along with each deity. The word 'Dik' (directions) refers to the four directions starting from East. The word 'Murdha' refers to the Upward direction (Zenith), which is mentioned separately because of its importance.

Thus ends the Sixth Anuvaka in the Chamaka Bhashya of the Yajur-Samhita, Fourth Kanda, Fifth Prapathaka, in the Madhaviya Vedartha Prakasha composed by Sayanacharya. || 6 ||
