[Na. Da. A.] Rudradhyaya | 91

"He should feed Brahmins and have them recite prayers for auspiciousness (Svasti-vachana). Then he obtains a brilliant, healthy, and long-lived son. In case of inauspicious sights or bad dreams, or being struck by the defects of black magic or poverty, or in case of conflict and impending fear, or in case of planetary affliction or possession by spirits, and in other such defects—having made the mandala as before and a new pot filled with scented water and all types of mud, containing cardamom, cloves, camphor, neem, and nutmeg—invoke the deity in the pot and worship and perform Homa. Chanting this mantra 1008 times and then performing the Abhisheka (consecration) on oneself or another, one is freed from all defects. One who daily chants the mantra 1,000 times after bathing in the morning—his wealth, health, and brilliance always increase. One who chants for a year while living only on milk, controlling the senses, and performing worship according to one's power 10,000 times daily—he attains incomparable success endowed with powers like Anima. What are human enjoyments to him when the Lord of the universe is pleased? What more is there to say? With his mind fixed on Him, being devoted to Him, a sage who chants the mantra with that feeling will be liberated."

Meditation:

"Meditate on Shiva in the form of Skanda (the six-faced), brilliant like molten gold, holding a spear (shakti), riding a peacock."

He states the third Rik:

We offer this thought to Rudra, the powerful, with matted hair, who destroys enemies, so that there may be peace for our bipeds and quadrupeds, and that everyone in this village may be well-nourished and free from disease. || 3 ||
