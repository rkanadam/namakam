48  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Cha. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Virupah' are the deformed ones, such as those who are naked or have shaven heads (nagnumunda). 'Vishvarupah' are the attendants who take various forms such as having the faces of horses, elephants, etc. Salutation to them. (6)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo virupebhyah'—to the deformed ones. To the naked and shaven-headed ones. Or to those with missing or extra limbs. 'Vishvarupebhyashcha vo namah'—to those who take various forms like elephants, lions, tigers, etc., by their will. [Grammatical note on 'vishva']. (6)

He speaks the seventh Yajus:
Salutation to the great, and to the small, salutation to you. (7)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | mahadbhyah | iti | mahat-bhyah | kshullakebhyah | cha | vah | namah || 7 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Mahantah' are those endowed with occult powers like Anima etc. 'Kshullakah' are those without them. (7)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo mahadbhyah'. To those who are great (brihantah) in knowledge, form, and power. Regarding whom this is said:
"Those who could place the earth with the sun, moon, planets, and stars on its surface; who do not go to great amazement even after attaining the eight-fold lordship; and at whose actions Lord Shiva is always pleased."
'Kshullakebhyashcha vo namah'—to those with small knowledge, form, and power. Or: to those who live by crushing (kshodayitva), agitating, and causing destruction. Others say: 'kshulla' are those who take (laanti) hunger (kshudh) or crushing (kshoda). [Grammatical note]. (7)

He speaks the eighth Yajus:
Salutation to the chariot-mounted, and to those without chariots, salutation to you. (8)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | rathibhyah | iti | rathi-bhyah | arathebhyah | cha | vah | namah || 8 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Rathinah' are those mounted on a chariot. 'Arathah' are those without them. Salutation to them. (8)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo rathibhyah'—to those mounted on chariots. 'Arathebhyashcha vo namah'—to those who are the opposite, i.e., foot-soldiers. [Grammatical note]. (8)
