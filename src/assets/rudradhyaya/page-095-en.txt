82 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Na. A.]

Now they are being saluted as a group. Some say that after saluting the Lord, his followers are now being saluted. Others say that the forms with well-known appearances have been saluted; now the others, who are secret rulers of the worlds and whose nature is unknown, are being saluted. This latter view is the most established. Being unknown, they are addressed as a group. It is said "Devaram Hridayebhyah" because only the gods know their nature/heart; therefore, this adjective applies to all of them because of the salutation. These others, distinct from those mentioned, are the secret rulers of the worlds, whose 'Kirika'-like nature only the gods know, not humans. Salutations to you too; be pleased and withdraw your weapons.

Salutations to the Kirikas. 'Kiri' refers to acts of violence/destruction, or they are those who shower/create worlds by their will. (Grammatical derivation of 'kirayah'). Those who create/produce worlds by their will are 'kirayah'. They themselves are the 'Kirikas'. 'Devaram Hridayebhyah': To those residing in the hearts of the gods. Only the gods know you. Or you are those who appear as grace in the hearts of the gods. Meaning, those who are always remembered by them for the success of their desires. Or to those who are the hearts of the gods, being their special beloved forms. The gods in the earth, atmosphere, and heaven—Agni, Vayu, and Surya—along with their opulences are 11 each. As in "Ye devaso divyekadasha stha" etc. Thus there are 33 in the three worlds. Each has 100 forms, so there are 3300 gods. There are three groups (ganas) headed by Agni, Vayu, and Surya, totaling 33 each. Together they are 99. The 100th is the head of the group. On earth, there are 11 heads, and being tripled they become 33. These 33 are groups, and there are 11 group-leaders. It is said: "Three gods, eleven, thirty-three, well-endowed gods" etc. These are the deities: the opulences of Agni, and the two forms of Vayu and Surya that follow Agni. Thus all together are 1139. There are as many Vayu-opulences in the atmosphere, where there are two special forms of Agni and Surya following Vayu. Thus all together are 3339 gods. It is heard: "Three hundred and three thousand and thirty-three gods worshipped Agni." All these have various forms and powers, have shares in rituals, and are sparks of the Great Being. This is the process in one universe. There are infinite such universes. Enough of this elaboration. || 15 ||
