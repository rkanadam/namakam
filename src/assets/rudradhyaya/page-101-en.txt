66 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Da. A.]

[Purashcharana for the First Rik]: Having fasted for one day, one should chant the mantra 10,000 times to complete the Purashcharana. Sacrifice 10,000 offerings of cow-ghee; one becomes wealthy. In case of fever or delirium (sannipata), sacrifice 5,000 parched grains (dvangra) near the head of the patient; he will be freed from fever and live long. One desiring freedom from black magic (abhichara) should sacrifice 10,000 Vakankata sticks; he will have no fear from black magic. In case of conflict with many people, sacrifice 10,000 sesame seeds smeared with ghee; fear will vanish. Sacrifice 10,000 Udumbara-sesame seeds; there will be no fear from any beings. In the middle of a village or city, having lit the fire, sacrifice 10,000 balls of sesame-powder smeared with ghee. Or offer them on Pipal leaves. At the start, sacrifice 8,000. Rudra Himself in the form of Bhairava, along with spirits, will become visible. If one wavers upon seeing Him, he goes mad. For the one who remains steady, great success follows. In times of calamity, meditate on that form; one is liberated from the calamity. Meditation:

"One should meditate on the Lord of Mridani (Parvati), whose body is adorned with the Bhairava dress, wearing a fine white garment, holding a damaru, with shining matted hair, holding a trident, with earrings, a tilak, and softly tinkling anklets, the rays of whose beautiful teeth are pure and whose face is white."

He states the second Rik:

O Rudra, your auspicious (Shiva) form, which is the medicine for all days, which is the medicine of Rudra—by that, make us happy so that we may live. || 2 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: O Rudra, by your 'Shiva' (peaceful/auspicious) body, make us happy ('mrid') so that we may live. Why is the body called 'Shiva'? It is explained: because it is the medicine ('bheshaji') for all days ('vishvaha'), being the cause of destruction of diseases, poverty, etc., at all times. Furthermore, because it is the medicine for attaining oneness with Rudra (liberation), removing the pains of birth and death through the grant of Knowledge. || 2 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The Rishi of "Ya te rudra" is Skanda. Chhandas is Anushtubh. The deity is Rudra in the form of Skanda. O Rudra, your 'Shiva' (auspicious/happiness-giving) body—by that, make us happy. May that body bring us auspiciousness. Auspiciousness is twofold: medicine for worldly miseries and medicine for absolute misery (Samsara). || 2 ||
