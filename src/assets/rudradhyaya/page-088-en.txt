[Na. A. A.] Rudradhyaya | 79

He who deserves/is among those who return again to the world (Samsara) is 'Ataryah'. The 'Aladah' is the Jiva (individual soul) who completely ('alam') experiences the fruits of its actions. According to the Shruti: "Of the two, the other eats the sweet pippala fruit." Because He (Rudra) is the prompter and is related to that (Jiva), He is called 'Aladyah'. || 15 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Ataryaya'. 'Atarah' are those who return again to the world due to the remnants of their actions, even after reaching the shore of the ocean of Samsara. By the rule of 'bahulam' (variety), the suffix 'yat' is applied. He who exists there to grace them is Ataryah. And to 'Aladyaya'. 'Aladah' refers to the cycles of creation. 'Aladah' is the wandering in the ocean of Samsara in the form of whirlpools again and again. In the root 'lanj', 'n' is replaced by 'd' by letter-substitution. Or 'ladir' is another root. He who exists in that is Aladyah. Some say: 'Aladah' is in heaven, for it surpasses other happiness. || 15 ||

Salutations to the one in the young grass (Shashpyaya) and to the one in the foam (Phenyaya). || 16 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Shashpam' refers to young grass, kusha sprouts, etc., growing on the banks of the Ganga; He who deserves that is 'Shashpyah'. He who deserves the foam in the middle of the river is 'Phenyah'. || 16 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Shashpyaya'. Shashpa is sacred grass like Kusha and Darbha; He who exists there. To 'Phenyaya'; He who exists in the foam. || 16 ||

Salutations to the one in the sands (Sikatyaya) and to the one in the flow (Pravahyaya). || 17 ||

|| Thus ends the Eighth Anuvaka ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: He who deserves the sand is 'Sikatyah'. He who deserves the flow (of the river) is 'Pravahyah'. The underlying meaning of these four terms (Shashpya etc.) is that He is in the form of the faithful person who constantly remains on the banks of the Ganga, etc., for purposes like bathing. || 17 ||

Thus ends the Eighth Anuvaka in the Shri Rudra Bhashya of the Yajur-Samhita, Fourth Kanda, Fifth Prapathaka, in the Madhaviya Vedartha Prakasha composed by Sayanacharya. || 8 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: Salutations to 'Sikatyaya'. He who exists in the sands. To 'Pravahyaya'; He who exists in the flow/current of the river. || 17 ||

Thus ends the Eighth Anuvaka in the Yajurveda Bhashya of Shri Rudra Prashna composed by Shri Bhatta Bhaskara. || 8 ||
