117 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. E. A.]

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: This Anuvaka, beginning with "Sahasrani sahasrashah", contains ten Anushtubh Riks and is known as the "Avatana" (unbending) section. The phrase "tesham sahasrayojane..." is added to all of them. In chanting, it is used at the beginning and the end. There are also three Yajus beginning with "Namo rudrebhyah". Sages say: The Rudras, created by the Lord at the request of Brahman, are equal to gods. They perform the protection, nourishment, and withdrawal of all stationary and moving beings, and distribute fruits according to deeds. They reside throughout the universe as wind, rays, humans, etc. They are now being pleased. Others say: The Lord, according to the needs of those to be graced, divides Himself into hundreds, thousands, and hundreds of thousands, and grants the grace of sight and desired objects. These infinite divisions exist in the beginningless Samsara for the grace of the world. These mantras are regarding them. Others say: These are sparks of anger of the destroying Lord who roam the worlds; this is the method to please them. Others say: Rudras are the lords of the Ganas similar to Rudra. For the first four mantras, the Rishi is Rushvansa. For the rest, the Rishi is Devala. Chhandas is Anushtubh. Rudra is the deity for all.

The first Rik: Thousands in many thousands. Existing in thousands. (Grammatical notes). Those Rudras who are on the earth (bhumyam). Or 'Adhi bhumyam' means the rulers of the earth. Those who exist as the rulers of the earth, restraining or gracing beings according to their deeds—their bows (dhanvani) we unbend (ava tanmasi); we make their strings unfastened. We please them through praises, salutations, and offerings of Havis, so that their bows become unbent and they become peaceful. 'Sahasrayojane': A thousand yojanas (1 yojana = 8,000 bows). The meaning is: We unbend the bows of those who are within a radius of a thousand yojanas from where I am; let those beyond that remain with their bows ready. Or, we unbend the bows of those who are even beyond a thousand yojanas; then what to say of those who are within that distance? || 1 ||

He states the second Rik:

Those Bhavas who are in this great ocean-like atmosphere... || 2 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Those Bhavas (special forms of Rudra) who reside in this visible, great, ocean-like (arnave) vast atmosphere (antarikshe)—to them, the second half "tesham sahasrayojane..." from the first Rik is added. This applies to the Riks from the second to the ninth. || 2 ||
