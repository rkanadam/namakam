120 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. E. A.]

He prescribes the Homa material:

If one were to sacrifice with the milk of domestic animals, he would afflict the domestic animals with pain. If one were to sacrifice with the milk of wild animals, he would afflict the wild animals. One should sacrifice with the porridge of wild sesame (jatila) or the porridge of Gavidhuka (wild wheat). Thus he harms neither domestic nor wild animals. || 2 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Shucha-arpayet' means he would connect them with a fatal disease. 'Jatila' is wild sesame. 'Gavidhuka' is wild wheat. || 2 ||

He prescribes an alternative:

But they say, "Wild sesame and Gavidhuka are not fit for sacrifice." One should sacrifice with goat's milk (aja-ksheera). This goat is related to Agni. By the oblation itself, he sacrifices; he harms neither domestic nor wild animals. || 3 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Only that substance which is edible is fit for sacrifice. According to the rule: "Whatever is the food of man, that is the food of his gods." Wild sesame and Gavidhuka are not eaten by men, therefore some say they are not fit for sacrifice. After presenting the previous view, he states the conclusion: Therefore, leaving both, one should sacrifice with goat's milk. Since both the goat (aja) and the Fire (Agni) were born from the mouth of Prajapati, they are of the same class. This goat is 'Agneya' (related to Fire) and thus fit for sacrifice. Therefore, he sacrifices with that milk. Since the milk of domestic (cows etc.) or wild animals is not taken, those animals are not harmed. || 3 ||

He prescribes another means to substitute for the sacrificial ladle (juhu):

The sages (Agnirasah), while going to the heavenly world, poured the 'Gharma' (hot milk/ghee) on a goat. She, suffering, cast off her hair. It became the Arka plant. That is the 'Arka-ness' of the Arka. He sacrifices with an Arka leaf, for the sake of common origin. || 4 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Gharma' is the heated ghee mixed with milk in the Mahavira vessel during the Pravargya ritual. The sages, busy with their journey, accidentally poured that Gharma on a goat. She, suffering from the heat of the ghee, dropped her hair, which was like a leaf, on the ground. That mass of hair grew into the well-known 'Arka' (milkweed) plant. Because it was born from the worshippable (archaniya) Gharma and the goat's hair, it got the name 'Arka'. || 16 ||
