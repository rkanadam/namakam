104 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Da. A.]

He states the tenth Rik:

O most bountiful, most auspicious, be auspicious and kind-minded to us. Having placed your weapon on the highest tree, come wearing a skin and bearing the Pinaka. || 10 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: O 'Midhushtama' (the most bountiful showerer of desires). O 'Shivatama' (the most peaceful/auspicious by nature). Be 'Shiva' (peaceful) and 'Sumana' (kind-minded/affectionate) towards us. Having placed Your weapon—the trident etc.—on a 'parame vrikshe' (a very high tree like Banyan or Pipal) so that it is not seen by us, and wearing ('vasanah') only a tiger-skin ('krittim'), come towards us. Even as You come, bearing ('bibhrat') only the Pinaka bow in Your hand for ornament—having discarded arrows etc.—come. || 10 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The Rishi of "Midhushtama" is Vairaja. Chhandas is Trishtubh with five feet. (Grammatical note on meter). The deity is Shambhu.

"Midhushtama": O most excellent showerer. Meaning, the best among males. This is explained. O 'Shivatama', the most auspicious-maker. (Grammatical notes on accents). He says what the Lord should do: "Be Shiva and Sumana towards us." Make us auspicious and make our minds beautiful. Act as if You are helping us. (Grammatical notes). For that purpose, He says: Having placed Your terrible weapon—the trident etc.—on a high tree like the Vata or Karnikara, where we do not see it. Or place it on a tree in a far-off place out of our sight. Wearing a tiger-skin, come towards us. (Grammatical notes). Furthermore, come bearing only the Pinaka—a mere staff without string or arrows—as an ornament in Your hand. Come towards us. (Grammatical notes). || 10 ||

[Purashcharana for this Mantra]: Having chanted for one night (15 praharas), one completes the Purashcharana. In case of fear of loss of wealth, danger to life, fear of fierce spirits, or fear of thieves—sacrifice 5,000 offerings of only ghee; one is freed from them. Meditation:

"One should meditate on the beautiful, youthful deity, the abode of auspiciousness, coming in the form of a forest-dweller (vanachara) and holding the Pinaka."

He states the eleventh Rik:

Salutations to the one who showers wealth, the white one, O Bountiful one! Let your thousand weapons strike someone other than us. || 11 ||
