30  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Dvi. Am.]

...to the Lord of foods (annanam)—to the Master of edible herbs and plants. Salutation to him who satisfies living beings by granting those. (4)

He speaks the fifth Yajus:
Salutation to the one with blue hair, wearing the sacred thread, salutation to the lord of the nourished ones. (5)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | hari-keshaya | iti | hari-keshaya | upavitine | iti | upa-vitine | pushtanam | pataye | namah || 5 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Harikeshaya'—one whose hair is blue (black), free from greying. 'Upavitine'—to Rudra who wears the sacred thread (yajnopavita) for the sake of auspiciousness. 'Pushtanam pataye'—salutation to the Master of men who are full of complete qualities. (5)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo harikeshaya'—salutation to the one with blue hair, who is ever young. 'Upavitine'—to the wearer of the sacred thread; it means for the sake of auspicious conduct. 'Pushtanam pataye namah'—to the Master of those who are full of complete qualities. Or: 'pushtayah' refers to nourishment. There are ten types of nourishment: nourishment of speech, of knowledge, of the body and senses, of house and field, of wealth and grain, of progeny, of cattle, of the village, of Dharma, and of occult powers like Anima etc. Salutation to the protector of those. (5)

He speaks the sixth Yajus:
Salutation to the weapon of existence, salutation to the lord of the worlds. (6)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | bhavasya | hetyai | jagatam | pataye | namah || 6 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Bhavah' is worldly existence (Samsara); 'heti' is its weapon. Salutation be to Rudra who cuts (destroys) Samsara. Salutation be to Rudra who is the protector of the worlds. (6)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo bhavasya hetyai'. 'Bhavah' is Samsara; 'heti' is the weapon; to the cutter of Samsara. Or: to the form of that injury which is the cause of Samsara, salutation to him in that form. [Grammatical note on the word 'heti']. 'Jagatam pataye namah'—to the Lord of the worlds like the Earth, etc. [Grammatical note on the word 'jagatam'].
