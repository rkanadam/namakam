12  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pa. Am.]

...say the etymologists. Indeed, with non-auspicious speech, the God might become angry. As it is said: "Let us not provoke you, O Rudra, let us not (offend you) with our salutations, O Bull, nor with ill-timed cries or joined invocations." Therefore, we praise you with auspicious words. If it is asked why, he says: so that (yatha) our entire moving world (sarvam-it jagat)—all beings, progeny, cattle, etc. The word 'it' includes stationary things like trees as well. 'Ayakshmam'—free from disease. 'Sumana'—here the gender changes; it means endowed with a good mind (soumanasya). 'Asat'—may it be so. It is the fifth mood (Let). This is the kind of thing for which we praise you. Or, in response to the question 'how?', he says: we praise you in such a way that while you are being praised, this (welfare) comes to pass.

For these two (mantras 4 and 5), the Purashcharana is continuous chanting for twenty-one days. Then, having completed the Purashcharana, one should perform the prevention of untimely death (akaala-marana) with these. A king should have it performed by a perfected Brahmin; then untimely death does not occur in the kingdom. With these two, one should offer a lakh (100,000) of oblations of sesame, rice, cow-ghee, and barley, smeared with curd, honey, and ghee, over twenty-one days. In that kingdom, there is no fear of untimely death. In nine days, disturbances vanish. In eighteen days, untimely death is pacified in the whole world.

Meditation (Dhyanam):
"One should meditate on the Enemy of the Cities (Purari), shining with a body prepared for battle, with a gentle smile that is like a thunderbolt to the straw-like three cities, desiring to burn the demons, holding the Mountain-Lord (Meru) as a bow in his hand, and mounted on a chariot consisting of the assembly of gods." (5)

|| He speaks the sixth verse ||

He has spoken for us, he who is the advocate, the first, the divine physician;
Destroying all serpents and all the sorceresses (yatudhanis). (6)

Sayana's Commentary: May that Rudra, who is the 'adhivakta'—one who is capable of speaking for me first, saying "this one is superior"—may he speak (adhyavochat) thus. If he says I am superior, then my superiority is established at once. Therefore, may such a Rudra speak for me. What kind of an advocate? 'Prathamah'—chief among the gods. 'Daivyah'—worthy of all gods; the meaning is he is capable of protecting the gods himself. 'Bhishak'—the physician; because by the mere meditation on him, all diseases are pacified, he is a healer.
