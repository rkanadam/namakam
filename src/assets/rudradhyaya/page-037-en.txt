24  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pra. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: O Rudra, let there be salutation to your weapon in the form of an arrow. What kind of a weapon? 'Anatataya'—not extended or drawn because it is not placed on the bow. 'Dhrishnave'—powerful or bold enough to kill by its own nature. Furthermore, salutation be to your two arms. And salutation to your bow. (14)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: Now the second. 'Namaste'. Let there be salutation to your weapon. 'Anatataya'—unstrung (without the string drawn). From this very adjective, the weapon here is intended to be the bow. 'Dhrishnave'—very firm/strong; or one whose nature is to overpower (dharshana-shila) the non-devotees. Furthermore, salutation to your two arms, the right and the left, of which we are afraid. To show this very thing, for the sake of respect, he makes salutation to the bow again. Or: the first mention of 'ayudha' (weapon) refers only to the arrow. 'Anatataya'—one that is not yet directed (at anyone). Or: weapons like the mace (gada), hammer (mudgara), etc. are meant. Salutation to that which, even though not prepared (unstrung), is terrifying like a painted snake. Then to the arms, where duality is intended. Then to the bow, which is the primary (weapon). (14)

|| He speaks the fifteenth verse ||

May the weapon of your bow avoid us from all sides;
And that quiver of yours, place it far away from us. (15)

[Word-for-word]: Pari | te | dhanvanah | hetih | asman | vrinaktu | vishvatah || atho | iti | yah | ishudhih | iti | ishu-dhih | tava | aare | asmat | ni | dhehi | tam || 15 ||
[Note]: In hand, in the directions, showering... total twenty-two (or similar counts).
|| Thus ends the First Anuvaka ||

Sayana's Commentary: O Rudra, may the 'heti'—the weapon or injury—of your bow avoid (vrinaktu) us from all sides (vishvatah), leave us out. The meaning is: let it not harm us. And also (atho), that quiver (ishudhi) of yours, place (nidhehi) it far (aare) from us. (15)

|| Thus in the Vedartha-prakasha, the commentary on the Yajur-samhita composed by Sayanacharya-Madhava, in the fourth Kanda, fifth Prapathaka, the commentary on the Sri Rudram, ends the First Anuvaka. ||
