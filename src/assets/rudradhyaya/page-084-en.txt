71  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Aa. Am.]

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah ugraya cha'—to him who is not to be overpowered by sins, diseases, hell, etc., and who forcibly overpowers those to protect his 'cattle' (subjects). He is the presiding deity of the form of the Sacrificer (Yajamana). 'Bhimaya cha'—to the cause of fear for those very sins, diseases, etc. 'Bhima' is he from whom one is afraid. [Grammatical note]. The idea is: those of whom the world is afraid, even they—sins, diseases, etc.—are afraid of you. He is the presiding deity of the form of Space (Akasha). (4)

Salutation to the one who kills in front and to the one who kills from afar. (5)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | agre-vadhaya | cha | dure-vadhaya | cha || 5 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Agrevadhah'—one whose killing (vadha) occurs in front (agre). Similarly, 'durevadhah' is one whose killing occurs from a distance. (5)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah agrevadhaya cha'—one who, standing in front (facing), kills. [Grammatical note]. Or: one who kills those desiring to kill, even before they act. 'Durevadhaya cha'—to the one who kills those even at a distance; to the one who kills the wicked even across a long span of time. (5)

Salutation to the killer and to the excessive killer. (6)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | hantre | cha | haniyase | cha || 6 ||

Sayana's Commentary: It means he who kills an opponent without effort, whether that opponent is in front or far away. In the world also, whoever kills an opponent, he (the God) alone is the killer in that form. Therefore, the Lord said to Arjuna:
"These have already been slain by me; be you a mere instrument, O left-handed archer (Savyasachin)."
'Haniyan' is the excessive killer of all at the time of destruction (samhara). (6)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah hantre cha'—to the self of whoever indeed kills; the idea is that every killer kills through your power. 'Haniyase cha'—to the excessive killer. [Grammatical note]. One whose power exceeds all, by which you alone deserve to be the Lord of beings (Pashupati). (6)

Salutation to the trees with green leaves. (7)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | vrikshebhyah | hari-keshebhyah || 7 ||
