[Na. Cha. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 47

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo gritsebhyah'—to the intelligent ones (medhavi). Those who live by pure livelihood (shuddhi-vritti) are 'gritsas'. 'Gritsapatibhyashcha vo namah'—to the Masters/Lords of those who live/act with such purity. [Grammatical note on the word 'gritsapatibhyah']. (3)

He speaks the fourth Yajus:
Salutation to the companies, and to the lords of companies, salutation to you. (4)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | vratebhyah | vratapatibhyah | iti | vrata-pati-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 4 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Vratah' are assemblies (samghas) of various castes. 'Vratapatayah' are their protectors. Salutation to them. (4)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo vratebhyah'. To the groups of various castes, whose livelihoods are unfixed, and who live by violence/force (utsedha-jivinah). 'Vratapatibhyashcha vo namah'—and to the protectors of those companies. (4)

He speaks the fifth Yajus:
Salutation to the groups, and to the lords of groups, salutation to you. (5)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ganebhyah | ganapatibhyah | iti | gana-pati-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 5 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Ganah' are specific classes of spirits (bhuta-visheshah) who are the God's attendants. 'Ganapatayah' are their protectors. Salutation to them. (5)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo ganebhyah'. To the groups of spirits (bhuta-ganas) such as the Pramathas, who are the attendants of the God. 'Ganapatibhyashcha vo namah'—to the protectors of those groups. (5)

He speaks the sixth Yajus:
Salutation to the deformed, and to those of universal form, salutation to you. (6)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | virupebhyah | vishvarupebhyah | iti | vishva-rupebhyah | cha | vah | namah || 6 ||
