116 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. E. A.]

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The tenth Rik. "Ya etavantashcha": As many Rudras as specified in the mantras from "Sahasrani..." to "Ye tirthani...", and even more numerous than that, who have pervaded (vitasthire) all directions. Or: 'Etavantah' means those who cannot be limited by such numbers. Those who exist pervading all directions beyond all limits. (Grammatical notes on 'vitasthire'). The repetition is to show the addition to all previous Riks. || 10 ||

Purashcharana and everything else as before. || 10 ||

Now three Yajus are stated:

Salutations to the Rudras who are on the earth, in the atmosphere, and in the heaven, whose food, wind, and rain are their arrows. To them, ten salutations to the East, ten to the South, ten to the West, ten to the North, ten to the Upward. To them salutations! May they make us happy. Him whom we hate and who hates us—him I place in your open jaws. || 11 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Here, three Yajus should be seen based on the division of worlds like earth etc. The reading is as follows: (1) "Namo rudrebhyo ye prithivyam yesham annam ishavah..." (2) "ye antarikshe yesham vata ishavah..." (3) "ye divi yesham varsham ishavah...". The rest "tebhyo dasha prachi..." is common to all. || 11 ||
