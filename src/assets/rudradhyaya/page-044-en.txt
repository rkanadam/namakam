[Na. Dvi. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 31

...the word 'jagat' is formed thus. [Grammatical note]. The collective meaning is: he who makes beings weary in worldly existence (Samsara) and also protects the weary. Some say: having described the five Brahmans separately with the first five mantras (1-5), the sixth mantra describes the God who is the destroyer of Samsara and the Lord of cattle (beings). (6)

He speaks the seventh Yajus:
Salutation to Rudra who protects with a drawn bow, salutation to the lord of fields. (7)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | rudraya | atatavine | iti | ata-tavine | kshetranam | pataye | namah || 7 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Atatavi' is he who protects (avati) with an 'atata'—a drawn bow. Salutation be to that Rudra. Salutation be to Rudra who is the protector of fields (kshetra). (7)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo rudraya'—explained already. 'Atatavine'—he who protects the worlds with an 'atata'—a bow on which the string is fixed; or he who holds such a bow. [Grammatical note on the word 'atatavine']. 'Kshetranam pataye namah'—to the protector or Master of the 'kshetras' (bodies). For instance:
"The Lord, O Arjuna, resides in the heart-region of all beings, making all beings revolve by his power (maya) as if mounted on a machine."
Or: to the Master of holy places (punya-kshetra) like Avimukta (Kashi) etc. Or: to him in the form of the protector of holy fields. The idea is: salutation to the cutter and promoter of Samsara. (7)

He speaks the eighth Yajus:
Salutation to the charioteer, to the one who cannot be harmed, salutation to the lord of forests. (8)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | sutaya | ahantyaya | vananam | pataye | namah || 8 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Sutah' is the charioteer (sarathi). 'Ahantyaya'—to him who is incapable of being killed (ahantya) by enemies. Salutation be to such a Rudra. Salutation be to Rudra who is the protector of forests (vana). (8)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah sutaya'. 'Sutah' is the charioteer, the controller; by being the controller of the chariot of the world-machine. Or: 'Sutah' is the narrator of the Puranas, in his position at the beginning of the Yugas. Or...
