List of Original Manuscripts (Adarsha-pustaka).

The manuscript of the words of the Rudradhyaya, designated as 'Ka', was given by Keshav Malhar Guruji, a resident of Pune.
Five manuscripts of Sayanacharya's commentary on the Rudradhyaya were obtained.
They are as follows:
   'Ka' and 'Kha'—Given by Vedashastra Sampanna Gangadhar Shastri Datar.
   'Ga'—Given by Vedashastra Sampanna Vasudev Shastri.
   'Gha'—Given by Mahadev Chimnaji Apte, a resident of Pune. It was written in the Shalivahana Shaka year 1739, in the year named Ishwara, on the ninth day of the bright fortnight of Ashadha.
   'Nga'—Sent by Narayan Bhatta Parshvanikar, a resident of Jaipur.
Only two manuscripts of Bhatta Bhaskara's commentary on the Rudradhyaya were obtained.
They are as follows:
   'Ka'—Sent by the illustrious Bappasaheb Mairal, a resident of Vadodara.
   'Kha'—Sent by the Nitsure family, residents of the Wai region.
Except for the book from the Apte family, the time of writing or collection of the other books was not seen.

Vamanashastri Sharma.

Preface for the Third Edition.

Now, a small submission is made to the assembly of scholars regarding the third printing of this Rudra-bhashya. At the time of the (second) printing, wherever errors occurred due to the inattentiveness of the printers or proofreaders, those have been entirely corrected in this third edition, printed after being refined in various places so as to be pleasing to the minds of the readers. If any errors are still seen due to inattentiveness, we request to be forgiven.

Hari Narayan Apte.
