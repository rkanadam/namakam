61  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Salutation to the sound and to the echo. (10)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | shravaya | cha | prati-shravaya | cha || 10 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shravah'—the sound (shabda) which is heard. 'Pratishravah'—the echo (pratidhwani). (10)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shravaya cha'—to him in the form of sound. It is called 'shravah' because it is heard. Or: to him who is heard everywhere, the very famous. [Grammatical note]. 'Pratishravaya cha'—to him in the form of an echo, or in the form of a reply (response). (10)

Salutation to the one with a swift army and to the one with a swift chariot. (11)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ashu-senaya | cha | ashu-rathaya | cha || 11 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Ashushenah'—one whose army (sena) is fast-moving (ashu). 'Ashurathah'—one whose chariot (ratha) is fast-moving. (11)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah ashushenaya cha'—to him with a rapidly pervading army. [Grammatical note]. 'Ashurathaya cha'—to him with a fast-moving chariot. (11)

Salutation to the hero and to the one who splits (the enemy). (12)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | shuraya | cha | ava-bhindate | cha || 12 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shurah'—the courageous one in battle. 'Avabhindan'—striking/splitting (enemies). (12)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shuraya cha'—to the great hero, the fearless. 'Avabhindate cha'—to him who splits (shatters) the haters of his devotees without effort. He who splits the head of those who hate Dharma. [Grammatical note]. (12)

Salutation to the one with a breastplate and to the one with a shelter. (13)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | varmine | cha | varuthine | cha || 13 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Varmi'—equipped with a breastplate (kanchuka). 'Varuthi'—equipped with a house/shelter. (13)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo varmine cha'—to the wearer of a breastplate. [Grammatical note]. 'Varuthine cha'—to him who possesses a 'varutha'—a secret place of protection for the charioteer. Or: to him with a special house. (13)

Salutation to the one with a helmet and to the one with armor. (14)
