[Na. Da. A.] Rudradhyaya | 101

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The Rishi of "Stuhi shrutam" is Vaitaghna. Chhandas is Trishtubh. Rudra is the deity. Because the form is extremely fierce, he addresses the Lord indirectly in the first half:

"Stuhi shrutam": This is the instruction of the inner soul to itself. O my inner soul, praise Him! Or it is a change of person: "I praise such as You." 'Shrutam' is explained. 'Gartasadam': 'Garta' is a name for a chariot; He who stands there, as in the burning of the three cities, etc. Or 'Garta' is the interior of the heart. Some say: 'Garta' is a specific mass of light that pervades the universe. Others say: 'Garta' is the cemetery where beings are consumed by fire. Others say: 'Garta' is the sky having sound. 'Yuvanam' is ever-youthful. Another mantra says: "Youthful father, well-acting Rudra...". 'Mrigam na bhimam': Like a lion or tiger, the king of beasts, of a terrifying form. 'Upahatnum': Ready to destroy/withdraw enemies etc. (Grammatical notes). 'Ugram': Unsurpassable and uplifted. Some say: "To withdraw death"; meaning fierce like death. Praise such a Lord. The second half is direct, as fear is slightly removed by the mention of the Lord. Here he hopes for happiness from the Lord. 'Mrida': Make us happy. 'Jaritre': For the chanter (stotre). (Grammatical note on cases). Make me, the chanter, happy. (Grammatical notes). 'Stavanah': You, the one to be praised. Furthermore, let Your armies—the groups of followers who agitate the world or the masses of rays—strike ('nivapantu') someone else besides us. 'Nivapatih' refers to the act of killing. || 8 ||

[Purashcharana for this Mantra]: Having fasted for one night, chant the mantra continuously for three nights. This completes the Purashcharana. Sacrifice 100,000 Vakankata sticks smeared with poison-oil in the cemetery fire; enemies perish. After taking the enemy's name, chant 10,000 times with ash in hand near the Lord; the enemy gets a high fever. Sacrifice 10,000 white mustard seeds; the enemy gets smallpox and dies from it. In the enemy's house or field, bury hair, bones, gravel, etc., enchanted with this mantra; he is quickly destroyed. Meditation:

"One should meditate on the fierce, auspicious Shiva, brilliant like ten million rising suns, filled with blazing fire, residing in the sky, and praised by the gods." Some say the Sharabha-form should be meditated upon. ||
