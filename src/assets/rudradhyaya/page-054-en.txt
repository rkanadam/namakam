41  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Tri. Am.]

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah ishumadbhyah'. From here on, because of the use of the word 'vah' (you), the mantras are directly addressed (pratyakshakrita) and salutations are offered to groups (samghata). Previously, for the most part, only their Lord (Pati) was saluted. 'Ishumadbhyah'—to those carrying arrows in hand. 'Dhanvavibhyashcha vo namah'—to you who hold bows in hand. [Grammatical note]. Salutation to you who are such. (9)

He speaks the tenth Yajus:
Salutation to those who are stringing (the bows) and to those who are fixing (the arrows), salutation to you. (10)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | a-tanvanebhyah | prati-dadhanebhyah | cha | vah | namah || 10 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Atanvanah' are those fixing the string on the bow. Salutation to you who are in that form. (10)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah atanvanebhyah'—to those who are making the bows strung. 'Pratidadhanebhyashcha vo namah'—to you who are placing (sandadhana) the arrows on the strung bow. [Grammatical note]. (10)

He speaks the eleventh Yajus:
Salutation to those who are drawing (the bow) and to those who are releasing (the arrow), salutation to you. (11)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ayachhadbhyah | vi-srijadbhyah | cha | vah | namah || 11 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Ayachhantah' are those who are pulling/drawing (vikarshana) the string. 'Visrijantah' are those who are releasing the arrow. The rest should be understood as before everywhere. (11)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah ayachhadbhyah'—to those who, having fixed the arrow on the bow, are pulling (akarshat) it. [Grammatical note]. 'Visrijadbhyashcha vo namah'—to those who, having drawn it, are releasing the arrows towards the body of the enemy. (11)
