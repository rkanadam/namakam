40  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Tri. Am.]

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo asimadbhyah'. To the attendants of Rudra who possess swords (asi). [Grammatical note]. To the attendants who move at night (naktamcharadbhyah). [Grammatical note]. 'Prakrintanam pataye namah'—to the Lord of those who cut (chhindatam) excessively. Moving at night, they steal wealth etc. only after killing beings. [Etymology]. (7)

He speaks the eighth Yajus:
Salutation to the turbaned one, to the one who moves on mountains, salutation to the lord of those who steal land/families. (8)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ushnishine | giri-charaya | kulunchanam | pataye | namah || 8 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Ushnishi' is one who wears a turban (ushnisha); a thief who, having wrapped his head in a turban like a common villager, stays and moves among them. 'Girichara' is one who moves on a mountain to steal wood etc. from those who gather it. Salutation to the one in both those forms. 'Kulunchah' are those who 'pluck' or take away (lunchanti) land (kum) such as houses and fields; salutation to their protector. (8)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah ushnishine'—to the one with a turban or a helmet (shirastrana). 'Giricharaya'—to the one who moves on mountains. 'Kulunchanam pataye namah'—those who take away the earth (koh) are 'kulunchas', stealers of the king's land. Or: those who steal in a despicable (kutshitam) way are 'kulunchas', those who steal wealth by causing various afflictions. Or: those who 'pluck' or ruin families (kula) are 'kulunchas'. [Grammatical note]. (8)

He speaks the ninth Yajus:
Salutation to those with arrows, and to you with bows, salutation. (9)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ishumat-bhyah | dhanvavi-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 9 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Ishumantah' are those who hold arrows in hand to terrify. Similarly, 'dhanvavinah' are those who hold bows in hand to terrify. O Rudras who are in both those forms, salutation to you (vah). The double salutation is used to mark the mantra sentence through two attributes. (9)
