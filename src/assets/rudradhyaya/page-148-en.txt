[Cha. Pra. A.] Rudradhyaya | 139

(Sayanacharya continued):
...until its end. 'Atma' is the Supreme Soul well-known in scriptures. 'Tanuh' is the body with beautiful form. 'Sharma' is happiness. 'Varma' is the armor etc. for protecting the body. 'Angani' are the complete limbs. 'Asthani' are the bones situated in their places. 'Parunshi' are the joints like those of fingers. 'Sharirani' are the organs of the body not mentioned before. || 1 ||

Thus ends the First Anuvaka in the Chamaka Bhashya of the Yajur-Samhita, Fourth Kanda, Seventh Prapathaka, in the Madhaviya Vedartha Prakasha composed by Sayanacharya. || 1 ||

He states the second Anuvaka:

May seniority, lordship, anger, outward anger, un-measureableness, water, victory, greatness, excellence, expanse, height, length, abundance, growth, truth, faith, world (cattle), wealth, control, brilliance, play, joy, children born, children to be born, well-spoken hymns, good deeds, wealth gained, wealth to be gained, past, future, easy path, good road, prosperity, success, agriculture, the act of tilling, thought, and good thought—may all these be mine through sacrifice. || 2 ||
