106 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Na. Da. A.]

Sayanacharya Bhashya: 'Kirayah' are the riches that are scattered ('kiryante') in various ways for devotees; He who gives those is 'Kiridah'. The one who gives them especially is 'Vikiridah'. 'Vilohitah' means one without redness, i.e., white. Therefore, the practitioners of the five-syllable mantra (Panchakshara) meditate on Him: "One should meditate on Him whose brilliance is like a mountain touched by the nectar-essence of pearls and coral." Or 'Vilohita' means especially red. Therefore, the practitioners of the eight-syllable mantra meditate on Him: "One should meditate on Him who is like gold and sits on a lotus seat." 'Bhagavah': O Bountiful one, endowed with the six opulences. The word 'Bhaga' is remembered to mean six qualities:

"Complete lordship, righteousness, fame, prosperity, knowledge, and detachment—these six are called 'Bhaga'."

Another definition of 'Bhagavan':

"One who knows the origin and destruction, the coming and going of beings, knowledge and ignorance—he is called 'Bhagavan'."

O Rudra, endowed with these three attributes! Salutations to You. Whatever thousand-numbered weapons ('hetayah') You have, let them all strike ('nivapantu') someone else, our opponent. || 11 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The Rishi of "Vikirida" is Pulaha. Chhandas is Anushtubh. Shambhu is the deity.

"Vikirida": O 'Vikirida'. The word 'Kirida' is explained. 'Kiri' means violence/destruction; to the one who gives destruction (to the wicked). Or 'Vikirida' means one without weapons for hurting others. The idea is that in truth, You have no weapons for hurting others. Or 'Vikirayah' are the sinful tendencies like transmigrating in the world which torment beings; He who cuts them is 'Vikiridah'. (Grammatical notes). 'Vilohita': One whose redness is gone. Blue in the heart and red elsewhere; as in "Daridran nilalohitah". (Grammatical notes). Others say: One whose redness (impurity/agitation) is gone; the changeless one. In truth, You have no causes of agitation. Anger and agitations arising from mental impurity are far away. (Grammatical notes). He states this clearly: (Quotes definition of Bhagavan). Salutations to You. (Grammatical notes). Even though in truth You have no weapons, yet at the time of dissolution etc., whatever thousand weapons You have, let them strike others than us by Your grace. Or, there are no causes of agitation in You, yet whatever thousand weapons/punishments You have for those who do not perform their duties, let them strike someone else besides us. We pray: "May we not be those who are to be punished." || 11 ||

[Purashcharana for this Mantra]: Eating only Charu for three nights, chant 10,000 times. Then chant 10,000 times while circumambulating. This completes the Purashcharana. In case of opposition from many people, sacrifice 10,000 sticks smeared with curd, honey, and ghee; there will be no fear. One desiring to defeat all should sacrifice 100,000 offerings of Apamarga sticks smeared with cow-ghee. By this, even wild animals like boars become killable/subdued. One desiring to defeat animals should sacrifice 100,000 Bilva sticks with curd, honey, and ghee. One desiring to defeat water-fear should sacrifice 100,000 milk offerings in the middle of water; no fear from water will arise. Similarly, whatever one desires to defeat, take that name and sacrifice 100,000 offerings of cow-ghee; one defeats everything. Meditation:

"Meditate on the peaceful, gentle Lord with Ambaya (Parvati), adorned with crescent moon and ornaments, praised by the assembly of gods." ||
