50  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Cha. Am.]

He speaks the twelfth Yajus:
Salutation to the carpenters and to the chariot-makers, salutation to you. (12)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | takshabhyah | rathakarebhyah | iti | ratha-karebhyah | cha | vah | namah || 12 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah takshabhyah'—to the carpenters (vardhaki). All these artisans and specific castes are saluted out of the suspicion that the God resides in them. 'Rathakarebhyashcha vo namah'—to the makers of excellent chariots. (12)

He speaks the thirteenth Yajus:
Salutation to the potters and to the blacksmiths, salutation to you. (13)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | kulalebhyah | karmarebhyah | cha | vah | namah || 13 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah kulalebhyah'—to the potters (kumbhakara). Indeed, they 'cherish' (lalayante) the earth (kum/clay). [Etymology]. 'Karmarebhyashcha vo namah'—to the blacksmiths (lohakara). Their work (karma) is very (aram) purposeful. (13)

He speaks the fourteenth Yajus:
Salutation to the bird-catchers and to the fishermen, salutation to you. (14)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | punjishthebhyah | nishadebhyah | cha | vah | namah || 14 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Punjishtah' are the killers of flocks (punja) of birds. 'Nishadah' are the killers of fish. Salutation to them. (14)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah punjishthebhyah'—to those who bind birds. Indeed, they surround or trap (veshtayitarah) birds that gather in flocks. [Etymology]. 'Nishadebhyashcha vo namah'—to the fishermen (matsyaghatinah); they are indeed 'destroyers' (sadayitarah) in a special way, because of their desire to kill beings in groups. (14)
