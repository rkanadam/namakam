|| Sri ||
Om, Salutations to Shri Ganesha.
Now begins the Rudradhyaya,
along with the commentaries of Sayanacharya and Bhatta Bhaskara.

Introduction (Upodghata) by Sayanacharya.

"I bow to Maheshwara (the Great Lord), who is the Lord of the Ford of Knowledge (Vidyatirtha), whose breath is the Vedas and who created the entire world from the Vedas. (1)
All the brick-pilings (for the altar) were completed in the fourth (chapter). In the fifth, named the Rudradhyaya, the oblation (homa) in the piled fire is described. (2)
Since it is recited in the ritual section (Karma-prakarana), its status as a part of the ritual is also established. Its role as a cause of knowledge is likewise stated in the Upanishads. (3)
When asked, 'Tell us, by what chanting do we attain immortality?', the Sage (Yajnavalkya) replied, 'By the Shatarudriya.' Indeed, the Jabala Upanishad followers state this. (4)
The praise of the Rudradhyaya in the Smritis, Agamas, and Puranas is extensive; that will be described in detail in the Rudra-kalpa. (5)
To support its ritual application as described in the Brahmana section, a clear explanation of the words and meanings is provided here." (6)

Kalpa (Ritual Manual):
"One offers the Shatarudriya with wild sesame gruel (jatila-yavagu) or coix gruel (gavidhuka-yavagu), or with wild sesame and coix flour, or with kusha grass, barley, and ghee, or with goat's milk or deer's milk, using a leaf of the Arka plant. [The ritual instructions continue, describing the position near the fire altar—the northern-western corner, the self-formed brick (svayamatrunna), etc.] One divides the anuvakas starting from 'Namaste rudra manyava...' and offers them. Or, starting from the first and offering up to 'Namah takshabhyah...' holding (the oblation) knee-high in the fire; and starting from 'Rathakarebhyashcha vah...' up to 'Namah svayudhaya...' holding it navel-high; and with the remainder, moving forward... Having offered after the mantra 'Sahasrani sahasra...', one offers the Anvaroha (concluding) oblations with 'Namo rudrebhyo ye prithivyam'..."
