[Na. Dvi. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 39

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah kritsnavitaya'. He whose entire (kritsnam) body is covered (vitam) or clothed with a breastplate (kanchuka) etc. Or: to him by whom the entire universe is enveloped or swallowed at the end of time. The Atharvashiras Upanishad also says: "Salutation again and again to that Great Swallower who has withdrawn (everything)." Someone says: to him who is the form of the entire universe's movement, etc. Or: when the world has perished (vite), he who remains alone as the Self of all is 'kritsnavitah'. As in: "Neither being nor non-being, only Shiva alone (remains)." 'Dhavate'—to him who moves with speed, or to him who is quickly pleased. He remains as the Self of all. Or: he who cleanses the sins of his devotees. 'Sattvanam pataye namah'—'sattvanah' has been explained already.

Now for the Purashcharana of this anuvaka: having performed half-Krichhra penance, chanting eight thousand times while eating charu, one should perform eleven circumambulations of the temple of Shambhu at each junction (sandhya) of the day. At the end of the chanting, having worshipped the God and having fed eleven Brahmins in his presence, one completes the Purashcharana. With this, first for kings: one desiring the death of enemies should offer ten thousand red mustard seeds smeared with oil into the fire; by that act the enemies perish. Having smeared the God with the paste of mustard seeds and chanting eight thousand times while mentioning the name of the enemy, a great fever arises in the enemy kings within eleven nights; by that they perish. When war is near, having placed a red (manjistha) cloth over the God and himself being purified, one should chant eight thousand times for five days, saying "he goes" at the end of the anuvaka while pointing with the right thumb. The enemy king will flee from his place. Now, one desiring to subjugate (vashikarana) should offer ten thousand sticks of Palasa, Ashvattha, Vaikangata, and Apamarga wood, smeared with honey and ghee; he brings the four castes into his control respectively. A king desiring a kingdom should offer ten thousand lilies (kumuda, saugandhika, utpala) mixed with honey; the goddess of the kingdom's prosperity waits upon him. One desiring wealth (shri) should offer ten thousand white lotuses; he becomes wealthy. One desiring intelligence (medha) should offer ten thousand Palasa flowers smeared with the ghee of a Kapila cow; he becomes intelligent. One desiring to conquer enemies should chant eleven thousand times; the enemies are conquered. One should perform a hundred and eight chants and forty-eight (ashtardhashati?) circumambulations and salutations daily for life. Having been happy in this world, at the end he attains Kailasa.

Meditation (Dhyanam):
"One should meditate on Hara (Shiva), whose entire body is decorated with pearls, who holds the moon and the Ganga, seated at the root of the wish-fulfilling tree (Kalpataru) along with Uma." (13)

|| Thus in the Yajurveda commentary composed by Sri Bhatta Bhaskara, in the Rudra-prashna, ends the Second Anuvaka. ||
|| This Second Anuvaka is completed. ||

Sayana's Commentary: In the second anuvaka, some Yajus with salutations at both ends were spoken. Now, twelve other such Yajus are spoken in the third anuvaka.
