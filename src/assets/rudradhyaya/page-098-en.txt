[Na. Da. A.] Rudradhyaya | 85

In a dense forest, having prepared a fire-pit, sacrifice 10,000 Bilva fruits in the fire. Sacrifice 5,000 handfuls of leaves with ghee. Gold in the shape of Bilva fruit will be produced; it will be a touch-gold lump. One desiring an imperial son should sacrifice 100,000 white lotuses smeared with cow-ghee over the Lord; an imperial son will be born. Having prepared Shami sticks, place them in a silver vessel and sacrifice 10,000 smeared with curd, honey, and ghee; he will obtain a Shiva-devotee son. Place white Vacha powder in a silver vessel, mix it with cow-ghee, chant 10,000 times, and consume it; he will become a poet. Sacrifice 10,000 Udumbara sticks with curd, honey, and ghee; he will become a person of great enjoyment. He will be able to travel at the speed of thought (Manojava). The special Purashcharana: Having performed a year-long Kricchra penance and purified oneself, chant for 18 days continuously, eating only Charu (boiled rice), vegetables, roots, and fruits. There should be no intentional sleep; if sleep occurs, chant again after waking. At the end, give five gold nishkas to the Guru. Then chant 50,000 times before the Guru or the Lord. Then Rudra, the husband of Uma, will appear. By His grace, one travels at the speed of thought. To go to any place, think of that place and the Guru, and chant the mantra 108 times; he will reach that place. Thus the method for speed of thought is stated. Now the method for invisibility: Here too, as before, at the time of the ritual, mix red sandalwood with cow-milk, place it on a Pipal leaf, and chant 8,000 times. Then anointing oneself with it, touching the deity and remembering Him, hold the breath; then one becomes invisible to everyone. One desiring to control kings should use red sandalwood paste, chant 10,000 times, make a tablet (gutika) wrapped in thread, and wear it on the forehead like a tilak when entering the royal palace; the king and royal officials will be controlled. To destroy someone, chant their name with 10,000 repetitions standing on one foot in front of the Lord; they will be destroyed within a year. Chant 108 times daily before Dakshinamurti; there will be no fear from royal officials. Meditation:

"One should meditate on Shiva, the mass of light, who has hands and feet everywhere, eyes, heads, and faces everywhere, who is brilliant and pervades the entire universe."

Thus ends the Ninth Anuvaka in the Yajurveda Bhashya of Shri Rudra Prashna composed by Shri Bhatta Bhaskara. || 9 ||
|| Thus ends the Ninth Anuvaka ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: In the Anuvakas from the second to the end of the ninth, various Yajus with salutations on both sides or one side were mentioned. Now, in the tenth Anuvaka, mantras in the form of Rik (verses) are being stated. ||
