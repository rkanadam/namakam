51  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Cha. Am.]

He speaks the fifteenth Yajus:
Salutation to the arrow-makers and to the bow-makers, salutation to you. (15)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ishu-kridbhyah | dhanva-kridbhyah | iti | dhanva-kridbhyah | cha | vah | namah || 15 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah ishukridbhyah'—to the makers of excellent arrows. 'Dhanvakridbhyashcha vo namah'—to the makers of excellent bows. (15)

He speaks the sixteenth Yajus:
Salutation to the hunters and to the dog-keepers, salutation to you. (16)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | mrigayubhyah | shvanibhyah | iti | shvani-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 16 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shvanayah' are the holders of the nooses/leashes (pasha) bound around the necks of dogs. (16)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah mrigayubhyah'—to hunters. Indeed, they seek deer (mriga) continuously with the desire to kill. [Grammatical note]. 'Shvanibhyashcha vo namah'—to the leaders of dogs; those who protect/keep dogs, the dog-handlers. [Grammatical note]. (16)

He speaks the seventeenth Yajus:
Salutation to the dogs and to the lords of dogs, salutation to you. (17)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | shvabhyah | shva-patibhyah | iti | shva-pati-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 17 ||

|| Thus ends the Fourth Anuvaka. ||

Sayana's Commentary: Here, the division should be seen as follows: the second anuvaka was undertaken to praise through the God's play-forms (leela-murti). The third anuvaka was undertaken to praise through the thief-forms (chore-murti). The fourth anuvaka was undertaken to praise through the various forms of different castes and beings.
