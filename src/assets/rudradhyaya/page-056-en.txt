43  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Tu. Am.]

[Word-for-word]: Namah | tishthadbhyah | dhavadbhyah | iti | dhavat-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 15 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah tishthadbhyo dhavadbhyashcha vo namah'. [Grammatical note]. (15)

Salutation to the assemblies and to the lords of the assemblies, salutation to you. (16)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | sabhabhyah | sabha-patibhyah | iti | sabha-pati-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 16 ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah sabhabhyah'—to those seated in assemblies. 'Sabhapatibhyashcha vo namah'—to the Masters of the assemblies, or to the Lords of those places. [Grammatical note]. (16)

Salutation to the horses and to the lords of horses, salutation to you. (17)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ashvebhyah | ashva-patibhyah | iti | ashva-pati-bhyah | cha | vah | namah || 17 ||

|| Thus ends the Third Anuvaka. ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo ashvebhyah'—to those with the bodies of horses. Some say: 'ashvah' means those who have no personal property (svam dhanam), the 'a-shvas'; to those who are property-less (ascetics). [Note: some read 's' and 'sh' as interchangeable]. 'Ashvapatibhyashcha vo namah'—to those who are supervisors of horses, or to the very wealthy. All these are either attendants of the God or specific play-forms of the God himself. (17)

Now for the Purashcharana of this anuvaka: having performed Krichhra penance, one achieves siddhi by five thousand chants. When there are epidemics (janapada-vyadhi), the king should have a peace-rite performed. The chanting should be done by eleven perfected Brahmins in a temple of the Lord. Five chant, five perform homa, and one Brahmin offers sesame-rice for each salutation. A lakh (100,000) of chants should be performed. When this is done, the epidemic perishes. In case of diseases of vehicles (horses, elephants, etc.), by offering eleven thousand oblations of cakes mixed with sesame paste, the common diseases of vehicles perish.
