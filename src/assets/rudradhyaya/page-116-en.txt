[Na. Da. A.] Rudradhyaya | 103

[Purashcharana for this Mantra]: Having performed a Kricchra penance, chant 8,000 times daily for nine days. This completes the Purashcharana. This should be done only by those desiring a son. It should be kept secret. At the west door of a Shiva temple, sacrifice 10,000 offerings of Palasha and Khadira sticks from a forest-fire, smeared with curd, honey, and ghee; he will obtain a son like Shiva. Start in a temple, or under an Ashvattha tree, or in one's own cowshed, on a Full Moon or New Moon day, or the 8th day of the dark fortnight. The couple should eat only Charu the previous day. For every syllable of the mantra, place a pot filled with water and wrapped in thread; in the middle, place a larger pot and invoke Soma. "May the Lord come from Kailasa"—having invoked thus and worshipped with scents etc., starting from the central pot, place one syllable of the mantra on each pot. Then chant 9,000 times. Then, for the desired purpose, sacrifice 8,000 offerings of black grains (shyamaka) and fried grains (laja) smeared with honey and ghee. Sacrifice only ghee as well. Then, taking the first pot and remembering the Lord in the form of that syllable, bathe (the couple). Sitting them on one seat, bathe them with the water from the central pot. Finally, feed 8,000, 800, or 101 Brahmins. Give the pots, clothes, rice, etc., to the Acharya and feed him. At the time of Dakshina, give five or three gold nishkas. There should be no reduction in this Dakshina. After giving this, the couple should eat. Their son will be a great devotee of Shiva and long-lived. If unable to perform this, another method is stated: The unable person should chant this mantra at the west door. Circumambulate the temple for eight nights. (Notes on persistence and effort). A Guru who has completed the Purashcharana should perform this carefully. For the unable, the poor, and those without a refuge: perform a vow for 108, 41, or 21 days, eating only Charu, and chant 800 times daily at the west door. Circumambulate 800 times. Daily chant 8,000 times starting from the root of an Ashvattha tree in the northeast. Chant 8,000 times daily in the four directions of the Ashvattha tree. After completing thus, feed as many Brahmins as possible. He will obtain a son who continues the lineage. Meditation:

"One should meditate on the deity, the destroyer of the elephant-demon, whose body is covered with the elephant-hide, brilliant with flashing weapons, and who removes all sins." ||
