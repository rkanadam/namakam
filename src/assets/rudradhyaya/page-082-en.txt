69  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Aa. Am.]

...one desiring health should offer five thousand Khadira wood-sticks soaked in curd, honey, and ghee. One desiring beauty (rupa) should offer ten thousand mango leaves (chuta-pallava) soaked in curd, honey, and ghee. One desiring wealth (shri) should dig an altar shaped like a heart (?) in a forest, and in a churned fire, offer five thousand white lotuses soaked in curd and ghee; Lakshmi (Shri) herself will wait upon him. By her grace, his lineage will grow as long as the earth exists. One desiring cotton clothes should offer ten thousand cotton seeds smeared with honey; by this, cotton clothes are obtained. One desiring cows should chant this anuvaka ten thousand times while circumambulating a cowshed; he obtains cows. One desiring a son should offer ten thousand Palasa wood-sticks soaked in curd, honey, and ghee; he obtains a son who continues the family. One desiring longevity should offer ten thousand Durva grass sticks soaked in ghee every year; he lives for a hundred years. One desiring knowledge (vidya) should offer a hundred thousand (lakh) Ashvattha wood-sticks soaked in ghee; he becomes an expert in all knowledge. One desiring a kingdom should offer five lakhs (500,000) of Saugandhika flowers smeared with honey; he becomes a sovereign emperor.

Now the means for liberation (mukti) is stated: first, having performed the Chandrayana penance and chanting this anuvaka twenty-one thousand times; then, having performed five thousand circumambulations and Pranayama; thereafter, in his own fire in the presence of Shambhu, one should offer three lakhs (300,000) of Palasa and Ashvattha wood-sticks soaked in honey and ghee. Then, by the grace of Shambhu, he becomes liberated. Others born in his family, even if they were being cooked in the Raurava hell, will delight in the city of Shiva. His lineage will grow as long as this earth exists, for the duration of five Indras.

Meditation (Dhyanam):
"One should meditate on the God with long hanging matted hair, granting absolute fearlessness, with a pure smile, and adorned with a tiger skin."

|| Thus in the Yajurveda commentary composed by Sri Bhatta Bhaskara, in the Sri Rudra-prashna, ends the Seventh Anuvaka. ||
|| This Seventh Anuvaka is completed. ||

Sayana's Commentary: In the seventh anuvaka, those Yajus with salutation at one end were spoken. Now, other such Yajus are being stated in the eighth. In the eighth anuvaka, he speaks seventeen Yajus.

Salutation to the one with Uma and to the remover of sorrow. (1)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | somaya | cha | rudraya | cha || 1 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Somah'—he who exists along with Uma (sa-Uma). 'Rudrah'—he who melts/removes (dravayati) the sorrow (rut) which is the cause of weeping.
[Note]: For 'Somaya', since no separation (avagraha) is seen in the Padapatha, the derivation as a compound (Sa+Uma) is not supported. Therefore, the derivation 'he who produces/gives (sute) nectar (Soma)' seems more appropriate. (1)
