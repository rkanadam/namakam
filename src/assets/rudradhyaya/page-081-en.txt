68  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Sa. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Idhram' is a shining autumn cloud due to its purity; one who exists there is 'idhriyah'. 'Atapyah'—one in the form of rain occurring with sunshine. (14)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah idhriyaya cha'. 'Idhram' is a shining autumn cloud, because of its waterlessness (purity). [Grammatical note]. To him existing there. Or: to him existing in water heated by contact with fire etc. 'Atapyaya cha'—to him existing in sunshine (atapa). (14)

Salutation to the one in the wind and to the one in the storm. (15)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | vatyaya | cha | reshmiyaya | cha || 15 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Vatyah'—one seen along with the wind (vata). 'Reshma' is the time of dissolution (pralaya) where beings perish (rishyanti); he who exists there in the form of a specific type of rainwater mixed with gravel, stones, etc., is 'reshmiyah'. (15)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah vatyaya cha'—to him existing in rains produced by the wind. 'Reshmiyaya cha'—'reshma' is the end of an age (yuganta) when a wind named Samvarta blows, raining stones and gravel; that is 'reshma'. Some say it refers to the summer season. Others say 'reshma' are the dust clouds raised by the wind. To him existing there. [Grammatical note]. (15)

Salutation to the one in the wealth and to the one who protects the site. (16)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | vastavyaya | cha | vastupaya | iti | vastu-paya | cha || 16 ||

|| Thus ends the Seventh Anuvaka. ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Vastu' is wealth in the form of cattle, horses, and other objects; he who exists there as those objects is 'vastavyah'. 'Vastu' is land for building a house; its protector is 'vastupah'. (16)

|| Thus in the Vedartha-prakasha, the commentary on the Yajur-samhita composed by Sayanacharya-Madhava, in the fourth Kanda, fifth Prapathaka, the commentary on the Sri Rudram, ends the Seventh Anuvaka. ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah vastavyaya cha'. 'Vastu' is wealth like cattle, horses, etc.; to him existing as those effects. Or: to him existing in the building-site (vastu). 'Vastupaya cha'—one who protects (pa-ti) the site. [Grammatical note]. Or to the Master of the dwelling. As it is said: "Salutation to Rudra, the Lord of the Dwelling (vastoshpati)." (16)

Now for the Purashcharana of this anuvaka: having fasted for one day and chanting eight hundred times, one becomes a perfected practitioner. By this, one desiring the control of the four castes should offer ten thousand sticks of Ashvattha, Udumbara, Vata, and Plaksha wood, soaked in curd, honey, and ghee; they will then be in his control. One desiring intelligence (medha) should offer five thousand oblations of Kapila-cow ghee; he becomes intelligent. One desiring nourishment (pushti) should offer nine thousand cakes (apupa).
