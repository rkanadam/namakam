[Na. Pra. Am.] Rudradhyaya. 3

"...becomes purified from the theft of gold; becomes purified from the drinking of liquor; becomes purified from the killing of a Brahmin; becomes purified from performing or not performing actions (as required). Therefore, one who has taken refuge in this becomes as described. One who is beyond the stages of life (Atyashrami) should chant it always or even once.
          'By this one attains knowledge that destroys the ocean of worldly existence.
          Therefore, knowing this thus, one enjoys the fruit of liberation (Kaivalya).'
          Thus, 'one enjoys the fruit of liberation.'"

Shakatatapa says: "Having committed theft, and having gone to the Guru's wife, having drunk liquor, and having committed the killing of a Brahmin—one who is covered in ashes, lying on a bed of ashes, and reciting the Rudradhyaya is freed from all sins."

Yajnavalkya says:
         "A drinker of liquor or a stealer of gold, standing in water and chanting the Rudra, or chanting the 'Sahasra-shirsha' (Purusha Suktam), is freed from all sins.
          The chanting of the Shukriya Aranyaka and especially the Gayatri—these are indeed removers of all sins, and so is the Rudra-ekadashini (reciting Rudram eleven times).
          By chanting the Veda once, one is purified even then; by chanting the Rudra-ekadashini, one is purified even then."

Shankha says: "A stealer of gold is freed by reciting the Rudradhyaya."

Atri (or Ratna-panjara) says: "Having brought together the Rudras eleven times like a righteous man, even if touched by great sins, one is freed; there is no doubt in this."

In the Vayu-prokta Purana (Vayu Purana):
"He who chants the Rudras daily, meditating on Maheshwara...
(compared to) one who would give as a gift the earth up to the ocean, with its mountains and forests, endowed with all natural qualities, beautiful with excellent trees and water, along with gold and medicinal herbs—greater than even that is the merit from a single chanting of the Rudra.
He who, having abandoned the sense of 'I' and 'mine', always chants the Rudras, surely becomes Rudra even in that very body.
By this chanting, O lords of Brahmins, kings and other twice-born ones, having left all sin, went to the city of Shiva in the past. I too have obtained proximity to the Supreme Lord in this way."

Also: "He who daily employs the triad—Chamakam, Namakam, and the Purusha Suktam—is glorified in the world of Brahma."

Also: "One should always chant Chamakam, Namakam, and the Purusha Suktam... to the Great Lord (Mahadeva), the best of beings, just as a householder stays in his house."
