36  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Tri. Am.]

He speaks the first Yajus:
Salutation to the vanquisher, to the one who pierces, salutation to the lord of the striking (armies). (1)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | sahamanaya | nivyadhine | iti | ni-vyadhine | avyadhininam | iti | a-vyadhininam | pataye | namah || 1 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Sahamanah'—to him who overpowers opponents. 'Nivyadhi'—to him who pierces (vidhyati) opponents extremely (ni). Salutation be to such a one. 'Avyadhinyah' are the heroic armies that pierce from all sides; salutation to their protector. (1)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: For this anuvaka starting with 'Namah sahamanaya', the Rishi is Mahadeva. The Metre is Brihati. The Deity is also Mahadeva. In the previous anuvaka, the God's lordship over material objects was established. Here, he begins to show his lordship over actions (kriya). 'Namah sahamanaya'—the root 'sah' in the Veda means to overpower. He overpowers everything other than himself. [Grammatical note on the word 'sahamanaya']. Another says: 'sahamanaya' means one who forgives (kshamamana), i.e., who bears the offenses committed by his devotees. 'Nivyadhine'—to one whose nature is to strike extremely. [Grammatical note]. It means one who pierces even his non-offending devotees. 'Avyadhininam pataye namah'—to the Lord/Master of armies that strike from the front or from all sides. [Grammatical note].

He speaks the second Yajus:
Salutation to the chief, to the sword-bearer, salutation to the lord of thieves. (2)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | kakubhaya | nishangine | iti | ni-sangine | stenanam | pataye | namah || 2 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Kakubhaya'—to him who is like a peak (kakubh), meaning the foremost/chief. 'Nishangine'—to him with a sword in his hand. 'Stenanam pataye'—salutation to the protector of 'stenas' (secret thieves). Rudra, like an actor, assumes various roles as a play (leela). Or: because he is the self of the whole world, in whatever form (people) exist anywhere, he is in that very form...
