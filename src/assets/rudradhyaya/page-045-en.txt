32  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Dvi. Am.]

...to the 'suta', the self of the pressed (abhishuta) Soma. [Grammatical note]. 'Ahantyaya'—to him who is not to be killed (ahantavya), who is the Light of the Self (Atma-jyotis). He is not killed because of his possession of great power. [Grammatical note]. Or: 'ahanta' is the feeling that "I am everything"; he who exists in that is 'ahantya'. Or: 'aham' is an indeclinable, established in the 'Ahamkara' (I-ness/Ego). Salutation to the Lord of forests (vananam)—to the Master of diverse forests or cremation grounds (shmashana). (8)

He speaks the ninth Yajus:
Salutation to the red one, to the chief, salutation to the lord of trees. (9)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | rohitaya | sthapataye | vrikshanam | pataye | namah || 9 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Rohitah' is the red-colored one. 'Sthapatih' is the Chief (Prabhu). Salutation be to that Rudra. Salutation to Rudra who is the protector of trees (vriksha). (9)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo rohitaya'—to the red-colored one. Someone says: the syllable 'Ro' refers to the form of speech; he who is beneficial (hita) for that, or residing in that. 'Sthapataye'—to the cutter (khandaka). Or: the creator of houses, etc. Another says: he who stays (sthata) everywhere and is the protector (pata) of everything. Salutation to the Lord of trees (vriksha). [Trees are those which he establishes and cuts]. (9)

He speaks the tenth Yajus:
Salutation to the minister, to the merchant, salutation to the lord of thickets. (10)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | mantrine | vanijaya | kakshanam | pataye | namah || 10 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Mantri' is one skilled in deliberation/counsel (mantra-alochana) in the royal assembly. He is 'vanijah' as he exists in the form of the master of merchants. Salutation be to that Rudra. To the protector of thickets (kaksha), which are bushes etc. found in the forest. (10)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo mantrine'—possessing the 'mantras' which are the five Brahmans. Or: 'mantra' is secret speech...
