52  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: 'Bilmi'—one possessing a 'bilma', which is a head-protector (helmet) with holes used in war. 'Kavachi'—one possessing a 'kavacha', which is a body-protector (armor). (14)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah bilmine cha'. 'Bilma' is a helmet etc. with holes (vila) like a burrow. Others say it is a vessel for ashes. Etymologists say "bilma is a vessel". To the one possessing that. 'Kavachine'—to the one with limb-protection. Or: a 'kavacha' is a breastplate hanging down to the ankles. (14)

Salutation to the famous and to the one with a famous army. (15)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | shrutaya | cha | shruta-senaya | cha || 15 ||

|| Thus ends the Sixth Anuvaka. ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Shrutah'—renowned in the Vedas. 'Shrutasenah'—one whose army (sena) is famous among the panegyrists. (15)

|| Thus in the Vedartha-prakasha, the commentary on the Yajur-samhita composed by Sayanacharya-Madhava, in the fourth Kanda, fifth Prapathaka, the commentary on the Sri Rudram, ends the Sixth Anuvaka. ||

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah shrutaya cha'—to the renowned, or to him famous in the Shrutis (Vedas). As it is said: "You are the Overlord of the universe, the Presider over the universe, the Form of the universe." The idea is that the Shrutis alone are the authority for this. 'Shrutasenaya cha'—to him with a famous army, or an army famous in the Shrutis. Those weapons of yours that were mentioned, like arrows etc., are also famous in the Shrutis. [Grammatical note].

Now for the Purashcharana of these two anuvakas (5th and 6th): having eaten charu for three nights, one should chant continuously thereafter. Within eleven days, one becomes a perfected practitioner. Then the specific rituals are told: for royal prosperity (rajya-shri), one should throw white lotuses on top of the Linga for each salutation eleven times for a month. Prosperity will be obtained. One should feed eleven Brahmins in the presence of the God Dakshinamurti. On the King's birthday, one should offer twenty-one thousand Ashvattha sticks soaked in ghee into the King's fire or a churned fire. Eleven thousand for each of the two (anuvakas). When this is done, the King's victory and longevity increase. For the sake of victory, one should chant a hundred thousand (lakh) times month after month. With these two, when war is near, having offered eleven times for each salutation cooked food from the King's kitchen along with side dishes, curd, honey, and ghee, one should place the remainder on the ground, take it back mixed with water, and throw it toward the enemy army. That army will be destroyed before long. Having offered pure rice soaked in ghee into the fire with these two, one should throw the remainder towards the enemy; he perishes. With these two, having touched all the King's weapons, one should consecrate them a thousand times; their brilliance increases. With these two alone, one should throw (objects) toward the enemy army; that army will be destroyed for a long time.
