55  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Sayana's Commentary: Through the guise of Indra, he is thousand-eyed. By manifesting with a thousand arms, he is endowed with a hundred bows. (5)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah sahasrakshaya cha'—to the many-eyed one, or to him in the form of Indra. 'Shatadhanvane cha'—to him with many bows, or with many weapons. (5)

Salutation to the one who resides on the mountain and to the one who is pervaded by rays. (6)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | girishaya | cha | shipivishtaya | cha || 6 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Girishah' is he who resides (shete) on the mountain Kailasa. 'Shipivishtah' is he who wears the form of Vishnu; for the Shruti says, "Vishnu is indeed Shipivishta." (6)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo girishaya cha'—already explained. 'Shipivishtaya cha'—to him who has cast off the remainder (nirveshtita-sheshaya), always in places like Deodar forests. Or: to the one who is the self of the Sun. As stated in the Skanda Purana:
"Water is called 'shiti' because of coldness or its connection with resting (shayana). Because of drinking or protecting that, rays (rashmayah) are known as 'shipayah'. The Sun who has entered into them is then called 'Shipivishta'."
Or: 'shipayah' means cattle. For the Shruti says: "Sacrifice is indeed Vishnu, cattle are 'shipi'." The meaning is: to him who has entered into all beings as the Inner Ruler (Antaryami). (6)

Salutation to the most bountiful and to the one with arrows. (7)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | midhuh-tamaya | cha | ishumate | iti | ishu-mate | cha || 7 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Midhustamah'—one who rains (shatters) excessively in the form of a cloud. 'Ishuman'—the holder of arrows. (7)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo midhustamaya cha'—to the greatest shedder/pourer. [Grammatical note]. 'Ishumate cha'—to the one with excellent arrows, or holding arrows in hand; it means a great hero. (7)

Salutation to the short and to the dwarf. (8)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | hrasvaya | cha | vamanaya | cha || 8 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Hrasva'—having a small measure in the body. 'Vamana'—dwarfness due to the contraction of limbs like fingers etc. (8)
