56  Sayanacharya—Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya-Sahita— [Na. Pam. Am.]

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah hrasvaya cha'—to the one of small measure. 'Vamanaya cha'—to the one with contracted limbs. (8)

Salutation to the great and to the most excellent. (9)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | brihate | cha | varshiyase | cha || 9 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Brihan'—large in size. 'Varshiyan'—rich in qualities. (9)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo brihate cha'—to the one great in size. [Grammatical note]. 'Varshiyase cha'—to the one most excellent in size, or in knowledge etc. [Grammatical note]. (9)

Salutation to the old and to the one who well-increases. (10)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | vriddhaya | cha | sam-vridhvane | cha || 10 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Vriddhah'—advanced in age. 'Samvridhva'—well-augmented by the hymns (of the Veda). (10)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo vriddhaya cha'—great in age. 'Samvridhvane cha'—to the one who is well-grown. It means one who is constantly increased (in glory) by hymns of praise. As it is said: "May good praises increase you." Or: to the one who well-increases his devotees. [Grammatical note]. (10)

Salutation to the foremost and to the first. (11)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | agriyaya | cha | prathamaya | cha || 11 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Agriyah'—existing before the creation of the world. 'Prathamah'—chief in the assembly. (11)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namo agriyaya cha'—to the one existing at the beginning (agre). [Grammatical note]. 'Prathamaya cha'—to the principal, the foremost. (11)

Salutation to the swift and to the agile. (12)

[Word-for-word]: Namah | ashave | cha | ajiraya | cha || 12 ||

Sayana's Commentary: 'Ashuh'—pervading quickly. 'Ajirah'—skilled in moving. (12)

Bhatta-Bhaskara-Bhashya: 'Namah ashave cha'—to the one who pervades rapidly. [Grammatical note]. 'Ajiraya cha'—to the one skilled in movement. From the root 'aj' (to go/throw). [Grammatical note]. (12)
