[Na. E. A. Rudradhyaya] 111

(Sayanacharya continued): This addition is specifically shown again in the tenth Rik. || 2 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The second Rik. "Asmin mahatyarnave": The word 'ye' (those who) is understood. In this great atmosphere (arnave), which is so called because it contains the water of rain (arnasa). Those Bhavas (Rudras) who are the rulers of the atmosphere or situated above it. The rest "tesham..." is as before. Or some say "Bhavas in the ocean and in the atmosphere." Atmosphere is that which resides between heaven and earth. || 2 ||

He states the third Rik:

Those Sharvas who are blue-necked and white-throated, who roam in the lower regions... || 3 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Those who are blue-colored in one part of the neck (nila-grivah) and white-colored in another (shiti-kanthah). Such Sharvas (special forms of Rudra). What kind? 'Adhah kshamacharah', who roam in the regions below the earth, i.e., in the Patala worlds. To them (the rest is as before). || 3 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The third Rik. Here too 'ye' is understood. Blue-necked and white-throated. Roaming below the earth, those who exist as rulers in the Patala worlds. Those Sharvas/Rudras. The rest is the same. || 3 ||

He states the fourth Rik:

Those Rudras who are blue-necked and white-throated, who have reached the heavens... || 4 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Those who have reached the heavens (divam), residing in the celestial worlds. || 4 ||

Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya: The fourth Rik. 'Ye' is understood. Those blue-necked and white-throated Rudras who have reached the heaven of Indra etc. as its owners. (Grammatical notes). || 4 ||
