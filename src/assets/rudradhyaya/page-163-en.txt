194 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Cha. Pra. A.]

He praises the sentences in the first part of the eleventh Anuvaka:

He offers the even and the odd (yugmad-ayuje), for the sake of pairing (mithunatvaya). || 16 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: "Four and eight..." etc. is the group of even numbers (yugmam). "One and three..." etc. is the group of odd numbers (ayugmam). These two sets, like male and female, are mutually distinct, and thus they result in 'pairing'. || 16 ||

He praises the progressive increase in both sets:

They are 'subsequent' (uttaravati), for the sake of over-reaching (abhikranyai). || 17 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: After one comes three. After that comes five. Similarly, after four comes eight, and after that twelve. Thus, by the 'subsequent' nature of both, there is a total pervasiveness (all-around coverage). || 17 ||

He shows the purpose of the odd and even number words:

He says "One and three...". The odd numbers (one and three) are the meters of the gods. The even numbers (four and eight) are the meters of men. Thus he gains the meters of the gods and the meters of men. || 18 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The meters dear to the gods are accomplished by odd numbers. Those dear to men are accomplished by even numbers. Therefore, he gains both. || 18 ||

He praises the goal of the progressive numerical increase in both sets:

He offers up to thirty-three. Thirty-three are indeed the deities. He gains the deities. He offers up to forty-eight. The Jagati meter has forty-eight syllables. Cattle are related to Jagati. By the Jagati itself he gains cattle for him. || 19 ||

He praises the sentences in the second part of this Anuvaka:

He offers the twelve starting with "Vaja and Prasava...". Twelve are the months, a year. He stands firm in the year itself. || 20 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Here is the summary of applications:
"One should offer a single continuous stream of ghee (Vasordhara) with the first eleven Anuvakas starting with Agnavishnu." || 21 ||
