[A. De. An.] Rudradhyaya | 161

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Because the duties of a Brahmin are accomplished by Fire (Agni) etc., these are the form of Brahmic brilliance (brahma-varchasa). || 12 ||

He praises the sentences in the second half of that same Anuvaka:
He says "the Rik and the Saman...". This is indeed the form of the meters (chhandas). By the form itself he gains the meters. || 13 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Rik etc. are well-known to be the forms of the meters or the Vedas. || 13 ||

He praises the sentences in the first half of the tenth Anuvaka:
He says "the fetuses and the calves...". This is indeed the form of cattle (pashu). By the form itself he gains cattle. || 14 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Fetuses, calves, etc., are well-known to be the form of cattle. || 14 ||

He praises the sentences in the second half of that Anuvaka:
He offers the 'Kalpas'. This is for the sake of making fit what is not fit (akliptasya kliptyai). || 15 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The mantras "May life-span be fit through sacrifice" etc. are 'Kalpas'. Whatever was previously 'unfit' (not capable of its own function), this Homa is for the sake of giving it that capability. || 15 ||
