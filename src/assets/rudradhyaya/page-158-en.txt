[Cha. Pra. A.] Rudradhyaya | 149

He states the tenth Anuvaka:

May the fetuses, the calves, the eighteen-month-old male, the eighteen-month-old female, the two-year-old male, the two-year-old female, the two-and-a-half-year-old male, the two-and-a-half-year-old female, the three-year-old male, the three-year-old female, the three-and-a-half-year-old male, the three-and-a-half-year-old female, the four-year-old male, the four-year-old female, the breeding bull, the barren cow, the majestic bull, the newly-calved cow (that miscarried), the draft-ox, and the milch-cow—may all these be mine; may life-span be fit through sacrifice, may Prana, Apana, Vyana, eyes, ears, mind, speech, the soul, and the sacrifice itself be fit through sacrifice. || 10 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: Fetuses and calves are well-known. 'Tryavih' is a male calf of one-and-a-half years. 'Tryavi' is a female of that age. 'Dityavat' is a bull of two years. 'Dityauhi' is a cow of that age. 'Panchavih' is a bull of two-and-a-half years. 'Panchavi' is a cow of that age. 'Trivatsah' is a bull of three years. 'Trivatsa' is a cow of that age. 'Turyavat' is a bull of three-and-a-half years. 'Turyauhi' is a cow of that age. 'Prashthavat' is a bull of four years. 'Prashthauhi' is a cow of that age. 'Uksha' is a bull capable of breeding. 'Vasha' is a barren cow. 'Rishabhah' is a bull older than an 'Uksha'. 'Vehat' is a cow that miscarries. 'Anadvan' is a draft-ox for a cart. 'Dhenuh' is a cow that has recently calved. Life-span and others are well-known. 'Atma' is the body. May all these be capable of their functions through my sacrifice. May the sacrifice to be performed (like Ashvamedha etc.) be capable of its result through this ritual being performed. || 10 ||

Thus ends the Tenth Anuvaka in the Chamaka Bhashya of the Yajur-Samhita, Fourth Kanda, Fifth Prapathaka, in the Madhaviya Vedartha Prakasha composed by Sayanacharya. || 10 ||
