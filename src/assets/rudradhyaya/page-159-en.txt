146 Sayanacharya—along with Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya— [Cha. Pra. A.]

He states the eleventh Anuvaka:

May one, three, five, seven, nine, eleven, thirteen, fifteen, seventeen, nineteen, twenty-one, twenty-three, twenty-five, twenty-seven, twenty-nine, thirty-one, thirty-three; and four, eight, twelve, sixteen, twenty, twenty-four, twenty-eight, thirty-two, thirty-six, forty, forty-four, forty-eight; and food, production, re-production, sacrifice, the sun, the zenith, the pervasive (sky), the end-born, the end-dweller, the earthly-born, the world, and the ruler—may all these be mine through sacrifice. || 11 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The words 'eka' (one) etc. are for numbers. (The numbers represent various powers and deities). May all these from 'Vaja' (food) onwards be mine. 'Vajah' is food. 'Prasavah' is its production. 'Apijah' is its repeated production. 'Kratuh' is the resolve or the sacrifice for enjoyment. 'Suvah' is the Sun, the cause of that production. 'Murdha' is the Heaven (Dyaus). 'Vyashniyah': that which pervades (ashnute) especially is 'vyashna' (space/sky etc.), and what is in it is 'vyashniyah'. 'Antyayanah' is one born at the end. 'Antyah' is one situated at the end. 'Bhauvanah' is one who is in the 'Bhuvana' (world). 'Bhuvanah' is the world itself. 'Adhipatih' is the Lord who presides and protects. According to Ahobala Bhashya, this means 'the King'. Alternatively, 'Vaja' and others are special names of the months starting from Chaitra. In that case, the sentence ends with: "May all they be pleased." The word 'cha' (and) is for mutual collection, or for including unmentioned deities of time etc. || 11 ||

He prescribes the Homa to be performed with the eleven Anuvakas mentioned:

He offers the 'Vasordhara' (stream of wealth). "May a stream of wealth be mine"—with this intent it is offered. This stream of ghee waits for him, swelling in that world (afterlife). || 1 ||

Sayanacharya Bhashya: The oblation by which a continuous stream ('dhara') of wealth ('vasu') is obtained is called 'Vasordhara'. One should offer it. Its definition is stated: The sacrificer who offers it first desires "May there be continuity of wealth for me." Then he offers this oblation. By that Homa, a stream of ghee waits for this sacrificer in the heavenly world, pleasing and serving him. || 1 ||
