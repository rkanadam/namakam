Broadly each Veda consists of four parts: (1) the Mantra-samhitā or hymns, (2) the brāhmaṇā or explanations of Mantras or rituals, (3) the āraṇyakās, and (4) the upaniṣads.

ṛig Veda: ṛig is derived from the word 'ṛic' which literally means 'praise or verse', especially a sacred verse recited in praise of a deity. It has 8 kāṇḍās (aṣṭakās) and 64 adhyāyās, or, 10 maṇḍalās (Sections) with 85 anuvākās (sub sections). There are 1028 sūktās in these maṇḍalās and in total 397265 akṣarās (Letters). Sūktās are a small group of Mantras, and have their own Devata, ṛiṣi & chandas. Totally there are 10552 Mantras in ṛig Veda. The total number of ṛiṣis in ṛig Veda is 403. There used to be 21 śākhās of the ṛig Veda. There are only two available today. They are 1. bhāshkala śākhā and 2. śākala śākhā. ṛig Veda has 1. kauṣitakī brāhmaṇā 2. śāṅkāyanā brāhmaṇā and 3. aitareyā Brāhmaṇā as Brāhmaṇās and 1. kauṣitakī āraṇyakā 2. aitareyā āraṇyakā as āraṇyakās. 1. kauṣitakī and 2. aitareyā Upanishads belong to ṛig Veda. For application, 'āśvalāyana sūtrā ' is followed.

Among the Vedic corpus available to us today, the ṛig Veda Samhitā is the biggest, oldest of all the Vedic Samhitās, grandest of the Indians, and the best in the world. Its style, the language and the tone are most beautiful and mysterious. Its priest in yajñā is called the hotā / hotrī / hotrā: It comprises of hymns in praise of Agni, indrā, rudrā, aśvini, Savitu, sūryā, varuṇā, marutā, sōmā, and ṛipu.

Yajur Veda: Yajur is derived from the word 'yajus', literally 'reverence, veneration or sacrifice'. Though it includes many of the hymns of the ṛig Veda, the Yajur Veda deviates more from the original text in its collection of the ritual formula for the priests (called Adhvaryu) to use in the sacrifices, (yajñā)
