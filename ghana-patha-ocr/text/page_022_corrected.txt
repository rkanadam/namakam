Vedāṅga (parts of Veda) are the different branches of study that one should undertake to understand Vedas fully. They are:

a. śikṣā: (Called Nose of Veda). It provides the rules of phonetics or pronunciation / duration of utterance of each syllable to give matching sound — euphony. The goal is to achieve correct pronunciation and articulation through, akṣarā śuddhi (syllable purity); svara śuddhi (tonal / pitch purity) and mātrā śuddhi (durational purity), balā (force of articulation), samam (evenness) and santāna: (continuity). Phonetics are most critical in the case of Vedic language because, a change in sound results in completely different effects. In śikṣā, there are lots of varieties like 1. pāṇinīya śikṣā (ṛig), 2. ātreya śikṣā (Yajur) 3. vyāḍi śikṣā (ṛig) 4. vyāsa śikṣā (Yajur) 5. Lomaśa śikṣā (Sāma) 6. bhāradvāja śikṣā (Yajur) 7. kauṇḍinya śikṣā (Yajur) 8. kāla nirṇaya śikṣā (Yajur) 9. pāri śikṣā (Yajur) 10. yājñavalkiya śikṣā (Common) 11. vāsiṣṭha śikṣā (ṛig) 12. āraṇyaka śikṣā (Yajur) and so on. svaras are of different types as 1. udāttā, 2. anudāttā, 3. svaritā, 4. pracayā, 5. ucca tarā (type of udāttā), 6. sannata tarā (type of anudāttā), and 7. ekaśruti.
