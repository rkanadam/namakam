The Vikritis are eight in number:

jaṭā mālā śikhā rekhā   dhvajo daṇḍo ratho ghana:
aṣṭau vikṛtaya: proktā:  kramapūrvā maharṣibhi:

jaṭā, mālā, śikhā, rekhā, dhvajā, daṇḍā, and rathā and ghanā

Two types of mālā (garland) exist: a) krama mālā and b)
puṣpa mālā. This is similar to krama pāṭhā in that two-word
units with the characteristic overlapping are the foundation.
śikhā (top knot) is similar to jaṭā except that, third word will be
combined at the end of jaṭā. Recitations in rekhā (row), dhvajā
(flag), daṇḍā (Rod), and rathā (chariot) are more complex and
the learner/reader should get advice from pundits or can refer
to the book "A Brief History of Vedas" for their respective
patterns.

Further, there are three types of rathā, namely, dvichakro (two
wheels), trichakro (three wheels) and chatuśchakro (4
wheels). Each wheel corresponds to a part of a verse (ṛik).

By repeating the words in manifold ways, the correct tally of
the words was also kept which naturally ensures the purity of
the texts. It is also believed that higher merits (punya)
accompany greater complexities in chanting – for example, a
Ghana recitation is several orders higher in merit (infinite) than
jatā recitation, (1000 units) which is higher in merit than krama
recitation (4 times), Padā recitation - 2 times the punya (or
merit or phala) of Samhita recitation.
