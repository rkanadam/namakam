Jaṭā Pāṭhā: In jaṭā Pāṭhā, the first two words are first recited
together and then the words are recited in a reverse order and
then again in the original order. Hence, Krama + Inverse of
Krama + Krama = jaṭā. The order will be 1-2-2-1-1-2, 2-3-3-2-
2-3, 3-4-4-3-3-4, 4-5-5-4-4-5 and so on. It is to be noted, that
all six padās have to be recited together with proper sandhi
(junction). In jaṭā Pāṭhā, there will be 5 Sandhis (join). When
the Padā itself is a Veṣṭanā, then, as soon as it leaves from its
turn, we have to recite the Veṣṭanā. For the last padā in a
sentence also, we have to recite the Veṣṭanā, whether it is a
Padā Veṣṭanā or not. If we express this in Mathematical series:

Mathematical Sequence, Series for jaṭā Pāṭhā:

Sentence (S) = P₁, P₂, P₃, ....... P(n-2), P(n-1), Pn
Jata Turn (T) number (T₁ to n-1)
Turn 1 (T₁) = P₁, P₂, P₂, P₁, P₁, P₂ ;
Turn 2 (T₂) = P₂, P₃, P₃, P₂, P₂, P₃ ;
............
............
Turn n-2 (Tn-2) = P(n-2), P(n-1), P(n-1), P(n-2), P(n-2), P(n-1);
Turn n-1(Tn-1) = P(n-1), Pn, Pn, P(n-1), P(n-1), Pn ; **

Hence General Combination for jaṭā Pāṭhā is:
Turn n-1(Tn-1) = P(n-1), Pn, Pn, P(n-1), P(n-1), Pn ;

where, n > 1, and Max No. of Turn < n (without any Veṣṭanā)
      Pn = nth Padā in a Sentence
      Tn = Turn of jaṭā Pāṭhā
      n = Number of Padā in a Sentence
