The literary value of Yajur Veda is mostly for its prose, which
consists of short terse sentences full of meaning and cadence. It
is the Veda of Sacrifice or Yajñā through oblations into the
Agni (holy fire). Its deities are the same as the ṛig Veda.

The Yajur Veda had originally 101 śākhās or branches. This
Veda is divided into kṛṣṇa Yajur Veda and śukla Yajur Vedā,
meaning black or mixed and white or Pure Yajur Veda
respectively.

The White Yajur consists of 40 adhyāyās (chapters), 303
anuvākās (sections) and 1975 kaṇḍikās. The two popular
recensions (śākhās) of the śukla Yajur Veda are: 1. vājasaneyī
mādyandīniya śākhā (This school dominates North India just
as the taittirīya school in South India) and 2. kāṇva śākhā (40
adhyāyās (chapters), 328 anuvākās (sections) 2086 kaṇḍikās
with 29666 padās); (this śākhā is very rare and pundits
affiliated to this school are found iñci kollai of Thanjavur
District and later in Tirunelveli, Southern Tamil Nadu) This
has śatapatha Brāhmaṇā as Brāhmaṇam and 1. īśā and 2.
bṛhadāraṇya as Upanishads.

śukla Yajus consists of 15 śākhās named vājasaneya -
ayātayāma. Maharaṣi Sri yājñavalkya, by his Tapas
(Devotion), learned this Veda from sūryā (Sun), and imparted
through his disciples. Hence, the name 'vājasaneya'.

There is no mixture of Brāhmaṇā and Mantra in this Veda,
hence it is called 'śukla Yajur Vedā or ayātayāmā'. There is a
brief note on this in the works of Sri ādhi śaṅkarā and
sāyaṇācārya. But there is elaborate history about Śukla Yajur
Veda and Maharaṣi yājñavalkya stated in the book, "A Brief
History of Vedas" authored by Sri. K Suresh.
