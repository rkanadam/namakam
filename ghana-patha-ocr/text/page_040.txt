padā Pāṭhā: To recite the Veda mantras, word by word,
instead of joining the words. This gives the student of the
Vedas, the knowledge of each word in a sentence.

Krama Pāṭhā: In Krama Pāṭhā, the first word of the mantra is
added to the second, the second to the third, the third to the
fourth and so on, until the whole sentence of the mantras is
completed. The order of words will be 1-2 ; 2-3 ; 3-4 ; 4-5 and
so on. This helps the students to understand changes occurring
in swara in such a combination. The person who is well versed
in reciting the Krama Pāṭhā is known as "Krama Vit" (like
Vedā vit) When the Padā itself is a Veṣṭanā, then, as soon as it
leaves from its turn, we have to recite the Veṣṭanā. For the last
padā in a sentence also, we have to recite the Veṣṭanā, whether
it is a Padā Veṣṭanā or not. If we express this in Mathematical
series:

Mathematical Sequence, Series for Krama Pāṭhā :

Sentence (S) = P₁, P₂, P₃, ....... P(n-2), P(n-1), Pn
Krama Turn (T) number (T₁ to n-1)

Turn 1 (T₁) = P₁, P₂ ;

Turn 2 (T₂) = P₂, P₃ ;

............

............

Turn n-2 (Tn-2) = P(n-2), P(n-1) ;

Turn n-1(Tn-1) = P(n-1), Pn ;

Hence General Combination for Krama Pāṭhā is:
Turn n-1 (Tn-1) = P(n-1), Pn ;

where, n > 1, and Max No. of Turn < n (without any Veṣṭanā)
      Pn = nᵗʰ Padā in a Sentence
      Tn = Turn of Krama Pāṭhā
      n = Number of Padā in a Sentence
