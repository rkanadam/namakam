The first Anuvaka has 15 Mantras describing the terror of
Rudra and requesting for the destruction of sins committed by
the devotee, by the grace of God and for illumination. The 3rd
Mantra is called "Rudra Gayatri" which many of us do not
know.

The second Anuvaka has 13 Yajus, without stopping in
between - breathless (same tempo is maintained till the 9th
Anuvaka) where we can see the cosmic vision of Rudra.

The third Anuvaka has 17 Yajus, gives various forms of Rudra
and states the activities of people in whom Rudra is present.

The fourth Anuvaka has 17 Yajus, which gives two extreme
forms of Rudra, like he is great and good but at the same time,
small and mean. Further states that He is the artisan and worker
of all kinds.

The fifth Anuvaka has 15 Yajus and set forth His unique
qualities and His presence in moving waters.

The sixth Anuvaka has 15 Yajus, which states the connection
of Rudra with the concepts of time and age, with the four kinds
of birth at different kinds of worlds, the warrior aspects of Him
and His army.

The seventh Anuvaka has 16 Yajus, describing Rudra as the
ambassador and agent, His presence in still water, rain, storm,
clouds and ends with the wealth of the household.
