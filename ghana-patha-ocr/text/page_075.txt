11.7 And those Rudras, who pierce the people who take food
and drinks from the vessels.

yē pathāṁ pathirakṣaya ailabr̥dā yavyudha:
11.8 Those Rudras, who are the protectors of pathways and
bestower of food and who fight with enemies.

yē tīrtthāni pracaranti sr̥kāvantō niṣaṅgiṇa:
11.9 Those Rudras, who travel frequently to protect the sacred
waters by carrying sharp weapons and long swords.

ya ētāvantaśca bhūyāṁ saśca ... dhanvāni tanmasi
11.10 We request all these Rudras and Rudra Ganās mentioned
so far, and others wherever they are, spread in different
direction; to make their strings of the bows loosened, keep their
bows thousand yojanas far away from us.

namō rudrēbhyō yē pr̥thivyāṁ ... taṁ vō jambhē
dadhāmi
11.11 My salutations to all Rudras, who are on this earth, sky
and heaven, to whom the food and air turns into weapons, I
bow to them through my speech. With my ten fingers joined, I
bow to them with my body facing the east, the south, the west,
the north and upwards. I bow to them with my mind. Let those
Rudras grant me happiness. I would deliver, my enemies and
those who consider me as their enemy, to those Rudras, wide
open mouths.
