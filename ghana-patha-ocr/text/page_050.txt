The ninth Anuvaka has 19 Yajus, praising Him as,
Omnipresent, like He is in forest, mountains, dry places, grassy
paths, He comes immediately to the devotees when prayed onto
Him, He resides in inaccessible caves, deep waters and
dewdrops, visible and invisible dust etc.

The tenth anuvaka has 12 Mantras, which is the extension of
1st Anuvaka with prayers, not to hurt us and our cattle, to
protect the village from any harm, to provide healing medicines
and make us happy, to give long life to us and not to harm our
children, our young and old relations, requesting Him to come
to us with smiling face and goodwill towards us, bearing
"Pinaka" bow as an ornament and Tiger's skin etc.

The eleventh Anuvaka has 10 Riks and 1 Yajus, focusing on
prayers to the Rudra Ganās who are in Earth, Sky, Interspace,
and salutations to them, in the East, South, West, North and
upward directions by joining 10 fingers.

The recital of 11 Anuvakas are supplemented by additional 7
Mantras. First Mantra is a prayer to Rudra by the devotee to
loosen him from death and confer immortality. Second Mantra
praises Rudra about his presence in Fire, Water, vegetation etc.
to represent that He is omnipresent. The third Mantra praises
Him as a source and repository of healing medicines and prays
to get His goodwill and grace. Fourth Mantra, the devotee
congratulates his hand for the good fortune in touching the
Sivalinga during his worship and declares that his hands
become a physician and can remove all ills. The fifth Mantra is
