c. Vyākarana- deals with grammar (Mouth of Veda). It explains about the noun, verb, gender, singular, dual & plural, timings etc

d. Niruktha - deals with etymology (origin and history of words). (Ears of Veda) Yāska is the author of this.

e. Jyotiṣam: Astronomy (Science of Stars and Planets) (Eyes of Veda) is very helpful to determine the time for performing any rituals or functions and further, to predict the future of any person. The time was calculated as 1. parārddha kālam (life of brahmā), 2. manvantaram, 3. Yuga, 4. Year, 5. Ayana, 6. ṛitu, 7. Month, 8. Paksha (Fortnight), 9. Week, 10. Day, 11. muhūrttam, 12. nāzhigai (24 Minutes) and 13. Seconds. Jyotiṣa could be classified as 1. Mathematics 2. Jataka (Horoscopes) and 3. Samhitā (Details). In Jyotiṣa, Mathematics is very important. Even in kṛṣṇa Yajur Veda, (in 4th kāṇḍā) and in Śukla Yajur Veda, (18th Adhyāya) the numbers 1,10,10² ... 10¹¹ are mentioned in Ashwameda recitals.

Time could be determined as manuṣyamānam, devamānam manvantaram, and Life of brahmā. Our 12 months (1 year) is equivalent to 1 day for Devas. Hence our 360 years equals to 1 year for Devas.
