Details about Rudra Mantras:
Lord Rudra is praised in both Rig Veda and Yajur Veda about
His warrior aspect, mighty arrows and bows, His healing
power, and His ability to ferry human sins and sorrows.
Rudram or Namakam in Yajur Veda consists of 37 Riks in
various Metres or Chandas in Anuvakas 1, 10 and 11 and 130
Yajus in Anuvaka 2 to 9 and last verse in Anuvaka 11 with
some additional Mantras. For the entire Rudra Prasna, the Rishi
is Bhagavan Rudra, Kanda Rishi is Agni, Chandas is
Mahavirat, the Devata is Chambu. Apart from this, various
Riks have their own Rishis, Chandas and Devatas. Out of the
Yajus portion, first 47 Yajus in the Anuvakas 2 to 4 are having
the word "Namaha" in the beginning and in the end, and the
remaining Anuvakas 5 to 9 starts with "Namaha" but does not
end with "Namaha".

Greatness of Rudram: Among the sources of learning- the
Vedas are supreme. In the Vedas- the Rudra Prasna is supreme.
In the Rudram- the Panchakshari Mantra "Namasivaya" is
supreme. In the Mantra- the two-letter word "Siva" stands
supreme.

Summary of Rudram or Namakam: In the first Anuvaka, the
grace of Rudra is sought. In the Anuvakas 2 to 9, He is bowed
as supreme Lord. In the 10th Anuvaka, He is prayed for
specific favours. In the 11th Anuvaka, Rudra's manifestations
are prayed for.
