The Black Yajur is divided into 7 kāṇḍās (books) 44 praśnas
(chapters) with 651 anuvākās. There are 2198 kaṇḍikās (or
panchadi comprising fifty words). The kṛṣṇa Yajur Vedā has
four śākhās: 1. taittirīya śākhā, (Majority of South Indians are
affiliated to this branch/school and is very popular in South
India) 2. pārakṣutra śākhā, 3. kaṭhaka śākhā, and 4.
maitrāyanī śākhā (understand, this is extremely rare, but very
few people still exist in and around Nasik, Maharashtra).
Majority of the Yajurvedins follow āpastamba sūtrā, few
follow the sūtrās of bodhāyana, and still very few follow
hiraṇyakeśī's (satyāṣāḍam), bhāratvājam, āgniveśyam, and
vaikānasam sūtrās. Its Brāhmaṇā is taittirīya Brāhmaṇam. Its
āraṇyaka is taittirīya āraṇyakam. Its Upanishads are 1.
taittirīya 2. kaṭa and 3. maitrāyanī.
