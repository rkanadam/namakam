The Vedas promote and accept all forms of religious practice
in the pursuit of understanding and merging with the Supreme.
Hence it is highly secular and tolerant in its teachings and/or
preaching.

Without resorting to writing, our forefathers had devised many
ways and different methods to keep it error free and to recite
with correct pronunciation of letters/ words, correct duration
for utterance of letters/ words and correct accent/ modulation
of letters by applying numerous safeguards.

How much time it should take to utter each word is indicated
by resort to the notation by "mātrā" - the time it takes to
pronounce a short vowel. How to regulate breathing so that the
vibrations can occur at what part of the body to give birth to
the pure word and sound is also laid down in the Vedānga
śikṣā. The taittirīya Upanishad, for e.g., begins with śikṣā
thus:

śikṣām Vyākyā syāmah - Varna Swarah - Mātrābalam -Sāma
Santānah

śikṣā deals with Varna, Svarā, Mātrā, Strength, Sāmā and
Santānā

Our ṛiṣis prescribed several fool-proof methods to correctly
recite the Veda mantra in various patterns and combinations
known as vākhyā, padā, kramā, jaṭā, mālā, śikhā, rekhā,
dhvajā, daṇḍā, rathā and ghana:

vākhya pāṭhā or Samhitā pāṭhā is to recite the mantras in a
sentence straight. When mantras come in sentences, some of
the words therein have to be conjoined in chanting.
